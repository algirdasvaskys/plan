How to Replace Deck Boards

Replacing damaged or worn deck boards is an essential maintenance task that can extend the life of your deck and improve its appearance. Follow this step-by-step guide to replace your deck boards effectively.

Tools Needed:

  • Pry bar
  • Hammer
  • Circular saw
  • Drill with screwdriver bits
  • Tape measure
  • Pencil
  • Safety glasses
  • Work gloves

Materials:

  • New deck boards
  • Deck screws
  • Wood preservative (if using untreated wood)

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Inspect and Measure

Carefully inspect your deck to identify damaged or rotting boards. Measure the length and width of the boards you need to replace, and purchase new boards accordingly.

Step 2: Remove Old Boards

Using a pry bar, carefully remove the damaged boards. Start at one end and work your way along the board, prying it up from each joist. Be cautious not to damage surrounding boards or the deck structure.

Step 3: Inspect and Repair Joists

With the old boards removed, inspect the joists for any damage or rot. Replace or repair any compromised joists before installing new deck boards.

Step 4: Prepare New Boards

If using untreated wood, apply wood preservative to all sides of the new boards, including cut ends. Allow the preservative to dry completely before installation.

Step 5: Cut New Boards to Size

Measure and mark your new boards to match the length of the old ones. Use a circular saw to cut the boards to size, ensuring straight and accurate cuts.

Step 6: Position New Boards

Place the new boards in position, ensuring they are properly aligned with adjacent boards. Use spacers if necessary to maintain consistent gaps between boards for proper drainage.

Step 7: Secure New Boards

Using a drill and deck screws, secure the new boards to each joist. Place two screws at each joist intersection, about 3/4 inch from the board edges to prevent splitting.

Step 8: Sand and Finish

If necessary, sand the new boards to ensure a smooth surface. Apply an appropriate finish or stain to match the rest of your deck, following the manufacturer's instructions.

Tips:

  • Always wear safety glasses and work gloves when removing old boards and installing new ones.
  • Check local building codes to ensure compliance with regulations.
  • Consider replacing multiple boards at once if your deck shows signs of widespread wear.
  • Use boards of the same thickness as the original deck boards to maintain a level surface.

By following these steps, you can successfully replace damaged deck boards and maintain the safety and appearance of your outdoor space.
