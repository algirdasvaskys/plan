How to Cut Perfect Rafter Feet or Tails

Cutting perfect rafter feet or tails is crucial for ensuring a stable and well-fitting roof structure. Follow these steps to achieve precise cuts every time.

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Rafters
  • Measuring tape
  • Carpenter's square
  • Pencil
  • Circular saw or handsaw
  • Safety glasses
  • Work gloves

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Measure and Mark the Plumb Cut

The plumb cut is the vertical cut at the top of the rafter where it meets the ridge board.

  • Place the carpenter's square on the rafter.
  • Align the square with the roof pitch (e.g., 4/12, 6/12).
  • Draw a line along the square to mark the plumb cut.

Step 2: Mark the Bird's Mouth

The bird's mouth is the notch cut into the rafter that sits on the top plate of the wall.

  • Measure from the top of the plumb cut to where the bird's mouth will be.
  • Use the square to mark the seat cut (horizontal) and heel cut (vertical) of the bird's mouth.

Step 3: Mark the Tail Cut

The tail cut is the angled cut at the end of the rafter that extends beyond the wall.

  • Measure from the bird's mouth to the desired overhang length.
  • Use the square to mark the tail cut, ensuring it's parallel to the plumb cut.

Step 4: Double-Check Measurements

  • Verify all measurements and marks before cutting.
  • Ensure the bird's mouth depth doesn't exceed 1/3 of the rafter depth.

Step 5: Make the Cuts

  • Put on safety glasses and work gloves.
  • Use a circular saw for straight cuts and a handsaw for the bird's mouth.
  • Cut along the marked lines, ensuring the saw blade is perpendicular to the rafter.
  • For the bird's mouth, make multiple passes with the circular saw, then finish with a handsaw.

Step 6: Test Fit and Adjust

  • Test fit the rafter in place.
  • Make minor adjustments if necessary.
  • Use the first rafter as a template for marking and cutting the remaining rafters.

Pro Tips:

  • Always cut on the waste side of the line to maintain accuracy.
  • Use a sharp blade for clean, precise cuts.
  • Consider using a rafter cutting jig for consistent cuts on multiple rafters.
  • Take your time and measure twice, cut once to avoid costly mistakes.

By following these steps and taking care with your measurements and cuts, you'll be able to create perfect rafter feet or tails for a professional-looking and structurally sound roof.
