Interlocking of Two Contactors: Explanation with Circuit Diagram

Introduction

Interlocking of contactors is a crucial safety measure in electrical systems, particularly in motor control applications. It prevents the simultaneous operation of two contactors that could lead to dangerous conditions or equipment damage.

Circuit Diagram

Explanation

In this circuit diagram:

  • K1 and K2 represent two contactors that need to be interlocked.
  • Each contactor has a normally closed (NC) auxiliary contact.
  • The NC contact of K1 is connected in series with the coil of K2.
  • The NC contact of K2 is connected in series with the coil of K1.

How It Works

  1. When K1 is energized:
    • Its main contacts close, allowing current to flow in its power circuit.
    • Its NC auxiliary contact opens, breaking the control circuit of K2.
  2. When K2 is energized:
    • Its main contacts close, allowing current to flow in its power circuit.
    • Its NC auxiliary contact opens, breaking the control circuit of K1.
  3. This arrangement ensures that when one contactor is closed, the other cannot be energized, thus preventing simultaneous operation.

Applications

This interlocking method is commonly used in:

  • Reversing motor starters
  • Star-delta motor starters
  • Automatic transfer switches
  • Any system where mutual exclusion of two electrical operations is required

Safety Considerations

While this interlocking method is effective, it's important to note:

  • The system relies on the proper functioning of the NC auxiliary contacts.
  • Regular maintenance and testing of the interlocking system is crucial.
  • For critical applications, additional safety measures may be necessary.
