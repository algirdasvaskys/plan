How to Size a Generator

Properly sizing a generator is crucial to ensure it can meet your power needs efficiently and reliably. This guide will walk you through the process step by step.

Step 1: Determine Your Power Requirements

List all the appliances and equipment you need to power. For each item, note:

  • Starting wattage (surge power)
  • Running wattage (continuous power)

The starting wattage is usually higher and is required when the appliance first turns on.

Step 2: Calculate Total Power Needs

Add up the wattages:

  1. Sum up all the running wattages
  2. Identify the appliance with the highest additional starting wattage
  3. Add this highest starting wattage to the total running wattage

This gives you the minimum wattage your generator should be able to produce.

Step 3: Consider Future Needs

Add 10-20% to your calculated wattage to account for:

  • Potential additional appliances
  • Increased power needs in the future

Step 4: Determine Fuel Type

Choose between:

  • Gasoline
  • Diesel
  • Propane
  • Natural gas

Consider factors like fuel availability, storage, and local regulations.

Step 5: Consider Portability vs. Standby

Decide between:

  • Portable generators: Good for temporary use or moving between locations
  • Standby generators: Permanently installed, automatically turn on during power outages

Step 6: Check Local Regulations

Ensure compliance with:

  • Local noise ordinances
  • Emission standards
  • Installation requirements

Pro Tip:

Always choose a generator with a slightly higher capacity than your calculated needs. This provides a safety margin and allows for future expansion.
