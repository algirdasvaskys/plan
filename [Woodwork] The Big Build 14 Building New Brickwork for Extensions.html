The Big Build 14: Building New Brickwork for Extensions

Introduction

This guide provides a comprehensive method for building new brickwork to form extensions as part of The Big Build 14 project. Follow these steps carefully to ensure a sturdy and professional construction.

Materials Needed

  • Bricks
  • Mortar mix
  • Sand
  • Cement
  • Water
  • Trowel
  • Level
  • String line
  • Tape measure
  • Brick hammer
  • Jointing tool
  • Wheelbarrow
  • Bucket
  • Safety equipment (gloves, goggles, dust mask)

Step-by-Step Method

Step 1: Prepare the Foundation

Ensure the foundation is level, clean, and damp-proofed. Mark out the dimensions of the extension using string lines and pegs.

Step 2: Mix the Mortar

In a wheelbarrow, mix 1 part cement to 4-5 parts building sand. Add water gradually until you achieve a consistent, workable mixture.

Step 3: Lay the First Course

Spread a layer of mortar along the foundation. Place the first brick at a corner, then use a string line to ensure subsequent bricks are straight. Use the level to check each brick is level both horizontally and vertically.

Step 4: Build Up the Corners

Build up the corners first, using a plumb line to ensure they're straight. This will serve as a guide for the rest of the wall.

Step 5: Lay Subsequent Courses

Continue laying bricks in a running bond pattern (each vertical joint should be centered on the brick below). Apply mortar to the top and ends of each brick before placing.

Step 6: Check for Level and Plumb

Regularly check that the wall is level horizontally and plumb vertically. Use a spirit level and plumb line.

Step 7: Cut Bricks as Needed

Use a brick hammer or masonry saw to cut bricks when necessary, such as at the ends of rows or around windows and doors.

Step 8: Install Lintels and Damp Proof Course

Install lintels above openings for windows and doors. Remember to include a damp proof course where necessary.

Step 9: Point the Joints

Once the mortar has begun to set (but before it's fully hardened), use a jointing tool to finish the mortar joints for a neat appearance.

Step 10: Clean and Finish

Clean any excess mortar from the face of the bricks. Allow the wall to cure for at least 24 hours before continuing with further construction work.

Safety Warning

Always wear appropriate safety gear, including gloves, goggles, and a dust mask. Be cautious when lifting heavy materials and working at heights. If you're unsure about any aspect of the construction, consult a professional.

Pro Tip

To ensure consistency in your mortar joints, use a piece of wood cut to the desired joint thickness as a spacer between bricks.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you should be able to successfully build new brickwork for your extension as part of The Big Build 14 project. Remember that bricklaying is a skill that improves with practice, so take your time and focus on maintaining consistent levels and joint thicknesses throughout the build.
