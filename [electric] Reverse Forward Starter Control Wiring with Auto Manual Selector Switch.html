Reverse Forward Starter Control Wiring with Auto Manual Selector Switch

Warning: Electrical wiring should only be performed by qualified electricians. Improper wiring can result in equipment damage, injury, or death.

Components Required:

  • Two contactors (one for forward direction, one for reverse)
  • Overload relay
  • Auto/Manual selector switch (3-position)
  • Forward and Reverse pushbuttons
  • Stop pushbutton
  • Control transformer
  • Fuses or circuit breakers
  • Three-phase motor

Wiring Method:

  1. Power Circuit:
    • Connect the three-phase power supply to the main circuit breaker or fuses.
    • Wire the output of the circuit breaker to the overload relay.
    • Connect the forward contactor between the overload relay and the motor terminals U1, V1, W1.
    • Wire the reverse contactor to swap two phases (e.g., V1 and W1) for reverse operation.
  2. Control Circuit:
    • Connect the control transformer primary to two phases of the main power supply.
    • Wire the secondary of the transformer to the control circuit, typically 120V AC.
    • Install a fuse on one side of the control circuit for protection.
  3. Auto/Manual Selector Switch:
    • Wire the common terminal of the selector switch to the control circuit power.
    • Connect the "Manual" position to the manual control circuit.
    • Wire the "Auto" position to the automatic control circuit (e.g., PLC outputs).
  4. Manual Control:
    • Wire the Forward and Reverse pushbuttons in parallel, each with a normally open contact.
    • Connect the Stop pushbutton with a normally closed contact in series with the Forward and Reverse buttons.
    • Wire the output of the pushbuttons to the coils of the respective contactors.
  5. Automatic Control:
    • Connect the "Auto" position of the selector switch to the PLC or other control system outputs.
    • Ensure the automatic system can control both forward and reverse operations.
  6. Interlocking:
    • Wire normally closed auxiliary contacts of each contactor in series with the coil of the opposite contactor to prevent simultaneous activation.
  7. Overload Protection:
    • Connect a normally closed contact from the overload relay in series with both contactor coils to disconnect power in case of an overload condition.
  8. Indicator Lights (Optional):
    • Wire indicator lights in parallel with each contactor coil to show the current operation status (Forward/Reverse).

Testing and Commissioning:

  1. Verify all connections are tight and properly insulated.
  2. Ensure the motor shaft is free to rotate.
  3. Power up the control circuit and test the manual operation:
    • Press the Forward button and verify the motor rotates in the forward direction.
    • Press the Stop button and ensure the motor stops.
    • Press the Reverse button and confirm the motor rotates in the opposite direction.
  4. Test the Auto/Manual selector switch functionality.
  5. Verify the automatic control system (if applicable) can properly control the motor in both directions.
  6. Test the overload protection by simulating an overload condition.
Important: Always follow local electrical codes and safety regulations. Regular maintenance and inspection of the control system are essential for safe and reliable operation.
