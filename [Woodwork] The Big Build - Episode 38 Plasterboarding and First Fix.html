The Big Build - Episode 38: Plasterboarding and First Fix

Introduction

Welcome to another exciting episode of The Big Build! Today, we're diving into the crucial stages of plasterboarding and first fix in construction. These steps are essential for creating a solid foundation for your interior walls and preparing for the installation of utilities.

Plasterboarding

Step 1: Prepare the Area

Ensure the wall framing is complete, level, and secure. Remove any debris or protruding nails/screws.

Step 2: Measure and Cut

Measure the wall dimensions and cut plasterboards to size using a utility knife and straight edge.

Step 3: Apply Adhesive

Apply dabs of adhesive to the wall studs or joists in a grid pattern.

Step 4: Position the Plasterboard

Lift the plasterboard into position, pressing it firmly against the adhesive dabs.

Step 5: Secure the Plasterboard

Use drywall screws to secure the plasterboard to the studs, spacing them about 30cm apart.

Pro Tip:

Start from the top of the wall and work your way down. This helps prevent sagging and ensures a smooth finish.

First Fix

The first fix stage involves installing the initial elements of electrical, plumbing, and heating systems before the walls are fully closed up.

Step 1: Electrical Wiring

Run electrical cables through the wall cavities, securing them with cable clips. Install back boxes for switches and sockets.

Step 2: Plumbing

Install water supply pipes and waste pipes. Ensure proper support and allow for thermal expansion.

Step 3: Heating

Install radiator pipes or underfloor heating elements as required.

Step 4: Ventilation

Install ventilation ducts and outlets for bathrooms and kitchens.

Pro Tip:

Always follow local building codes and regulations. It's often required to have first fix work inspected before proceeding to the next stage.

Conclusion

Proper plasterboarding and first fix work are crucial for creating a solid foundation for your interior finish and ensuring smooth operation of utilities. Take your time, plan carefully, and don't hesitate to consult professionals when needed. In our next episode, we'll cover the second fix stage. Stay tuned!
