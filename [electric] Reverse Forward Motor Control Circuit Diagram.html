Reverse Forward Motor Control Circuit Diagram

Circuit Diagram

Components

  • M: Three-phase induction motor
  • F1, F2, F3: Fuses
  • C1, C2: Contactors
  • OL: Overload relay
  • PB1: Start pushbutton (NO - Normally Open)
  • PB2: Stop pushbutton (NC - Normally Closed)
  • PB3: Forward pushbutton (NO)
  • PB4: Reverse pushbutton (NO)

Circuit Operation

  1. The circuit is powered by a three-phase supply, protected by fuses F1, F2, and F3.
  2. To start the motor in the forward direction:
    • Press PB1 (Start) and PB3 (Forward) simultaneously.
    • Contactor C1 energizes, closing its main and auxiliary contacts.
    • The motor starts rotating in the forward direction.
  3. To start the motor in the reverse direction:
    • Press PB1 (Start) and PB4 (Reverse) simultaneously.
    • Contactor C2 energizes, closing its main and auxiliary contacts.
    • The motor starts rotating in the reverse direction.
  4. To stop the motor:
    • Press PB2 (Stop).
    • The engaged contactor (C1 or C2) de-energizes, opening its contacts.
    • The motor stops.
  5. Interlocking:
    • C1 and C2 are electrically interlocked using their auxiliary contacts.
    • This prevents both contactors from engaging simultaneously, avoiding a short circuit.
  6. Overload protection:
    • The overload relay (OL) protects the motor from overcurrent.
    • If an overload occurs, OL opens its contact in the control circuit, stopping the motor.

Safety Features

  • Fuses (F1, F2, F3) protect against short circuits.
  • The overload relay (OL) protects against prolonged overcurrent situations.
  • Electrical interlocking prevents simultaneous engagement of forward and reverse contactors.
  • The stop button (PB2) is normally closed for fail-safe operation.
