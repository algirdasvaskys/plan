Nominal Mixed Concrete: Understanding and Calculations

What is Nominal Mixed Concrete?

Nominal mixed concrete is a type of concrete mix where the proportions of cement, sand, and aggregates are specified by volume rather than by weight. It's commonly used in small-scale construction projects where precise mix design isn't critical.

Common Nominal Mix Ratios

Mix Ratio Cement Sand Aggregate Typical Use
1:2:4 1 2 4 General construction
1:3:6 1 3 6 Mass concrete, foundations
1:1.5:3 1 1.5 3 High-strength applications

Calculating Quantities of Materials

To calculate the quantities of materials needed for a given volume of concrete, follow these steps:

Step 1: Determine the required volume of concrete

Measure the dimensions of the area where concrete will be poured and calculate the volume.

Step 2: Add extra volume for wastage

Add 10-15% extra volume to account for wastage and variations in thickness.

Step 3: Calculate the dry volume of materials

The total volume of dry materials (cement, sand, and aggregate) is typically about 1.54 times the volume of concrete required.

Dry volume = Required concrete volume × 1.54

Step 4: Calculate individual material volumes

Using the mix ratio, calculate the volume of each material:

Cement volume = (Dry volume × Cement ratio) / (Sum of ratio parts)
Sand volume = (Dry volume × Sand ratio) / (Sum of ratio parts)
Aggregate volume = (Dry volume × Aggregate ratio) / (Sum of ratio parts)

Step 5: Convert volumes to weights (optional)

If needed, convert volumes to weights using typical densities:

  • Cement: 1440 kg/m³
  • Sand: 1600 kg/m³
  • Aggregate: 1550 kg/m³

Calculating Water Quantity

The water content in concrete is crucial for workability and strength. It's typically expressed as the water-cement ratio (W/C ratio).

Step 1: Determine the appropriate W/C ratio

Common W/C ratios range from 0.45 to 0.60, depending on the required strength and workability.

Step 2: Calculate water volume

Water volume = Cement weight × W/C ratio

Note: This calculation gives a starting point. The actual water content may need to be adjusted on-site based on the moisture content of the aggregates and desired workability.

Example Calculation

Let's calculate the materials for 1 m³ of concrete using a 1:2:4 mix ratio and a W/C ratio of 0.5:

  1. Required volume: 1 m³
  2. Dry volume: 1 × 1.54 = 1.54 m³
  3. Individual volumes:
    • Cement: (1.54 × 1) / 7 = 0.22 m³
    • Sand: (1.54 × 2) / 7 = 0.44 m³
    • Aggregate: (1.54 × 4) / 7 = 0.88 m³
  4. Weights:
    • Cement: 0.22 × 1440 = 316.8 kg
    • Sand: 0.44 × 1600 = 704 kg
    • Aggregate: 0.88 × 1550 = 1364 kg
  5. Water: 316.8 × 0.5 = 158.4 liters

Remember, these calculations provide a starting point. Adjustments may be necessary based on site conditions, material properties, and specific project requirements.
