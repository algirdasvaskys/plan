Miniature Circuit Breaker Labels and Marking Decoded

Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs) are crucial components in electrical systems, protecting circuits from overloads and short circuits. Understanding their labels and markings is essential for proper selection and installation. This guide will help you decode the information provided on MCB labels.

1. Current Rating

The current rating is typically the most prominent number on the MCB. It indicates the maximum continuous current the breaker can handle.

Common Ratings Description
6A, 10A, 16A, 20A, 25A, 32A, 40A, 50A, 63A Ampere ratings for residential and light commercial use

2. Breaking Capacity

This indicates the maximum fault current the MCB can safely interrupt. It's usually expressed in kA (kiloamperes).

Symbol Breaking Capacity
B 3-5 kA
C 5-10 kA
D 10-20 kA

3. Tripping Characteristic

This indicates how quickly the MCB will trip in response to an overload.

Type Description Applications
B Trips between 3-5 times rated current Resistive loads, general purpose
C Trips between 5-10 times rated current Slightly inductive loads, lighting
D Trips between 10-20 times rated current Highly inductive loads, motors

4. Number of Poles

MCBs can have different numbers of poles, typically represented by symbols:

  • 1P: Single pole
  • 1P+N: Single pole with neutral
  • 2P: Double pole
  • 3P: Three pole
  • 4P: Four pole

5. Voltage Rating

This indicates the maximum voltage the MCB is designed to handle. Common ratings include:

  • 230/240V AC for single-phase systems
  • 400/415V AC for three-phase systems

6. Standards Compliance

MCBs often display markings indicating compliance with specific standards:

  • IEC 60898-1: International standard for MCBs
  • UL 489: North American standard
  • CE: European Conformity

7. Energy Limiting Class

This classification (1, 2, or 3) indicates the MCB's ability to limit the amount of energy let through during a fault condition.

Note: Always consult the manufacturer's datasheet for complete and accurate information about a specific MCB model. The markings and their meanings can vary slightly between manufacturers and regions.
