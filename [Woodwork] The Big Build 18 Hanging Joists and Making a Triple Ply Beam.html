The Big Build 18: Hanging Joists and Making a Triple Ply Beam

Introduction

In this guide, we'll walk through the process of hanging joists and creating a triple ply beam using a router to flush joist hangers. This method is crucial for ensuring a strong and stable structure in your building project.

Tools and Materials Needed

  • Joists
  • Lumber for triple ply beam
  • Joist hangers
  • Router with flush trim bit
  • Circular saw
  • Drill with appropriate bits
  • Framing square
  • Level
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Safety equipment (goggles, gloves, dust mask)
  • Nails or screws (as per local building codes)
  • Hammer or nail gun
Safety Note: Always wear appropriate safety gear and follow proper safety procedures when working with power tools and construction materials.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Prepare the Triple Ply Beam:

  • Select three pieces of lumber of the same dimensions for your beam.
  • Align the pieces and clamp them together securely.
  • Use a circular saw to trim the ends if necessary, ensuring they're perfectly square.

2. Secure the Beam Layers:

  • Following local building codes, nail or screw the three layers together.
  • Stagger the fasteners to ensure maximum strength.
  • Double-check that the layers remain aligned during this process.

3. Mark Joist Locations:

  • Measure and mark the locations for your joists on the beam.
  • Typically, joists are spaced 16" or 24" on center, but always check local building codes.
  • Use a framing square to ensure your marks are perpendicular to the beam.

4. Attach Joist Hangers:

  • Place a joist hanger at each marked location.
  • Ensure the hanger is level and square to the beam.
  • Nail the hanger to the beam using the appropriate nails as specified by the hanger manufacturer.

5. Prepare the Router:

  • Install a flush trim bit in your router.
  • Adjust the depth so that the bearing rides along the joist hanger's edge.

6. Route the Joist Hangers:

  • Carefully run the router along each joist hanger.
  • This process will create a perfect recess for the joist to sit flush with the beam.
  • Work slowly and steadily to ensure a clean cut.

7. Install the Joists:

  • Cut your joists to the appropriate length if necessary.
  • Place each joist into its corresponding hanger.
  • Ensure the joist sits snugly in the routed recess.

8. Secure the Joists:

  • Using the specified nails or screws, attach the joist to the hanger.
  • Ensure you're using the correct number of fasteners as indicated by the hanger manufacturer or local building codes.

9. Final Checks:

  • Use a level to ensure all joists are level and properly aligned.
  • Double-check that all fasteners are secure.
  • Verify that the entire structure is square and plumb.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you've successfully hung joists and created a triple ply beam using a router to flush joist hangers. This method ensures a strong, stable structure with joists that sit perfectly flush with the beam. Always remember to consult local building codes and seek professional advice for complex projects.
