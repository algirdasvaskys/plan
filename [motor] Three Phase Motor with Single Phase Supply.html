Running a Three Phase Motor on Single Phase Supply

To run a three-phase motor using a single-phase supply, you can use a capacitor to create a phase shift. This method is commonly known as the capacitor-start or capacitor-run method. Here is an explanation and a diagram to illustrate the concept:

Diagram: Three Phase Motor Connection with a Capacitor for Single Phase Supply

Steps to Connect a Three Phase Motor to a Single Phase Supply:

  1. Identify the Motor Terminals: The three terminals of the motor are T1, T2, and T3.
  2. Connect the Single Phase Supply: Connect the single-phase supply (L and N) to two of the motor terminals (e.g., T1 and T2).
  3. Use a Capacitor: Connect a capacitor in series between the third terminal (T3) and one of the supply terminals (e.g., T1). This creates a phase shift.
  4. Start the Motor: When power is applied, the capacitor generates a phase shift, allowing the motor to start and run.
