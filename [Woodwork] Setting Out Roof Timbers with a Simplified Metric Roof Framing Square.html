Setting Out Roof Timbers with a Simplified Metric Roof Framing Square

This guide will walk you through the process of setting out roof timbers using a simplified metric roof framing square. This method is particularly useful for carpenters and DIY enthusiasts working on roof construction projects.

What is a Roof Framing Square?

A roof framing square, also known as a rafter square or framing square, is an L-shaped tool used in carpentry to lay out rafters, stair stringers, and other angled cuts. The simplified metric version typically has measurements in millimeters and degrees.

Steps to Set Out Roof Timbers

Step 1: Determine the Roof Pitch

Before you begin, you need to know the roof pitch. This is usually expressed as a ratio, such as 4:12 (rises 4 units for every 12 units of horizontal run).

Step 2: Position the Square

Place the square on the timber with the longer arm (blade) along the top edge of the timber and the shorter arm (tongue) perpendicular to it.

Step 3: Find the Correct Markings

On the blade of the square, find the marking that corresponds to your roof pitch. For example, if your roof pitch is 4:12, find the 4 on the blade.

Step 4: Mark the Plumb Cut

With the square positioned correctly, draw a line along the tongue of the square. This line represents the plumb cut (vertical cut) of your rafter.

Step 5: Mark the Bird's Mouth

The bird's mouth is the notch cut into the rafter that sits on the wall plate. To mark this:

  1. Measure the width of the wall plate from the plumb line.
  2. Make a mark at this point on the bottom edge of the rafter.
  3. Use the square to draw a vertical line up from this mark to intersect with the top edge of the rafter.

Step 6: Mark the Tail Cut

The tail cut is the angled cut at the overhanging end of the rafter. Position the square as in step 2, but this time at the end of the rafter, and mark along the blade.

Step 7: Cut the Timber

Using a saw, carefully cut along all the lines you've marked. Always double-check your measurements before cutting.

Safety Warning

Always wear appropriate safety gear, including safety glasses and gloves, when working with power tools. Ensure your work area is well-lit and free from obstacles.

Tips for Using a Metric Roof Framing Square

  • Keep your square clean and free from debris for accurate measurements.
  • Use a sharp pencil for precise markings.
  • Double-check all measurements before cutting.
  • For complex roofs, consider creating a template rafter first.

Remember, practice makes perfect. The more you use your roof framing square, the more comfortable and proficient you'll become with it. Always prioritize safety and accuracy in your carpentry projects.
