How to Fit Skirting Boards in 2023

Tools Needed:

  • Miter saw or hand saw
  • Tape measure
  • Pencil
  • Nail gun or hammer
  • Spirit level
  • Caulking gun
  • Sandpaper (120-grit and 240-grit)

Materials Needed:

  • Skirting boards
  • Wood filler
  • Decorators caulk
  • Wood glue
  • Nails (50mm for nail gun or lost head nails for hammering)
  • Paint or varnish (if required)

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Measure and Mark

Measure the length of each wall where you'll be fitting skirting boards. Mark these measurements on your skirting boards, allowing for any corners or joins.

Step 2: Cut the Skirting Boards

Use a miter saw to cut the skirting boards to the correct length. For internal corners, cut at a 45-degree angle. For external corners, use a scribe joint for a neater finish.

Step 3: Sand the Edges

Sand the cut edges with 120-grit sandpaper to remove any roughness, then finish with 240-grit for a smooth edge.

Step 4: Prepare the Wall

Ensure the wall is clean, dry, and free from debris. Mark the position of any cables or pipes to avoid them when nailing.

Step 5: Apply Adhesive

Apply wood glue to the back of the skirting board in a zigzag pattern for extra hold.

Step 6: Position and Level

Place the skirting board against the wall, ensuring it's level using a spirit level. Press firmly to bond with the adhesive.

Step 7: Secure the Skirting Board

Use a nail gun or hammer to secure the skirting board to the wall. Place nails about 60cm apart and 15mm from the top and bottom edges.

Step 8: Fill Nail Holes and Gaps

Use wood filler to cover nail holes. Fill any gaps between the skirting board and the wall with decorators caulk.

Step 9: Sand and Clean

Once the filler and caulk are dry, sand the filled areas smooth. Clean off any dust.

Step 10: Paint or Varnish

If required, paint or varnish the skirting boards to match your decor. Use masking tape to protect the wall and floor.

Tips for 2023:

  • Consider using primed MDF skirting boards for easier painting and moisture resistance.
  • For a modern look, try oversized skirting boards or painted in bold colors.
  • Use a laser level for perfectly straight lines across long walls.
  • Consider eco-friendly, sustainably sourced wood options for skirting boards.

Remember, if you're unsure about any step or if you encounter complex situations (like uneven floors or walls), it's always best to consult a professional.
