Brick Technical Terms and Standard Dimensions

Common Technical Terms

Frog: A depression or indentation on the top surface of a brick, which reduces weight and allows for better mortar adhesion.

Arris: The sharp edge formed by the intersection of two surfaces of a brick.

Stretcher: The long face of a brick when laid horizontally in a wall.

Header: The short end of a brick when laid horizontally in a wall.

Bed: The horizontal mortar joint between courses of bricks.

Perpend: The vertical mortar joint between bricks in the same course.

Course: A horizontal layer of bricks in a wall.

Soldier: A brick laid vertically with its long narrow side showing.

Rowlock: A brick laid on its edge with its short end showing.

Bat: A portion of a brick cut across its width.

Brick Bonding Patterns

Brick bonding patterns refer to the arrangement of bricks in a wall. Common patterns include:

  • Stretcher Bond: The most common pattern, with each course showing only stretchers.
  • English Bond: Alternating courses of headers and stretchers.
  • Flemish Bond: Headers and stretchers alternating in each course.
  • Stack Bond: Bricks aligned vertically and horizontally, creating a grid pattern.
  • Herringbone Bond: Bricks laid at 45-degree angles, creating a V-shaped pattern.

Understanding these terms and dimensions is crucial for proper brick laying and construction planning. Always consult local building codes and standards, as requirements may vary by region.
