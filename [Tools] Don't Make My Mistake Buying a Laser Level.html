Don't Make My Mistake: Buying a Laser Level

Laser levels are incredibly useful tools for various projects, from hanging pictures to complex construction work. However, choosing the right one can be tricky. Here's a guide to help you avoid common mistakes when purchasing a laser level.

1. Not Considering Your Specific Needs

Mistake: Buying a laser level without thinking about your specific projects and requirements.

Tip: Consider the types of projects you'll be working on. Do you need a simple line level for hanging pictures, or a more complex rotary laser for outdoor construction?

2. Ignoring Accuracy Specifications

Mistake: Assuming all laser levels are equally accurate.

Tip: Check the accuracy specifications. For most DIY projects, Â±1/4 inch at 100 feet is sufficient. Professional work may require higher accuracy.

3. Overlooking Battery Life

Mistake: Forgetting to check the battery life and type.

Tip: Look for models with long battery life. Rechargeable batteries can be more cost-effective in the long run.

4. Disregarding Durability

Mistake: Choosing a fragile model that can't withstand job site conditions.

Tip: Look for laser levels with rubber overmolding and IP ratings for dust and water resistance if you'll be using it outdoors or in tough conditions.

5. Forgetting About Visibility

Mistake: Not considering the visibility of the laser in different lighting conditions.

Tip: For outdoor use, choose a green laser as it's more visible in bright light. For indoor use, red lasers are usually sufficient.

6. Neglecting Self-Leveling Capabilities

Mistake: Buying a manual laser level when a self-leveling one would save time and increase accuracy.

Tip: Self-leveling laser levels are worth the extra cost for most users. They're faster to set up and generally more accurate.

7. Ignoring Additional Features

Mistake: Not considering useful additional features that could make your work easier.

Tip: Look for features like pulse mode for use with a detector, multiple line projections, or bluetooth connectivity for advanced applications.

Conclusion

By avoiding these common mistakes, you'll be much more likely to choose a laser level that meets your needs and provides good value for your money. Remember to research thoroughly, read reviews, and if possible, try out the tool before making your final decision.
