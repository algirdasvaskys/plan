Cladding with Lamello: A Comprehensive Guide

Lamello connectors offer an excellent solution for cladding projects, providing strong, invisible joints. This guide will walk you through the process of using Lamello for cladding installation.

What is Lamello?

Lamello is a joinery system that uses biscuit-shaped connectors to join wood pieces. It's known for its strength, precision, and ease of use.

Materials Needed:

  • Lamello joiner (biscuit joiner)
  • Lamello biscuits
  • Cladding panels
  • Wood glue
  • Clamps
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Safety equipment (goggles, dust mask)

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Preparation

Measure and mark your cladding panels where you want to place the Lamello joints. Typically, place them every 30-40 cm along the length of the panel.

Step 2: Cutting the Slots

Use the Lamello joiner to cut slots in both the cladding panel and the surface you're attaching it to. Ensure the slots align perfectly.

Step 3: Applying Glue

Apply wood glue into the slots and on the surface where the panel will be attached.

Step 4: Inserting Biscuits

Insert Lamello biscuits into the slots on the cladding panel.

Step 5: Joining

Carefully align the cladding panel with the prepared surface, ensuring the biscuits slide into the corresponding slots.

Step 6: Clamping

Use clamps to hold the panel in place while the glue dries. Follow the glue manufacturer's instructions for drying time.

Step 7: Finishing

Once the glue is dry, remove the clamps. Your cladding should now be securely attached with invisible joints.

Pro Tips:

  • Always do a dry run before applying glue to ensure everything fits correctly.
  • Use a level to ensure your cladding panels are straight during installation.
  • Clean excess glue immediately with a damp cloth to avoid staining.
  • For exterior cladding, use weather-resistant Lamello biscuits and appropriate glue.

Conclusion

Lamello provides a strong and aesthetically pleasing solution for cladding installation. With practice, you'll find it's an efficient and reliable method for your projects.
