The Big Build 6: Scaffold Erection, Tile Stripping, and Demolition Prep

1. Scaffold Erection

  • Assess the work area and determine scaffold requirements
  • Obtain necessary permits and approvals
  • Mark out the scaffold footprint
  • Begin with base plates and adjust for level ground
  • Erect vertical standards and connect with ledgers and transoms
  • Install guardrails, toe boards, and access ladders
  • Add scaffold boards to create working platforms
  • Secure the scaffold to the building structure as required
  • Conduct a final inspection and safety check

2. Stripping Tiles

  • Ensure proper personal protective equipment (PPE) is worn
  • Set up debris chutes or waste removal system
  • Start at the highest point of the roof
  • Use pry bars or specialized tile removal tools to lift tiles
  • Work methodically across the roof, removing tiles row by row
  • Carefully stack and dispose of tiles as you go
  • Inspect underlying structure for damage or rot
  • Remove any remaining nails or fasteners
  • Clean the roof deck of debris

3. Preparing for Demolition

  • Conduct a thorough structural assessment
  • Identify and mark load-bearing walls
  • Locate and shut off all utilities (gas, electricity, water)
  • Remove any hazardous materials (e.g., asbestos) if present
  • Set up temporary supports where necessary
  • Establish clear demolition zones and safety perimeters
  • Brief all workers on the demolition plan and safety procedures
  • Prepare necessary equipment and tools for demolition
  • Set up dust control measures
  • Arrange for waste management and recycling
Warning: Always prioritize safety. Ensure all workers are properly trained and equipped. Follow local building codes and regulations. If unsure about any aspect of the process, consult with professional contractors or structural engineers.

4. Final Checks

  • Review and double-check all safety measures
  • Ensure all necessary permits are in place
  • Confirm that neighboring properties have been notified
  • Verify that all valuable items have been removed from the work area
  • Test all communication systems
  • Conduct a final team briefing before commencing work
