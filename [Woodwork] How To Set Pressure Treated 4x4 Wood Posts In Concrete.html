How To Set Pressure Treated 4x4 Wood Posts In Concrete (VERY STRONG!)

Materials Needed:

  • Pressure-treated 4x4 wood posts
  • Quick-setting concrete mix
  • Gravel
  • Water
  • Post hole digger or auger
  • Level
  • Tape measure
  • Shovel
  • Wheelbarrow or mixing tub
  • Safety gear (gloves, safety glasses, dust mask)

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Plan and Mark

Determine the location for your posts and mark them clearly. Ensure they're properly spaced and aligned.

Step 2: Check for Utilities

Before digging, call your local utility companies to mark any underground lines.

Step 3: Dig the Holes

Dig holes that are about three times the width of the post and 1/3 the height of the post plus an additional 6 inches for gravel. For example, for a 6-foot post, dig a hole about 30 inches deep.

Step 4: Add Gravel

Pour about 6 inches of gravel into the bottom of the hole for drainage.

Step 5: Place the Post

Place the 4x4 post in the center of the hole. Use a level to ensure it's perfectly vertical.

Step 6: Brace the Post

Temporarily brace the post to keep it straight while the concrete sets.

Step 7: Mix the Concrete

Follow the manufacturer's instructions to mix the quick-setting concrete. Usually, you'll pour the dry mix into the hole and then add water.

Step 8: Pour the Concrete

Fill the hole with concrete, stopping about 3-4 inches from the surface. Use a stick to poke the concrete and remove air bubbles.

Step 9: Slope the Top

Slope the top of the concrete away from the post to direct water away.

Step 10: Let It Set

Allow the concrete to set according to the manufacturer's instructions, typically 24-48 hours.

Warning:

Always wear appropriate safety gear when working with concrete and power tools. The dust from concrete can be harmful if inhaled.

Pro Tip:

For extra strength, you can add metal brackets or Simpson ties where the post meets the concrete.
