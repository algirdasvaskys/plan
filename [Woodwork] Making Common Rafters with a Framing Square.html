Making Common Rafters with a Framing Square

This guide will walk you through the process of making common rafters using a framing square, including how to cut birdsmouths. A framing square is an essential tool for accurately measuring and marking your rafters.

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Framing square
  • Pencil
  • Tape measure
  • Circular saw or handsaw
  • Lumber for rafters (typically 2x4, 2x6, or 2x8)
  • Safety glasses
  • Work gloves

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Determine the Roof Pitch

Before you begin, you need to know the roof pitch. This is usually expressed as a ratio, such as 4/12 (4 inches of rise for every 12 inches of run).

Step 2: Mark the Plumb Cut

1. Place the framing square on the rafter board.

2. Align the number on the tongue (narrow part) that corresponds to the rise of your roof pitch with the edge of the board.

3. Make sure the 12-inch mark on the body (wider part) of the square is also on the edge of the board.

4. Draw a line along the tongue. This is your plumb cut line.

Step 3: Mark the Bird's Mouth

1. Measure down from the top edge of the rafter along the plumb cut line the depth of the seat cut (typically 1.5 inches for a 2x4 top plate).

2. Make a mark at this point.

3. Place the framing square on the board again, aligning it with this mark and the edge of the board.

4. Draw a line along the body of the square. This is your seat cut line.

Step 4: Mark the Tail Cut

1. Measure from the plumb cut line to where you want the rafter to end.

2. Use the framing square to mark another plumb cut line at this point for the tail cut.

Step 5: Cut the Rafter

1. Put on your safety glasses and work gloves.

2. Using a circular saw or handsaw, carefully cut along the plumb cut lines at both ends of the rafter.

3. Cut out the bird's mouth by making the plumb cut and seat cut where marked.

Step 6: Check and Adjust

1. Test fit your rafter to ensure it sits properly on the top plate and ridge board.

2. Make any necessary adjustments.

3. Use this rafter as a template to mark and cut the remaining rafters.

Tips:

  • Always double-check your measurements before cutting.
  • The depth of the bird's mouth should not exceed 1/3 of the rafter's depth to maintain structural integrity.
  • For more complex roof designs, consider using rafter tables or software to assist with calculations.

Remember, precision is key when making rafters. Take your time with each measurement and cut to ensure a sturdy and well-fitting roof structure.
