How to Make a Simple MCB Trip Indicator

Materials Needed:

  • 1 LED (any color)
  • 1 Resistor (appropriate for your LED and voltage)
  • Wire
  • Electrical tape
  • Small piece of perfboard or breadboard (optional)
  • Soldering iron and solder (if using perfboard)

Steps to Create the MCB Trip Indicator:

Step 1: Understand the Concept

The MCB trip indicator works by connecting an LED in parallel with the MCB. When the MCB trips, the LED will light up, indicating the trip status.

Step 2: Prepare the Components

Calculate the appropriate resistor value for your LED based on the voltage of your electrical system. Typically, a 1kΩ to 10kΩ resistor works well for most applications.

Step 3: Assemble the Circuit

Connect the LED and resistor in series. The anode (longer leg) of the LED should connect to the resistor, and the cathode (shorter leg) to the neutral wire.

Step 4: Install the Indicator

Connect the free end of the resistor to the load side of the MCB. Connect the cathode of the LED to the neutral wire. Ensure all connections are secure and properly insulated.

Step 5: Test the Indicator

Turn on the power and test the MCB. The LED should remain off when the MCB is closed (on) and light up when the MCB trips.

Safety Warning

Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. If you're not comfortable or qualified to work with electrical systems, please consult a licensed electrician. Always turn off the main power before working on any electrical circuits.
