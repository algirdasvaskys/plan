SSR - Solid State Relay Connection with Wi-Fi Controller

Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always ensure power is disconnected before making any connections. If you're not comfortable or qualified to work with electrical systems, please consult a licensed electrician.

Components Needed:

  • Solid State Relay (SSR)
  • Wi-Fi Controller (e.g., ESP8266 or ESP32)
  • Power supply for the Wi-Fi controller
  • Load (e.g., lamp, motor, heater)
  • AC power source
  • Wires and connectors

Connection Steps:

Step 1: Prepare the SSR

Identify the following terminals on your SSR:

  • Input terminals (usually labeled + and -)
  • Output terminals (usually labeled L1 and L2 or Line and Load)

Step 2: Connect the Wi-Fi Controller to the SSR

Connect the Wi-Fi controller to the input side of the SSR:

  • Connect a GPIO pin from the Wi-Fi controller to the + terminal of the SSR
  • Connect the ground (GND) of the Wi-Fi controller to the - terminal of the SSR

Step 3: Connect the Load to the SSR

On the output side of the SSR:

  • Connect one wire from your AC load to one of the SSR output terminals
  • Connect the other wire from your AC load to the neutral wire of your AC power source

Step 4: Connect the AC Power Source

Complete the circuit by connecting the live wire from your AC power source to the remaining output terminal of the SSR.

Step 5: Power the Wi-Fi Controller

Connect your Wi-Fi controller to its power supply.

Step 6: Program the Wi-Fi Controller

Program your Wi-Fi controller to control the GPIO pin connected to the SSR. Here's a basic pseudocode example:


// Set up Wi-Fi connection
setupWiFi();

// Set up GPIO pin
pinMode(SSR_PIN, OUTPUT);

// In your main loop or in response to a command
if (commandReceived == "ON") {
    digitalWrite(SSR_PIN, HIGH);  // Turn on the SSR
} else if (commandReceived == "OFF") {
    digitalWrite(SSR_PIN, LOW);   // Turn off the SSR
}

Safety Considerations:

  • Ensure all connections are secure and properly insulated
  • Use appropriate wire gauges for your load current
  • Consider adding a fuse or circuit breaker for additional protection
  • Ensure proper heat dissipation for the SSR, especially for high-current applications
  • Always follow local electrical codes and regulations

Troubleshooting:

  • If the SSR doesn't switch, check the control voltage from the Wi-Fi controller
  • Verify that the SSR is rated for your load current and voltage
  • Ensure the Wi-Fi controller is properly connected to your network
  • Double-check all wiring connections

Remember, this setup allows you to control your AC load remotely via Wi-Fi. Always prioritize safety when working with electrical systems.
