One-Phase Load Calculation

Introduction

Load calculation in a single-phase electrical system is crucial for determining the power consumption and designing appropriate electrical installations. This document explains the method and provides a practical calculator for one-phase load calculations.

Formula

The basic formula for calculating power in a single-phase system is:

P = V * I * PF

Where:

  • P = Power (in Watts)
  • V = Voltage (in Volts)
  • I = Current (in Amperes)
  • PF = Power Factor (dimensionless, between 0 and 1)

Method

  1. Determine the voltage of your single-phase system (typically 120V or 240V in residential settings).
  2. Measure or estimate the current drawn by the load.
  3. Determine the power factor of the load. For purely resistive loads, it's 1. For most household appliances, it ranges from 0.8 to 0.95.
  4. Apply the formula: multiply voltage, current, and power factor.
  5. The result gives you the real power consumption in Watts.

Practical Example

Let's say you have a single-phase appliance operating at 240V, drawing 10A of current, with a power factor of 0.9:

P = 240V * 10A * 0.9 = 2,160 Watts or 2.16 kW
