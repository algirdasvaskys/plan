USE THIS WEATHERSTRIP ON OLD DOORS!

Save Money, Prevent Cold Air Drafts - BEST DOOR WEATHERSTRIPPING

Weatherstripping your old doors is an excellent way to improve energy efficiency, reduce heating costs, and increase comfort in your home. This guide will walk you through the process of installing the best door weatherstripping to prevent cold air drafts.

Step 1: Gather Materials

  • V-strip weatherstripping (metal or plastic)
  • Measuring tape
  • Scissors or utility knife
  • Screwdriver
  • Cleaning supplies (cloth, mild detergent, water)

Step 2: Measure the Door

Measure the sides and top of the door frame where you'll be applying the weatherstripping. Add an extra inch to each measurement to ensure full coverage.

Step 3: Clean the Door Frame

Thoroughly clean the areas where you'll be applying the weatherstripping. Use a mild detergent and water solution, then dry completely.

Step 4: Cut the Weatherstripping

Cut the V-strip weatherstripping to the measured lengths using scissors or a utility knife.

Step 5: Install the Weatherstripping

  1. Start with one side of the door frame.
  2. Peel off the adhesive backing (if present) or prepare to nail in place.
  3. Position the weatherstripping so the "V" faces outside when the door is closed.
  4. Press firmly to adhere or nail in place, ensuring a tight seal.
  5. Repeat for the other side and top of the door frame.

Step 6: Test the Door

Close the door to ensure it shuts properly and creates a good seal. Make adjustments if necessary.

Tips for Success

  • Choose high-quality weatherstripping for longer-lasting results.
  • For very uneven surfaces, consider using foam tape instead of V-strip.
  • Apply weatherstripping when the temperature is above 20°F (-6°C) for better adhesion.
  • Replace weatherstripping every few years or when you notice wear and tear.

Warning

Ensure the weatherstripping doesn't interfere with the door's locking mechanism or prevent it from closing properly.

Benefits of Proper Weatherstripping

  • Reduced energy bills
  • Improved comfort by eliminating drafts
  • Prevention of moisture, dust, and insects entering your home
  • Reduced noise from outside

By following these steps and using quality weatherstripping, you can significantly improve your old doors' energy efficiency and comfort. Remember to check and maintain your weatherstripping regularly for optimal performance.
