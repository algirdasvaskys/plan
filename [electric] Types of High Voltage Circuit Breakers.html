Types of High Voltage Circuit Breakers

High voltage circuit breakers are crucial components in power systems, grids, and substations. They are designed to interrupt fault currents and isolate faulty sections of the power system. Here are the main types of high voltage circuit breakers:

1. Oil Circuit Breakers

Types:

  • Bulk Oil Circuit Breakers
  • Minimum Oil Circuit Breakers

Oil circuit breakers use oil as an arc-quenching medium. When a fault occurs, the arc is formed underwater, and the heat of the arc vaporizes the surrounding oil. This creates a high-pressure oil vapor that helps extinguish the arc.

2. Air Blast Circuit Breakers

These breakers use a blast of compressed air to extinguish the arc. When a fault occurs, a high-pressure air blast is directed across the arc, cooling it and blowing it out. Air blast circuit breakers are suitable for high voltage applications.

3. Sulfur Hexafluoride (SF6) Circuit Breakers

SF6 circuit breakers use sulfur hexafluoride gas as the arc-quenching medium. SF6 has excellent insulating and arc-quenching properties. These breakers are widely used in high voltage and extra-high voltage applications due to their superior performance and compact size.

4. Vacuum Circuit Breakers

Vacuum circuit breakers interrupt the current by separating contacts in a high vacuum. The vacuum provides excellent insulation and quickly extinguishes the arc. These breakers are commonly used in medium voltage applications and are known for their reliability and long service life.

5. Gas Insulated Switchgear (GIS)

GIS is a compact assembly that combines various high voltage components, including circuit breakers, in a gas-insulated metal enclosure. The insulating gas is typically SF6. GIS is used in substations where space is limited or in harsh environmental conditions.

Comparison of High Voltage Circuit Breakers

Type Advantages Disadvantages
Oil Circuit Breakers
  • Simple construction
  • Low cost
  • Fire hazard
  • Regular maintenance required
Air Blast Circuit Breakers
  • Suitable for high voltages
  • Fast operation
  • Noisy operation
  • Requires air compressor system
SF6 Circuit Breakers
  • Excellent arc-quenching properties
  • Compact size
  • Environmental concerns (SF6 is a greenhouse gas)
  • Higher cost
Vacuum Circuit Breakers
  • Long service life
  • Minimal maintenance
  • Limited to medium voltage applications
  • Higher initial cost
Gas Insulated Switchgear
  • Compact design
  • Suitable for harsh environments
  • High initial cost
  • Specialized maintenance required

Conclusion

The choice of high voltage circuit breaker depends on various factors, including voltage level, fault current level, environmental conditions, space constraints, and cost considerations. Each type of circuit breaker has its advantages and disadvantages, and the selection should be based on the specific requirements of the power system or substation.
