Star Delta Starter Control Wiring Explained

Introduction

A Star Delta Starter is a method of starting a three-phase induction motor to reduce the start-up current. This method is widely used for high power motors to limit the initial inrush current.

Components of a Star Delta Starter

  • Main Contactor (K1)
  • Star Contactor (K2)
  • Delta Contactor (K3)
  • Overload Relay (OL)
  • Timer (TR)
  • Start and Stop Push Buttons

Wiring Explanation

  1. Power Circuit: Connect the three-phase supply to the main contactor K1.
  2. Motor Connections: Connect the six leads of the motor to the appropriate terminals of K1, K2, and K3.
  3. Control Circuit: Wire the control circuit as follows:
    • Connect the start button to the main contactor K1 coil.
    • Wire K1 normally open auxiliary contact in parallel to the start button.
    • Connect the stop button in series with the above circuit.
    • Wire the timer TR coil in parallel with K1 coil.
    • Connect K2 (star contactor) coil to a normally closed contact of TR.
    • Connect K3 (delta contactor) coil to a normally open contact of TR.

Operation Sequence

  1. Press the start button. This energizes K1 and K2, starting the motor in star connection.
  2. K1's auxiliary contact maintains the circuit even after releasing the start button.
  3. The timer TR starts counting.
  4. After a preset time (usually 5-10 seconds), TR switches its contacts.
  5. K2 (star contactor) is de-energized, and K3 (delta contactor) is energized.
  6. The motor now runs in delta connection at full speed.

Note: The transition from star to delta should be quick to prevent motor speed drop. Adjust the timer accordingly.

Safety Considerations

  • Always ensure proper insulation and earthing.
  • Use appropriate wire sizes based on the motor current.
  • Install overload protection devices.
  • Follow local electrical codes and regulations.

This explanation provides a practical understanding of Star Delta Starter control wiring. Always consult with a qualified electrician for specific installations.
