How to Tee into Copper Pipe: The Easy Way

Warning: Working with plumbing can be challenging. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's always best to consult a professional plumber.

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Copper tee fitting
  • Pipe cutter
  • Deburring tool
  • Emery cloth or sandpaper
  • Flux brush
  • Soldering flux
  • Lead-free solder
  • Propane torch
  • Flame-resistant cloth
  • Safety glasses
  • Gloves

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Work Area

Turn off the water supply and drain the pipes. Ensure the area is well-ventilated and keep a fire extinguisher nearby.

Step 2: Mark and Cut the Pipe

Measure and mark where you want to install the tee. Use a pipe cutter to cut the copper pipe at this point.

Step 3: Deburr and Clean the Pipe

Use a deburring tool to remove any burrs from the cut edges. Clean the outside of the pipe ends and the inside of the tee fitting with emery cloth or sandpaper.

Step 4: Apply Flux

Apply a thin, even layer of soldering flux to the outside of both pipe ends and the inside of the tee fitting using a flux brush.

Step 5: Assemble the Fitting

Slide the tee fitting onto the pipe, ensuring it's fully seated and aligned correctly.

Step 6: Heat the Joint

Use a propane torch to heat the joint evenly. Start heating the pipe, then move to the fitting. The flux will liquefy and bubble when the correct temperature is reached.

Step 7: Apply Solder

Touch the lead-free solder to the joint. If it's hot enough, the solder will melt and be drawn into the joint. Continue applying solder until it appears at the end of the fitting.

Step 8: Cool and Clean

Allow the joint to cool naturally. Once cool, wipe away any excess flux with a damp cloth.

Step 9: Test the Joint

Turn the water supply back on and check for any leaks. If you spot any, you may need to drain the pipe and resolder the joint.
