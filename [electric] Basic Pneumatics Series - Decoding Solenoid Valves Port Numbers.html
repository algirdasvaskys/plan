Basic Pneumatics Series - Decoding Solenoid Valves Port Numbers

Introduction

In pneumatic systems, solenoid valves play a crucial role in controlling the flow of compressed air. Understanding how to decode the port numbers on these valves is essential for proper installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting.

Basic Concept

Solenoid valve ports are typically numbered to indicate their function and connection points. The numbering system helps technicians and engineers identify how to connect the valve within a pneumatic circuit correctly.

Common Numbering Systems

There are two primary numbering systems used for solenoid valve ports:

  1. ISO 5599 System: Used internationally and becoming increasingly common.
  2. Traditional System: Still widely used, especially in older equipment.

ISO 5599 Numbering System

The ISO 5599 system uses single-digit numbers for each port:

Port Number Function
1 Pressure supply (inlet)
2, 4 Working ports (outlets to actuator)
3, 5 Exhaust ports

Traditional Numbering System

The traditional system uses letters and numbers:

Port Label Function
P Pressure supply (inlet)
A, B Working ports (outlets to actuator)
R, S, or EA, EB Exhaust ports

Interpreting Port Numbers

To interpret port numbers:

  1. Identify which numbering system is being used (ISO or Traditional).
  2. Locate the pressure supply port (1 or P).
  3. Identify the working ports (2 and 4, or A and B).
  4. Note the exhaust ports (3 and 5, or R and S).
