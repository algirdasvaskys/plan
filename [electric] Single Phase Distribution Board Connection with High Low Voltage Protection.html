Single Phase Distribution Board Connection with High Low Voltage Protection

Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always consult a licensed electrician and follow local electrical codes and regulations.

Components Required:

  • Single Phase Distribution Board
  • Main Circuit Breaker (MCB)
  • Residual Current Device (RCD)
  • High-Low Voltage Protection Relay
  • Contactor
  • Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs) for individual circuits
  • Voltmeter
  • Ammeter
  • Indicator Lights
  • Wires and connectors

Connection Procedure:

Install the Distribution Board: Mount the distribution board securely on the wall.
Connect the Main Power Supply: Connect the incoming power supply (Live and Neutral) to the main MCB.
Install RCD: Connect the output of the main MCB to the input of the RCD.
Install High-Low Voltage Protection Relay: Connect the output of the RCD to the input of the High-Low Voltage Protection Relay.
Install Contactor: Connect the output of the High-Low Voltage Protection Relay to the input of the Contactor.
Connect MCBs: Connect the output of the Contactor to the bus bars that feed the individual MCBs.
Install Voltmeter and Ammeter: Connect the voltmeter in parallel and the ammeter in series with the main power supply.
Connect Indicator Lights: Install indicator lights to show the status of the power supply and protection devices.
Individual Circuit Connections: Connect the loads to the respective MCBs.
Grounding: Ensure proper grounding of the distribution board and all components.

High-Low Voltage Protection Settings:

Configure the High-Low Voltage Protection Relay with appropriate voltage thresholds:

  • High Voltage Cut-off: Typically set at 240V + 10% (264V)
  • Low Voltage Cut-off: Typically set at 240V - 10% (216V)
  • Time Delay: Set an appropriate time delay (e.g., 5 seconds) to prevent nuisance tripping

Safety Considerations:

  • Always use properly rated components and wires.
  • Ensure proper insulation and separation between live parts.
  • Label all circuits and components clearly.
  • Perform thorough testing before energizing the system.
  • Regularly inspect and maintain the distribution board and its components.
Important: After installation, have the entire system inspected and approved by a certified electrician before use.
