Concealed Beam Hanger for Glulam by Simpson: Installation Guide

Introduction

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for installing a concealed beam hanger for glulam beams, manufactured by Simpson Strong-Tie. These hangers are designed to provide a hidden connection for glulam beams, offering both structural integrity and aesthetic appeal.

Warning: Always consult local building codes and structural engineers before installation. Proper installation is crucial for the safety and integrity of the structure.

Materials Needed

  • Simpson Strong-Tie Concealed Beam Hanger (appropriate model for your beam size)
  • Glulam beam
  • Support structure (e.g., wall, beam, or column)
  • Fasteners (as specified by Simpson Strong-Tie for your model)
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Screwdriver or impact driver
  • Level
  • Pencil or marker
  • Measuring tape
  • Safety equipment (goggles, gloves, etc.)

Installation Steps

Step 1: Preparation

Ensure the support structure is properly prepared and capable of bearing the load. Verify that you have the correct hanger model for your glulam beam size and load requirements.

Step 2: Mark the Installation Location

Measure and mark the exact location where the beam will be installed on the support structure. Use a level to ensure the marks are perfectly horizontal.

Step 3: Install the Hanger to the Support Structure

Position the hanger on the support structure according to your marks. Drill pilot holes if required, then fasten the hanger securely using the specified fasteners.

Step 4: Prepare the Glulam Beam

Mark the location on the glulam beam where it will connect with the hanger. This typically involves creating a notch or recess in the beam end to accommodate the concealed portion of the hanger.

Step 5: Install the Beam

Carefully lift the glulam beam and position it onto the installed hanger. Ensure the beam is fully seated in the hanger and level.

Step 6: Secure the Beam to the Hanger

Using the specified fasteners, secure the beam to the hanger. This usually involves driving screws or bolts through pre-drilled holes in the hanger into the beam.

Step 7: Final Checks

Verify that all connections are tight and secure. Check that the beam is level and properly aligned. Make any necessary adjustments.

Maintenance

Regularly inspect the connection for any signs of wear, corrosion, or loosening. Address any issues promptly to maintain the structural integrity of the connection.

Note: This guide provides general instructions. Always refer to the specific installation instructions provided by Simpson Strong-Tie for your particular hanger model, as details may vary.

Conclusion

Proper installation of concealed beam hangers is crucial for both the structural integrity and aesthetic appeal of glulam beam connections. Always prioritize safety and consult professionals when in doubt.
