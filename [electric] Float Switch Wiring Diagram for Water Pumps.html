Float Switch Wiring Diagram for Water Pumps

Wiring Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Components

Ensure you have the following components:

  • Float switch
  • Water pump
  • Power source (usually 120V AC)
  • Electrical wire (14 or 12 gauge, depending on the pump's power requirements)
  • Wire nuts
  • Electrical tape

Step 2: Connect the Power Source to the Float Switch

Connect the hot (black) wire from the power source to one terminal of the float switch.

Step 3: Connect the Float Switch to the Water Pump

Connect the other terminal of the float switch to the hot (black) wire of the water pump. This creates a switched hot connection.

Step 4: Complete the Circuit

Connect the neutral (white) wire from the power source directly to the neutral wire of the water pump.

Step 5: Ground the System

Connect the ground (green or bare copper) wires from the power source and the water pump together and to a proper ground.

Step 6: Secure Connections

Use wire nuts to secure all connections, and wrap them with electrical tape for added protection.

Step 7: Test the System

Before finalizing the installation, test the system to ensure the float switch properly controls the water pump.

Note: This is a basic wiring diagram. Depending on your specific pump and local electrical codes, you may need additional components such as a pump start relay, circuit breaker, or GFCI protection. Always consult local electrical codes and a licensed electrician for complex installations.
