How To Select Right MCB and Overload Relay Size For Motor

Introduction

Selecting the correct size for Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs) and Overload Relays is crucial for the safe and efficient operation of motors. This guide will walk you through the process of choosing the right sizes for these protective devices.

Step 1: Determine Motor Full Load Current (FLC)

The first step is to find the Full Load Current (FLC) of the motor. This information is typically provided on the motor nameplate or in the motor's specifications.

Step 2: Calculate MCB Size

To determine the MCB size, use the following formula:

MCB Rating = 2.5 × FLC

Round up the result to the next standard MCB size. Standard MCB sizes are typically 6A, 10A, 16A, 20A, 25A, 32A, 40A, 50A, 63A, etc.

Step 3: Select Overload Relay Size

For the Overload Relay, use the following guideline:

Overload Relay Setting = 1.15 × FLC to 1.3 × FLC

Choose an Overload Relay with an adjustable range that includes this calculated value.

Example Calculation

Let's consider a motor with the following specifications:

  • Power: 5.5 kW
  • Voltage: 415V (3-phase)
  • Full Load Current (FLC): 11A

MCB Size Calculation:

MCB Rating = 2.5 × 11A = 27.5A

Round up to the next standard size: 32A MCB

Overload Relay Setting:

Minimum setting = 1.15 × 11A = 12.65A
Maximum setting = 1.3 × 11A = 14.3A

Choose an Overload Relay with an adjustable range that includes 12.65A to 14.3A.

Additional Considerations

  • Always refer to local electrical codes and standards when selecting protective devices.
  • Consider the starting current of the motor, which can be 5-7 times the FLC for Direct-On-Line (DOL) starting.
  • For motors with special starting methods (e.g., star-delta, soft starter), consult the manufacturer's guidelines.
  • Ensure the short-circuit breaking capacity of the MCB is suitable for the installation point.

Conclusion

Proper selection of MCB and Overload Relay sizes is essential for motor protection. By following these steps and considering the specific requirements of your installation, you can ensure the safety and longevity of your motor system.
