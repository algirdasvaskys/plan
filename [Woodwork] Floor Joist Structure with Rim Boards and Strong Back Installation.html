The Big Build 19: Completing Floor Joist Structure with Rim Boards and Strong Back Installation

This guide will walk you through the process of completing a floor joist structure, including the installation of rim boards and strong backs. This is a crucial step in ensuring the stability and integrity of your floor system.

Materials Needed:

  • Floor joists (sized according to your plan)
  • Rim boards
  • Strong backs (typically 2x4 or 2x6 lumber)
  • Joist hangers
  • Nails (16d for general framing, 10d for joist hangers)
  • Screws (3-inch deck screws for strong backs)
  • Framing square
  • Measuring tape
  • Circular saw
  • Hammer or nail gun
  • Drill with driver bit
  • Level
  • Chalk line

Step-by-Step Process:

Step 1: Prepare the Area

Ensure that the foundation or support structure is level and ready for joist installation. Clean the area of any debris.

Step 2: Install Rim Boards

Rim boards are the outer frame of your floor system:

  1. Cut rim boards to fit the perimeter of your floor plan.
  2. Attach rim boards to the sill plate or foundation wall using 16d nails, ensuring they're level and flush with the outside edge.
  3. Use a framing square to ensure corners are at 90-degree angles.

Step 3: Mark Joist Locations

Typically, floor joists are spaced 16 inches on center:

  1. Use a measuring tape and chalk line to mark joist positions on the rim boards.
  2. Double-check measurements to ensure accurate spacing.

Step 4: Install Joist Hangers

Joist hangers provide support for each joist:

  1. Align joist hangers with your marked locations on the rim boards.
  2. Nail joist hangers in place using 10d nails, ensuring they're flush and level.

Step 5: Place and Secure Floor Joists

  1. Cut joists to the required length if necessary.
  2. Place each joist into its corresponding hanger.
  3. Nail the joist to the hanger using 10d nails, following the manufacturer's nailing pattern.
  4. Ensure each joist is level and plumb before fully securing.

Step 6: Install Strong Backs

Strong backs (or blocking) provide additional support and prevent joist twisting:

  1. Cut strong backs to fit snugly between joists at the midpoint of the span.
  2. Install strong backs in a straight line across the middle of the floor system.
  3. Secure each strong back to the joists using 3-inch deck screws, two at each end.

Step 7: Final Checks

  1. Verify that all joists are securely fastened to hangers and rim boards.
  2. Check that strong backs are properly aligned and secured.
  3. Ensure the entire structure is level and square.

Safety Warning

Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment, including safety glasses, gloves, and a hard hat. Use caution when working with power tools and at heights. If you're unsure about any step, consult a professional.

Pro Tip

For longer spans, consider using engineered wood I-joists or floor trusses for improved strength and reduced deflection. Always consult local building codes and structural plans before beginning construction.
