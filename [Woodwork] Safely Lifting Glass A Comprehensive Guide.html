Safely Lifting Glass: A Comprehensive Guide

1. Preparation

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE):
    • Safety glasses or goggles
    • Cut-resistant gloves
    • Steel-toed safety shoes
    • Long-sleeved shirt and long pants
  • Clear the path to your destination
  • Ensure the destination area is prepared and clear

2. Assessing the Glass

  • Determine the weight and size of the glass
  • Check for any cracks, chips, or damage
  • Decide if you need assistance based on the glass size and weight

3. Proper Lifting Technique

  • Stand close to the glass
  • Bend your knees and keep your back straight
  • Grip the glass firmly on the edges or use suction cups for larger pieces
  • Lift with your legs, not your back
  • Keep the glass close to your body
  • Move slowly and deliberately
Important: Never carry glass on your shoulder or under your arm. Always keep it vertical and perpendicular to the ground.

4. Moving with the Glass

  • Walk slowly and carefully
  • Take small steps
  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Avoid twisting your body
  • If you need to change direction, move your feet, not your body

5. Setting Down the Glass

  • Slowly bend your knees
  • Keep your back straight
  • Gently place the glass on a soft surface or padding
  • Ensure the glass is stable before releasing your grip

6. Additional Safety Tips

  • Always use mechanical aids (e.g., vacuum lifters, glass dollies) for very large or heavy glass pieces
  • Work in pairs for large or awkward pieces
  • Take breaks if moving multiple pieces to avoid fatigue
  • Be extra cautious in wet or slippery conditions
