Auto Transformer Starter Power Wiring Connection Explained

Warning: Working with electrical connections can be dangerous. Always ensure that power is disconnected and proper safety procedures are followed. If you're not qualified, please consult a licensed electrician.

Introduction

An auto transformer starter is a type of reduced voltage starter used for starting three-phase induction motors. It helps reduce the initial inrush current and provides a smooth acceleration of the motor. This document explains the power wiring connection for an auto transformer starter.

Components

  • Auto transformer
  • Main contactor
  • Starting contactor
  • Overload relay
  • Control circuit (not covered in this guide)

Wiring Connection Steps

  1. Connect the three-phase power supply (L1, L2, L3) to the main contactor's input terminals.
  2. Connect the output terminals of the main contactor to the auto transformer's primary windings.
  3. Connect the auto transformer's secondary (tapped) windings to the starting contactor's input terminals.
  4. Connect the output terminals of the starting contactor to the motor terminals (U, V, W).
  5. Connect the overload relay in series with the motor circuit, typically on the output side of the starting contactor.
  6. Connect the neutral point of the auto transformer (if available) to the neutral of the power supply.
  7. Ensure proper grounding of all components and the motor frame.

Operation Sequence

  1. When the start command is given, the starting contactor closes first.
  2. The motor starts with reduced voltage through the auto transformer taps.
  3. After a preset time or when the motor reaches a certain speed, the main contactor closes.
  4. Simultaneously, the starting contactor opens, connecting the motor directly to the full line voltage.
  5. The motor continues to run at full voltage.

Important Considerations

  • Ensure proper sizing of the auto transformer based on the motor's requirements.
  • Use appropriate wire sizes for the current ratings of the motor and components.
  • Implement proper control circuitry for automatic operation and protection.
  • Regular maintenance and inspection of connections are crucial for safe operation.

Conclusion

Proper wiring of an auto transformer starter is crucial for the safe and efficient starting of large three-phase induction motors. Always follow local electrical codes and manufacturer's guidelines when installing and operating this equipment.
