Ceiling Fan Connection: Three Wire and Four Wire With Capacitor

Warning: Working with electrical connections can be dangerous. If you're not confident in your ability to perform these tasks safely, please consult a licensed electrician.

Three-Wire Ceiling Fan Connection

A three-wire ceiling fan typically includes the following wires:

  • Black: Live/Hot wire
  • White: Neutral wire
  • Green or Bare Copper: Ground wire

Connection Steps:

  1. Connect the black (hot) wire from the ceiling to the black wire on the fan.
  2. Connect the white (neutral) wire from the ceiling to the white wire on the fan.
  3. Connect the green or bare copper (ground) wire from the ceiling to the ground wire on the fan.

Four-Wire Ceiling Fan Connection

A four-wire ceiling fan typically includes the following wires:

  • Black: Live/Hot wire for the fan motor
  • Red or Blue: Live/Hot wire for the light kit
  • White: Neutral wire
  • Green or Bare Copper: Ground wire

Connection Steps:

  1. Connect the black (hot) wire from the ceiling to the black wire on the fan.
  2. Connect the red or blue wire from the ceiling to the wire for the light kit on the fan.
  3. Connect the white (neutral) wire from the ceiling to the white wire on the fan.
  4. Connect the green or bare copper (ground) wire from the ceiling to the ground wire on the fan.

Using a Capacitor

A capacitor is often used in ceiling fans to control the fan's speed. Here's how to connect it:

Capacitor Connection Steps:

  1. Locate the capacitor. It's usually a small cylindrical component.
  2. The capacitor will have two wires coming out of it.
  3. Connect one wire from the capacitor to the black (hot) wire for the fan motor.
  4. Connect the other wire from the capacitor to one of the speed control wires from the fan's switch.
  5. The remaining speed control wires from the switch should be connected to the appropriate terminals on the fan motor.

Important Notes

  • Always turn off the power at the circuit breaker before working on any electrical connections.
  • Use wire nuts to secure all connections.
  • If your fan comes with a blue wire instead of a red wire for the light kit, treat it the same as you would a red wire.
  • Some fans may have additional wires for features like reverse function or multiple light kits. Always refer to the manufacturer's instructions for specific wiring diagrams.
  • If you're unsure about any step in the process, consult a professional electrician.
