Building a Pool: Rendering and Tanking Guide

This guide will walk you through the process of rendering and tanking a pool to ensure a perfect, long-lasting finish.

1. Preparation

Step 1: Clean the pool shell thoroughly, removing any debris, dust, or loose materials.

Step 2: Repair any cracks or imperfections in the concrete shell.

Step 3: Install all necessary plumbing and electrical components.

2. Rendering

Step 1: Mix the render according to manufacturer's instructions. Typically, this is a mixture of cement, fine sand, and water.

Step 2: Dampen the pool surface to improve adhesion.

Step 3: Apply the first coat of render (scratch coat) using a trowel, ensuring even coverage.

Step 4: Allow the first coat to partially set, then scratch the surface to create a key for the next coat.

Step 5: Apply the second coat of render, smoothing it out for a uniform finish.

Step 6: Allow the render to cure for at least 7 days, keeping it damp during this period to prevent cracking.

3. Tanking

Step 1: Ensure the rendered surface is clean and free from dust.

Step 2: Apply a waterproofing primer to the entire pool surface.

Step 3: Mix the tanking compound according to manufacturer's instructions.

Step 4: Apply the first coat of tanking compound using a brush or roller, ensuring even coverage.

Step 5: Allow the first coat to dry completely (usually 24 hours).

Step 6: Apply a second coat of tanking compound, ensuring all areas are well covered.

Step 7: Allow the tanking to cure fully before filling the pool (typically 7 days).

4. Finishing Touches

Step 1: Install pool tiles if desired, using a suitable adhesive.

Step 2: Grout the tiles and clean off any excess.

Step 3: Install pool fittings such as lights, skimmer boxes, and return jets.

Step 4: Fill the pool with water and balance the chemical levels.

Important Notes:

  • Always follow manufacturer's instructions for all products used.
  • Ensure proper ventilation when working with chemicals.
  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) throughout the process.
  • Consider hiring a professional if you're unsure about any step in the process.
