A Simple Trick to Modify the CT Ratio to Connect with Ammeter

Warning: Working with electrical equipment can be dangerous. Always follow proper safety procedures and consult with a qualified electrician before making any modifications.

Introduction

Current Transformers (CTs) are widely used in electrical systems to measure high currents. Sometimes, you may need to modify the CT ratio to connect it with an ammeter. This guide will walk you through the process.

Materials Needed

  • Current Transformer (CT)
  • Ammeter
  • Insulated wire
  • Wire cutters and strippers
  • Electrical tape
  • Multimeter (for testing)

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Understand the current CT ratio

Before modifying, know your CT's current ratio. For example, a 100:5 CT means it can measure up to 100A and outputs 5A.

Step 2: Determine the desired ratio

Decide on the new ratio you need. For instance, if you want to measure 20A with a 5A ammeter, you'll need a 20:5 ratio.

Step 3: Calculate the number of turns

Use this formula: New turns = (Original primary current / Desired primary current) x Original turns
For example: (100A / 20A) x 1 turn = 5 turns

Step 4: Prepare the wire

Cut a length of insulated wire that can wrap around the CT core the calculated number of times.

Step 5: Wind the wire

Carefully wind the wire around the CT core the calculated number of times. Ensure the windings are tight and don't overlap.

Step 6: Secure the winding

Use electrical tape to secure the windings in place, ensuring they don't move or come loose.

Step 7: Connect to the ammeter

Connect the ends of your newly wound wire to the ammeter, observing proper polarity.

Step 8: Test the setup

Use a known current source and a multimeter to verify that your modified CT and ammeter are measuring correctly.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you can modify a CT's ratio to work with your ammeter. Remember to always prioritize safety and consult with a professional if you're unsure about any step in the process.
