Making a Simple Flat Roof Structure with Trimmers for a Lantern or Roof Light

Materials Needed:

  • Timber joists (size depends on span and loading requirements)
  • Timber for trimmers and headers
  • Joist hangers
  • Nails or screws
  • Plywood or OSB board for decking
  • Roofing felt or membrane
  • Insulation

Tools Required:

  • Circular saw or handsaw
  • Drill with appropriate bits
  • Hammer
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Level
  • Safety equipment (goggles, gloves, etc.)

Safety Warning:

Always prioritize safety when working on roofs. Ensure proper fall protection is in place, and if you're not confident in your abilities, consult a professional.

Method:

Step 1: Plan and Measure

Determine the size of your roof and the location of the lantern or roof light. Calculate the joist spacing based on the load requirements and span.

Step 2: Install the Main Joists

Cut the joists to size and install them at the calculated spacing. Secure them to the walls using joist hangers.

Step 3: Mark the Opening

Mark the location of the lantern or roof light opening on the joists.

Step 4: Install Trimmers

Cut and install trimmers perpendicular to the joists at the marked locations. These will form the short sides of the opening.

Step 5: Install Headers

Cut and install headers parallel to the joists, connecting the trimmers. These form the long sides of the opening.

Step 6: Secure Connections

Use joist hangers to secure the connections between the trimmers, headers, and main joists.

Step 7: Install Decking

Cover the joists with plywood or OSB board, leaving the opening for the lantern or roof light.

Step 8: Apply Waterproofing

Apply roofing felt or membrane over the decking, ensuring proper overlap and sealing around the opening.

Step 9: Install Insulation

Install insulation between the joists, ensuring proper ventilation if required.

Step 10: Install Lantern or Roof Light

Follow the manufacturer's instructions to install the lantern or roof light in the prepared opening.

Step 11: Finish and Seal

Finish the roof edges and ensure all joints and connections are properly sealed to prevent water ingress.

Remember that building regulations and requirements may vary depending on your location. Always check local building codes and obtain necessary permits before starting your project.
