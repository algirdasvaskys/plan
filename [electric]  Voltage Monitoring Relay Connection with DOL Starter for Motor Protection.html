Voltage Monitoring Relay Connection with DOL Starter for Motor Protection

1. Introduction

Connecting a Voltage Monitoring Relay with a Direct-On-Line (DOL) Starter is an effective method for protecting motors against voltage fluctuations. This setup helps prevent damage to the motor caused by undervoltage, overvoltage, or phase loss conditions.

2. Components Required

a. Voltage Monitoring Relay (VMR)

A device that monitors the voltage supply and trips when voltage levels are outside the set parameters.

b. Direct-On-Line (DOL) Starter

A motor starting method that connects the motor directly to the main power supply.

c. Contactor

An electrically controlled switch used for switching an electrical power circuit.

d. Overload Relay

A device that protects the motor from overload conditions.

e. Circuit Breaker or Fuses

For short circuit and overcurrent protection.

3. Connection Steps

Step 1: Power Supply Connection

Connect the three-phase power supply to the main circuit breaker or fuse unit.

Step 2: Voltage Monitoring Relay Connection

Connect the input terminals of the VMR to the output of the circuit breaker or fuse unit. This allows the VMR to monitor the incoming voltage.

Step 3: Control Circuit Connection

Connect the output contact of the VMR in series with the control circuit of the DOL starter. This ensures that the starter can only operate when the voltage is within acceptable limits.

Step 4: DOL Starter Connection

Connect the output of the circuit breaker to the input of the DOL starter's contactor.

Step 5: Overload Relay Connection

Connect the overload relay in series with the motor windings. The overload relay should be properly sized for the motor's full load current.

Step 6: Motor Connection

Connect the output of the DOL starter to the motor terminals, ensuring proper phase sequence.

4. Operation

When the power is supplied, the VMR monitors the voltage. If the voltage is within the set parameters, it allows the control circuit to operate. The DOL starter can then be activated to start the motor. If at any point the voltage falls outside the acceptable range, the VMR will trip, opening the control circuit and stopping the motor.

5. Safety Considerations

Warning: Always ensure that the power is disconnected before working on electrical connections. Only qualified electricians should perform these connections.
  • Ensure all components are properly rated for the motor and supply voltage.
  • Verify that all connections are tight and secure.
  • Use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) when working with electrical systems.
  • Follow all local electrical codes and regulations.
  • Perform thorough testing of the system before putting it into regular operation.

6. Maintenance

Regular maintenance should include:

  • Checking all connections for tightness
  • Inspecting components for signs of wear or damage
  • Testing the operation of the VMR and DOL starter
  • Verifying that the overload relay is set correctly
  • Keeping the equipment clean and free from dust and debris
