Valley Roof Construction: Noggins

Introduction

Noggins, also known as blocking or dwangs, are essential components in valley roof construction. They provide crucial support and stability to the roof structure, particularly in the valley area where two roof planes intersect.

Purpose of Noggins in Valley Roof Construction

  • Provide lateral support to rafters
  • Prevent twisting or warping of rafters
  • Distribute loads evenly across the roof structure
  • Create a solid fixing point for roof sheeting or battens
  • Enhance the overall structural integrity of the roof

Materials Needed

  • Timber (usually the same size as the rafters)
  • Nails or screws
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil or marker
  • Saw (hand saw or power saw)
  • Level
  • Safety equipment (gloves, safety glasses, etc.)

Step-by-Step Installation Process

  1. Measure the distance between rafters where noggins will be installed.
  2. Cut the noggins to the correct length, ensuring a snug fit between rafters.
  3. Mark the position of the noggins on the rafters. They should be level and aligned with each other.
  4. Install the first noggin at the top of the valley, just below the ridge board.
  5. Work your way down the valley, installing noggins at regular intervals (typically 400-600mm apart).
  6. Ensure each noggin is level before fixing it in place.
  7. Nail or screw the noggins securely to the rafters on both sides.
  8. Install additional noggins near the bottom of the valley for extra support.
  9. Check all noggins are firmly in place and provide adequate support.

Tips for Proper Installation

Tip 1: Use a template to mark and cut noggins quickly and accurately.

Tip 2: Pre-drill holes in the noggins to prevent splitting, especially when working with hardwoods.

Tip 3: Stagger the noggins slightly to allow for easier nailing or screwing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Warning 1: Don't install noggins too far apart, as this can compromise roof stability.

Warning 2: Avoid using undersized timber for noggins, as they may not provide adequate support.

Warning 3: Don't forget to install noggins at the bottom of the valley, where additional support is crucial.

Conclusion

Proper installation of noggins in valley roof construction is crucial for the structural integrity and longevity of the roof. By following these guidelines and best practices, you can ensure a strong and stable valley roof structure.
