Difference Between Motor STAR Connection and DELTA Connection

Introduction

In three-phase electrical systems, two common ways to connect the windings of motors, generators, and transformers are the Star (Y) connection and the Delta (Δ) connection. Each has its own characteristics and applications.

STAR (Y) Connection

In a Star connection:

  • The three phases are connected to a common point, called the neutral point or star point.
  • The other ends of the phases are connected to the three line terminals.
  • The voltage between any line and the neutral is called the phase voltage.
  • The voltage between any two lines is called the line voltage.

DELTA (Δ) Connection

In a Delta connection:

  • The three phases are connected end-to-end, forming a closed loop.
  • There is no neutral point in a delta connection.
  • The voltage across each phase winding is the same as the line voltage.

Key Differences

Aspect STAR Connection DELTA Connection
Neutral Point Present Absent
Voltage Relationship Vline = √3 × Vphase Vline = Vphase
Current Relationship Iline = Iphase Iline = √3 × Iphase
Power P = √3 × VL × IL × cos φ P = √3 × VL × IL × cos φ
Starting Current Lower Higher
Starting Torque Lower Higher

Applications

STAR Connection

  • Used for starting high-power motors to reduce initial current surge
  • Preferred for long transmission lines due to lower line currents
  • Suitable for low-speed, high-torque applications

DELTA Connection

  • Used for running motors at full speed
  • Preferred for short transmission lines and high-current applications
  • Suitable for high-speed, low-torque applications

Star-Delta Starter

Many industrial motors use a star-delta starter, which initially connects the motor in star configuration for starting, then switches to delta configuration for running. This provides the benefits of both configurations:

  • Reduced starting current (star connection)
  • Full power and efficiency during operation (delta connection)
