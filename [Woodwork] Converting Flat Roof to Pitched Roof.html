Converting a Flat Roof to a Pitched Roof

Converting a flat roof to a pitched roof can improve drainage, increase attic space, and enhance the overall aesthetics of a building. This guide outlines the process of stripping the existing structure and installing a new pitched roof.

Warning: This is a complex structural change that requires professional expertise. Always consult with a licensed structural engineer and obtain necessary permits before beginning this project.

Step-by-Step Process

1. Planning and Preparation

  • Assess the existing structure and determine if it can support a pitched roof
  • Obtain necessary permits and approvals
  • Create detailed plans and blueprints
  • Choose roofing materials and pitch angle
  • Arrange for temporary weatherproofing during construction

2. Stripping the Existing Roof

  • Remove all roofing materials (e.g., gravel, asphalt, membrane)
  • Inspect and repair the roof deck if necessary
  • Remove any unnecessary roof structures (e.g., vents, chimneys) that won't be part of the new design

3. Reinforcing the Existing Structure

  • Add additional support beams or trusses as needed
  • Strengthen wall plates to support the new roof load
  • Install collar ties to prevent walls from spreading under the new roof's weight

4. Building the New Roof Frame

  • Construct and install new roof trusses or rafters
  • Add ridge beams and hip rafters as required by the design
  • Install purlins to support roofing materials

5. Installing Roof Decking

  • Apply plywood or oriented strand board (OSB) sheathing over the new frame
  • Ensure proper spacing between panels for expansion
  • Secure decking with appropriate fasteners

6. Adding Waterproofing and Insulation

  • Install a waterproof underlayment over the decking
  • Apply ice and water shield in vulnerable areas (e.g., eaves, valleys)
  • Install insulation between rafters or in the attic space

7. Installing Roofing Materials

  • Apply starter strips along the eaves
  • Install chosen roofing materials (e.g., shingles, tiles, metal panels)
  • Add flashing around chimneys, vents, and other penetrations
  • Install ridge caps to finish the roof peak

8. Finishing Touches

  • Install gutters and downspouts
  • Add ventilation systems (e.g., soffit vents, ridge vents)
  • Conduct a final inspection to ensure proper installation and water-tightness

Important Considerations

  • Ensure proper load calculations are done to verify the existing structure can support the new roof
  • Consider the impact on neighboring properties and local building codes
  • Plan for potential interior work, such as adjusting ceilings or adding attic access
  • Factor in the cost of materials, labor, and potential unforeseen issues
  • Consider energy efficiency improvements during the conversion process

Remember, converting a flat roof to a pitched roof is a significant undertaking that requires careful planning, professional expertise, and adherence to local building codes. Always work with licensed professionals to ensure a safe and successful conversion.
