How to Remove Rivets from Gutters (Quick Guide)

This guide will walk you through the process of removing rivets from both aluminum and plastic gutters. Follow these steps carefully to ensure a successful removal without damaging your gutters.

⚠️ Safety Warning

Always wear safety glasses and work gloves when removing rivets. Be cautious of sharp edges and falling debris.

Tools You'll Need:

  • Drill with metal drill bits
  • Center punch
  • Hammer
  • Pliers
  • Flathead screwdriver
  • Safety glasses
  • Work gloves

Method 1: Drilling Out the Rivet

  1. Put on your safety glasses and work gloves.
  2. Identify the rivet you want to remove.
  3. Use a center punch and hammer to create a small indentation in the center of the rivet head. This will help guide your drill bit.
  4. Select a drill bit slightly smaller than the rivet's shaft diameter.
  5. Carefully drill into the center of the rivet head. Apply steady pressure and drill slowly to avoid damaging the surrounding gutter material.
  6. Continue drilling until the head of the rivet separates from the shaft.
  7. Use pliers to remove any remaining pieces of the rivet shaft.

Method 2: Chiseling the Rivet (for stubborn rivets)

  1. Place the tip of a flathead screwdriver against the edge of the rivet head.
  2. Gently tap the screwdriver with a hammer to begin separating the rivet head from the shaft.
  3. Work your way around the rivet head, tapping the screwdriver at different points.
  4. Once the head is loose, use pliers to pull it off completely.
  5. Push or pull the remaining shaft through the hole.

Special Considerations for Plastic Gutters

When working with plastic gutters, keep these points in mind:

  • Use a lower drill speed to avoid melting the plastic.
  • Be extra careful not to apply too much pressure, as plastic can crack more easily than aluminum.
  • If using the chiseling method, be very gentle to avoid cracking or damaging the plastic.

After Removing the Rivet

  • Inspect the hole for any damage or burrs.
  • Smooth any rough edges with a small file if necessary.
  • If you plan to reattach the gutter section, consider using gutter screws instead of rivets for easier future maintenance.

⚠️ Important Note

If you're unsure about your ability to remove rivets safely or if your gutters are in a difficult-to-reach location, consider hiring a professional to avoid potential injury or damage to your home.
