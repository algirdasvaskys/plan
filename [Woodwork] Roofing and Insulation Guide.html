Roofing and Insulation Guide

Roofing Square Update

A roofing square is a unit of measurement used in the roofing industry. It equals 100 square feet of roof area. Recent updates in the industry include:

  • Increased focus on energy-efficient materials
  • Integration of solar panels into roofing squares
  • Use of recycled materials in roofing production
  • Enhanced durability standards for extreme weather conditions

Insulation Tips

Tip 1: Assess your current insulation and identify areas for improvement.
Tip 2: Choose the right type of insulation for each area of your home (e.g., fiberglass for attics, spray foam for walls).
Tip 3: Pay special attention to attics, as they are often the biggest source of heat loss.
Tip 4: Don't forget to insulate pipes and ducts in unconditioned spaces.
Tip 5: Consider professional installation for best results, especially for spray foam insulation.

Gapotape: A Revolutionary Insulation Product

Gapotape is an innovative product designed to improve insulation in buildings. Here are its key features:

  • Versatility: Can be used to seal gaps and cracks in various areas of a building
  • Easy Application: Comes in tape form, making it simple to apply even in tight spaces
  • High Performance: Provides excellent air sealing and insulation properties
  • Durability: Resistant to moisture, mold, and aging
  • Energy Efficiency: Helps reduce energy costs by preventing air leakage

To use Gapotape effectively:

  1. Clean the surface where you'll apply the tape
  2. Measure and cut the tape to the required length
  3. Remove the backing and apply the tape, pressing firmly to ensure good adhesion
  4. Smooth out any bubbles or wrinkles
  5. For best results, apply at temperatures above 40°F (4°C)
