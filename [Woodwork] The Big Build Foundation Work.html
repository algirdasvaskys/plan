The Big Build: Digging Foundations, Shuttering Bad Ground, and Pouring Concrete

1. Site Preparation

1.1 Site Survey

Conduct a thorough site survey to determine soil conditions, ground water levels, and any potential obstacles.

1.2 Marking Out

Use stakes and string lines to mark out the foundation area according to architectural plans.

2. Excavation

2.1 Topsoil Removal

Remove topsoil and vegetation from the marked area.

2.2 Digging

Excavate to the required depth using appropriate machinery (e.g., excavators, backhoes).

Warning: Always call local utilities to mark underground lines before digging.

3. Dealing with Bad Ground

3.1 Assessment

Evaluate the excavated area for any soft spots, organic material, or unsuitable soil.

3.2 Soil Replacement

Remove bad soil and replace with compacted granular fill or engineered fill as specified.

4. Shuttering (Formwork)

4.1 Formwork Installation

Install wooden or steel formwork around the perimeter of the foundation area.

4.2 Bracing

Secure formwork with braces to ensure it remains straight and plumb during concrete pouring.

5. Reinforcement

5.1 Steel Placement

Place steel reinforcement bars (rebar) or mesh according to structural engineer's specifications.

5.2 Spacers

Use concrete spacers to ensure proper concrete cover over reinforcement.

6. Concrete Pouring

6.1 Mixing

Prepare concrete mix according to specified strength and slump requirements.

6.2 Pouring

Pour concrete into formwork, ensuring even distribution and proper filling around reinforcement.

6.3 Vibrating

Use concrete vibrators to eliminate air pockets and ensure proper consolidation.

7. Finishing and Curing

7.1 Leveling

Level and smooth the top surface of the concrete using appropriate tools.

7.2 Curing

Cover the concrete with plastic sheeting or apply curing compound to ensure proper hydration and strength development.

8. Formwork Removal

8.1 Timing

Remove formwork after concrete has reached sufficient strength (typically 24-48 hours for walls, 5-7 days for suspended slabs).

8.2 Inspection

Inspect the cured concrete for any defects or honeycombing, and repair if necessary.

Important Note: Always consult with a structural engineer and adhere to local building codes throughout the foundation construction process.
