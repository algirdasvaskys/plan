Street Light Wiring with Auto/Manual Selector Switch and Timer

Components Required:

  • Street Light Fixture
  • Photocell Sensor
  • Timer
  • Auto/Manual Selector Switch (DPDT - Double Pole Double Throw)
  • Contactor
  • MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker)
  • Power Supply

Wiring Method:

  1. Connect the power supply to the MCB.
  2. From the MCB, connect one wire to the common terminal of the Auto/Manual selector switch.
  3. Connect the "Auto" terminal of the selector switch to the timer input.
  4. Connect the timer output to the photocell sensor input.
  5. Connect the photocell sensor output to one terminal of the contactor coil.
  6. Connect the "Manual" terminal of the selector switch directly to one terminal of the contactor coil.
  7. Connect the other terminal of the contactor coil to the neutral wire.
  8. Connect the contactor's main contacts between the power supply and the street light fixture.

Operation:

In "Auto" mode, the timer will activate the circuit during scheduled hours. Within those hours, the photocell sensor will turn the lights on when it gets dark and off when it becomes light again. In "Manual" mode, the lights can be turned on and off regardless of the time or ambient light conditions.

Safety Considerations:

  • Ensure all connections are properly insulated.
  • Use appropriate gauge wires for the current load.
  • Install the system in a weather-proof enclosure.
  • Follow local electrical codes and regulations.
  • If unsure, consult a licensed electrician.
