Rafter Making and Building a Lean-to Roof

Safety First

Always wear appropriate safety gear, including safety glasses, gloves, and a hard hat. Use caution when working at heights and with power tools.

Tools Needed

  • Circular saw
  • Framing square
  • Tape measure
  • Pencil
  • Hammer
  • Nail gun (optional)
  • Level
  • Ladder

Materials Needed

  • 2x4 or 2x6 lumber (depending on span and load)
  • Roofing nails
  • Joist hangers
  • Hurricane ties
  • Roofing material (shingles, metal sheets, etc.)
  • Underlayment
  • Fascia board

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Measure and Plan

Measure the width of the building and decide on the pitch of your lean-to roof. A common pitch for a lean-to is 3/12 (3 inches of rise for every 12 inches of run).

Step 2: Calculate Rafter Length

Use the Pythagorean theorem to calculate the rafter length: sqrt((rise^2) + (run^2)). Add extra length for overhang if desired.

Step 3: Cut a Pattern Rafter

Use your framing square to mark the bird's mouth cut (where the rafter meets the wall) and the plumb cut (at the peak). Cut a single rafter as a pattern.

Step 4: Cut Remaining Rafters

Use your pattern rafter to mark and cut the remaining rafters. Typically, rafters are spaced 16 or 24 inches apart.

Step 5: Install Ledger Board

Attach a ledger board to the existing structure, ensuring it's level and properly fastened to wall studs or other structural supports.

Step 6: Install Rafters

Begin installing rafters, starting at one end. Use joist hangers to attach rafters to the ledger board. Ensure each rafter is plumb before nailing.

Step 7: Install Fascia Board

Attach the fascia board to the ends of the rafters, ensuring it's straight and level.

Step 8: Add Roof Sheathing

Install roof sheathing (typically plywood or OSB) over the rafters. Start at the bottom and work your way up, staggering the seams.

Step 9: Apply Underlayment

Cover the sheathing with roofing underlayment, starting at the bottom and overlapping each layer as you move up.

Step 10: Install Roofing Material

Apply your chosen roofing material according to manufacturer's instructions, starting at the bottom edge and working upwards.

Step 11: Finishing Touches

Install any necessary flashing, particularly where the lean-to meets the existing structure. Add gutters if desired.

Remember, building codes vary by location. Always check local regulations and consider hiring a professional for complex projects or if you're unsure about any steps in the process.
