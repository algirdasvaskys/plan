Timber Featheredge Cladding: The Carpenter's Method

This guide will walk you through the process of setting out, measuring, cutting, and fixing timber featheredge cladding using traditional carpentry techniques.

1. Setting Out

  1. Inspect the wall surface for evenness. Address any irregularities.
  2. Install a damp-proof course (DPC) if the cladding starts near ground level.
  3. Fix vertical battens to the wall at regular intervals (typically 400-600mm apart).
  4. Ensure battens are plumb using a spirit level.
  5. Install additional battens around openings and at corners.
Tip: Use treated timber for battens to prevent rot and insect infestation.

2. Measuring

  1. Measure the height of the wall to determine the number of boards needed.
  2. Calculate the exposed face of each board (typically 125-150mm).
  3. Measure and mark the starting point for the first board, ensuring it's level.
  4. Use a story pole to mark consistent board heights along the wall.
Tip: Allow for a 15mm overlap between boards for proper water run-off.

3. Cutting

  1. Use a sharp handsaw or power saw to cut boards to length.
  2. For angled cuts at corners, use a miter saw or carefully mark and cut by hand.
  3. Sand cut edges to remove splinters and achieve a clean finish.
  4. Pre-drill nail holes to prevent splitting, especially near board ends.
Tip: Cut boards slightly longer than needed and trim to fit for a precise installation.

4. Fixing

  1. Start at the bottom of the wall, fixing a starter strip to support the first board.
  2. Nail the first board in place, ensuring it's level.
  3. Use galvanized or stainless steel nails to prevent rusting.
  4. Continue upwards, overlapping each board by 15mm.
  5. Check for level and plumb every few boards.
  6. Cut and fit boards around windows, doors, and other openings.
  7. Install corner boards or miter joints at external corners.
Tip: Leave a small expansion gap (about 3mm) between boards to allow for natural wood movement.

5. Finishing Touches

  1. Install trim pieces around windows and doors.
  2. Apply a suitable wood preservative or paint to protect the cladding.
  3. Caulk any gaps to ensure weathertightness.
  4. Clean up the work area and dispose of waste responsibly.
Tip: Regular maintenance, including cleaning and repainting, will extend the life of your featheredge cladding.

By following these steps, you'll be able to install timber featheredge cladding like a professional carpenter. Remember to always prioritize safety, use appropriate tools, and work methodically for the best results.
