Carpentry Tip: How to Check for Square

This simple yet effective method, known as the "3-4-5 rule" or "Pythagorean triple," is a quick way to check if corners are square without using specialized tools.

Materials Needed:

  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • The object or surface you want to check for square

Steps:

Step 1: Measure along one edge

Starting from the corner you want to check, measure and mark 3 units (e.g., 3 feet or 30 inches) along one edge.

Step 2: Measure along the perpendicular edge

From the same corner, measure and mark 4 units along the edge perpendicular to the first measurement.

Step 3: Measure the diagonal

Measure the distance between the two marks you made. If the corner is perfectly square, this diagonal measurement should be exactly 5 units.

Step 4: Check the result

If the diagonal measures exactly 5 units, your corner is square. If it's less than 5, the angle is less than 90 degrees. If it's more than 5, the angle is greater than 90 degrees.

Why this works:

This method is based on the Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²). In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (longest side) is equal to the sum of squares of the other two sides. 3² + 4² = 5², which is why these numbers work perfectly for checking square corners.

Tips:

  • You can use any multiple of 3-4-5 for larger projects (e.g., 6-8-10, 9-12-15).
  • For small projects, you can use centimeters or inches instead of feet.
  • Always measure carefully and precisely for the most accurate results.
  • This method works for checking square corners on floors, walls, frames, and many other carpentry projects.
