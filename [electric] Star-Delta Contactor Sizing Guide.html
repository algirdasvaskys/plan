Star-Delta Contactor Sizing Guide

Introduction

Star-delta starting is a method used to reduce the starting current of three-phase induction motors. This guide will help you determine the correct contactor sizes for star-delta motor starting.

Step 1: Determine Motor Specifications

Before sizing contactors, you need to know the following motor specifications:

  • Rated power (kW or HP)
  • Rated voltage (V)
  • Rated current (A)
  • Power factor (cos φ)

Step 2: Calculate Star and Delta Currents

Delta Current (IΔ): This is the rated current of the motor.

Star Current (IY): IY = IΔ / √3 ≈ 0.58 × IΔ

Step 3: Determine Contactor Ratings

For star-delta starting, you need three contactors:

  1. Main Contactor (K1): Rated for full delta current (IΔ)
  2. Star Contactor (K2): Rated for star current (IY)
  3. Delta Contactor (K3): Rated for delta current (IΔ)

Step 4: Select Contactor Sizes

Choose contactors with ratings equal to or greater than the calculated currents. Use the manufacturer's sizing charts to select the appropriate contactor models.

Contactor Sizing Example

Let's consider a motor with the following specifications:

  • Power: 30 kW
  • Voltage: 400V
  • Rated current: 58A
Contactor Current Calculation Minimum Rating
Main (K1) IΔ = 58A 58A
Star (K2) IY = 58A / √3 ≈ 33.5A 33.5A
Delta (K3) IΔ = 58A 58A

Additional Considerations

  • Always round up to the next available contactor size.
  • Consider the switching frequency and duty cycle when selecting contactors.
  • Ensure the contactors are compatible with the control voltage of your system.
  • Consult local electrical codes and standards for specific requirements.

Conclusion

Proper sizing of star-delta contactors is crucial for the safe and efficient operation of three-phase induction motors. Always consult with a qualified electrician or engineer when designing and implementing motor control systems.
