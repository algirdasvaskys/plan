Marking and Measuring Valley Jack Rafters Using the Diminish Method

Introduction

The diminish method is an efficient technique for marking and measuring valley jack rafters in roof construction. This method allows carpenters to accurately determine the length and cut angles of jack rafters without complex calculations.

Materials Needed

  • Framing square
  • Tape measure
  • Pencil
  • Straight edge or chalk line
  • Calculator (optional)

Steps for the Diminish Method

1. Determine the Run and Rise

Measure the run (horizontal distance) and rise (vertical height) of the common rafter. This will be used to set up your framing square.

2. Set Up the Framing Square

Place the framing square on the rafter stock with the tongue (shorter arm) representing the rise and the body (longer arm) representing the run.

3. Mark the Plumb Cut

Make a mark along the tongue of the square. This represents the plumb cut at the ridge.

4. Measure and Mark the Length

Measure from the plumb cut mark along the top edge of the rafter stock to the required length of the jack rafter.

5. Apply the Diminish

The diminish is the amount each jack rafter is shorter than the one before it. To find the diminish:

  1. Measure the length of the common rafter
  2. Divide this length by the number of spaces between jack rafters
  3. The result is your diminish value

6. Mark Subsequent Jack Rafters

For each subsequent jack rafter, subtract one diminish value from the previous rafter's length.

7. Cut the Rafters

Cut along the plumb cut marks for the ridge end and the birdsmouth cut at the wall plate end.

Tips and Considerations

  • Always double-check your measurements before cutting
  • Account for the thickness of the ridge board in your calculations
  • Use a sharp pencil for accurate markings
  • Consider using a construction calculator for complex roof designs

Conclusion

The diminish method simplifies the process of marking and measuring valley jack rafters. With practice, this technique can significantly improve the efficiency and accuracy of roof framing.
