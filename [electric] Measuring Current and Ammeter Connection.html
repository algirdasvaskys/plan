How to Measure Current (Amperes) & Why Ammeters are Connected in Series

What is Electric Current?

Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. It is measured in amperes (A), which represent the amount of charge flowing past a point in a circuit per second.

How to Measure Current

  1. Use an ammeter or multimeter set to measure current.
  2. Ensure the meter's range is appropriate for the expected current.
  3. Turn off the circuit before connecting the ammeter.
  4. Connect the ammeter in series with the component you want to measure the current through.
  5. Turn on the circuit and read the current measurement on the meter.

Safety Warning

Always exercise caution when working with electrical circuits. Ensure the circuit is de-energized before making connections, and never exceed the meter's rated capacity.

Why Ammeters are Connected in Series

Ammeters are connected in series for several important reasons:

  1. To measure all the current: By being in series, all the current flowing through the circuit must pass through the ammeter, ensuring an accurate measurement.
  2. Low resistance: Ammeters are designed to have very low internal resistance to minimize their impact on the circuit.
  3. Voltage drop: The series connection results in a minimal voltage drop across the ammeter, maintaining circuit integrity.
  4. Circuit continuity: A series connection maintains the continuity of the circuit, allowing current to flow as normal.

Parallel Connection Issues

If an ammeter were connected in parallel:

  • It would create a low-resistance path, potentially damaging the meter and the circuit.
  • The current reading would be inaccurate as only a fraction of the total current would pass through the meter.

Conclusion

Measuring current correctly and safely is crucial in electrical work. Always remember to connect ammeters in series and take necessary safety precautions when working with electrical circuits.
