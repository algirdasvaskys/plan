Normally Closed Contact Push Button Switch: Working Principle

A Normally Closed (NC) Contact Push Button Switch is a type of electrical switch that maintains a closed circuit in its normal state and opens the circuit when activated.

Key Components

  • Button or actuator
  • Spring mechanism
  • Fixed contact
  • Movable contact

Working Principle

The working principle of an NC push button switch can be explained in two states:

1. Normal State (Closed Circuit)

In its normal state, when the button is not pressed:

  • The movable contact is in contact with the fixed contact
  • The circuit is closed, allowing current to flow
  • The spring mechanism keeps the contacts together

2. Activated State (Open Circuit)

When the button is pressed:

  • The actuator pushes against the spring mechanism
  • The movable contact is separated from the fixed contact
  • The circuit is opened, interrupting the current flow

Applications

NC push button switches are commonly used in:

  • Safety systems
  • Emergency stop buttons
  • Door switches in refrigerators or microwaves
  • Limit switches in industrial machinery

Advantages

  • Fail-safe operation: If the switch fails, it typically remains in the closed position
  • Quick response time
  • Simple and reliable design
  • Suitable for high-current applications

Conclusion

Normally Closed Contact Push Button Switches provide a simple yet effective way to control electrical circuits. Their default closed state and ability to open circuits when activated make them ideal for applications where safety and reliability are paramount.
