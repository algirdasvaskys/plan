Plasterboarding a Garden Room: Tools, Materials, and Tips

Tools Required

  • Tape measure
  • Utility knife
  • T-square
  • Pencil
  • Drywall saw
  • Electric screwdriver or drill
  • Drywall screws
  • Putty knife
  • Sanding block
  • Dust mask
  • Safety goggles
  • Step ladder

Materials Needed

  • Plasterboard sheets (also known as drywall or gypsum board)
  • Drywall joint compound
  • Drywall tape (paper or mesh)
  • Drywall primer
  • Insulation (if required)

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Prepare the Room

    Ensure the garden room's frame is complete and level. Install any required insulation between studs.

  2. Measure and Cut Plasterboard

    Measure the wall height and width. Cut plasterboard sheets to fit using a utility knife and T-square.

    Tip: Cut plasterboard on the paper side, then snap it back to break cleanly.

  3. Install Plasterboard

    Start at a corner and work your way across. Secure sheets to studs using drywall screws every 8 inches along edges and 12 inches in the field.

    Tip: Slightly countersink screws without breaking the paper surface.

  4. Cut Openings

    Mark and cut openings for windows, doors, and electrical boxes using a drywall saw.

  5. Apply Joint Tape

    Apply a thin layer of joint compound over seams. Press drywall tape into the wet compound and smooth out.

  6. Apply Joint Compound

    Once the tape is dry, apply three coats of joint compound, allowing each to dry and sanding between coats.

    Tip: Use progressively wider knives for each coat to feather the edges.

  7. Sand the Walls

    Once all coats are dry, sand the entire surface to achieve a smooth finish.

  8. Prime the Walls

    Apply a coat of drywall primer to prepare the surface for painting.

Additional Tips

  • Always wear a dust mask and safety goggles when cutting or sanding plasterboard.
  • Use moisture-resistant plasterboard in areas prone to dampness.
  • For ceiling installation, use a drywall lift or get help to hold boards in place.
  • Stagger the joints between plasterboard sheets to increase wall strength.
  • Use a damp cloth to wipe down walls after sanding to remove all dust before priming.
