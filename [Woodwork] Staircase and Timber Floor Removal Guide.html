The Big Build 13: Staircase Removal

Taking out a staircase and associated timber floor construction

Warning: This process involves structural changes to your building. Always consult with a structural engineer and obtain necessary permits before proceeding. Ensure proper safety measures are in place throughout the removal process.

Method:

  1. Preparation:
    • Turn off all utilities (electricity, gas, water) that may be affected.
    • Clear the area of furniture and valuables.
    • Set up proper lighting and ventilation.
    • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE): hard hat, safety glasses, dust mask, work gloves, and steel-toed boots.
  2. Remove handrails and balusters:
    • Start by removing any decorative elements, handrails, and balusters.
    • Use a pry bar or screwdriver to remove fasteners.
    • Be careful not to damage surrounding walls or flooring.
  3. Remove treads and risers:
    • Start from the top of the staircase.
    • Use a crowbar to pry up treads and risers.
    • Remove any nails or screws securing them to the stringers.
  4. Remove stringers:
    • Cut through the stringers using a reciprocating saw.
    • Make cuts near the top and bottom where they connect to the floor and landing.
    • Remove stringers carefully to avoid damaging surrounding structures.
  5. Remove associated timber floor:
    • Identify the area of timber flooring to be removed.
    • Use a circular saw to cut the flooring into manageable sections.
    • Set the saw depth to slightly less than the thickness of the flooring to avoid damaging the subfloor.
    • Pry up the cut sections using a pry bar or floor scraper.
  6. Remove floor joists:
    • Identify load-bearing walls and consult with a structural engineer before removing joists.
    • Use a reciprocating saw to cut through joist nails or hangers.
    • Remove joists one at a time, working from one end to the other.
  7. Clean up and inspect:
    • Remove all debris and vacuum the area thoroughly.
    • Inspect surrounding structures for any damage that may have occurred during removal.
    • Check for any exposed wiring, plumbing, or other utilities that may need attention.
  8. Structural reinforcement:
    • Consult with a structural engineer to determine if additional support is needed.
    • Install any necessary beams, posts, or other structural elements as advised.
  9. Finish work:
    • Repair any damage to surrounding walls, ceilings, or floors.
    • Patch and paint affected areas to match existing finishes.
    • Install new flooring or prepare the area for its intended new use.
Remember: Staircase and floor removal can significantly impact the structural integrity of your building. Always work with professionals and obtain necessary approvals before undertaking such a project.
