Difference Between MCB, RCCB & RCBO Circuit Breakers

1. MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker)

An MCB is designed to protect electrical circuits from overloads and short circuits.

  • Primary function: Overcurrent protection
  • Does not provide earth leakage protection
  • Automatically trips when current exceeds the rated value
  • Available in various current ratings and breaking capacities

2. RCCB (Residual Current Circuit Breaker)

An RCCB is designed to protect against earth leakage currents and electric shock hazards.

  • Primary function: Earth leakage protection
  • Does not provide overcurrent protection
  • Trips when it detects an imbalance between live and neutral currents
  • Typically has a high sensitivity (30mA) for personal protection

3. RCBO (Residual Current Breaker with Overload)

An RCBO combines the functions of both MCB and RCCB in a single device.

  • Provides both overcurrent and earth leakage protection
  • Trips on overload, short circuit, and earth leakage
  • Space-saving solution for comprehensive circuit protection
  • Available in various current ratings and sensitivities

Conclusion

When choosing between MCB, RCCB, and RCBO, consider the following:

  • Use MCB for basic overcurrent protection in circuits without earth leakage concerns.
  • Use RCCB in addition to MCB for circuits requiring earth leakage protection, especially in wet areas or where shock hazards are a concern.
  • Use RCBO for comprehensive protection in a single device, ideal for new installations or when upgrading existing systems.

Always consult with a qualified electrician or follow local electrical codes when selecting and installing circuit protection devices.
