Distribution Board in Electrical Wiring

Part 1: Introduction and Basics

Safety Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always consult a licensed electrician for installation or modifications to your electrical system.

What is a Distribution Board?

A distribution board, also known as a panel board, breaker panel, or electrical panel, is a crucial component of a building's electrical system. It divides the main power feed into subsidiary circuits while providing a protective fuse or circuit breaker for each circuit in a common enclosure.

Functions of a Distribution Board

  • Distributes electrical power to various circuits in the building
  • Provides overcurrent protection through circuit breakers or fuses
  • Allows for easy isolation of individual circuits for maintenance or emergencies
  • Houses ground fault circuit interrupters (GFCIs) and arc fault circuit interrupters (AFCIs) for additional safety

Components of a Distribution Board

  1. Main Switch: Controls the power supply to the entire distribution board
  2. Circuit Breakers: Protect individual circuits from overload and short circuits
  3. Bus Bars: Conduct electricity within the distribution board
  4. Neutral Bar: Connects all neutral wires
  5. Earth Bar: Connects all earthing wires
  6. Indicators: Show the status of the power supply

Types of Distribution Boards

  • Main Distribution Board (MDB): The primary board that receives power from the utility company
  • Sub Distribution Board (SDB): Secondary boards that distribute power to specific areas or floors
  • Single Phase Board: Used in most residential applications
  • Three Phase Board: Used in larger residential, commercial, and industrial applications

Importance in House Wiring

In residential applications, the distribution board plays a vital role in:

  • Organizing and managing the electrical system
  • Providing safety through circuit protection
  • Allowing for future expansion of the electrical system
  • Facilitating quick identification and resolution of electrical issues
