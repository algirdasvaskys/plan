How to Add a Socket to a Wall - Easy Step By Step Guide

Warning: Working with electricity can be dangerous. If you're not confident in your abilities or knowledge, it's always best to hire a qualified electrician.

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Electrical socket
  • Pattress box
  • Cable (2.5mmÂ² twin and earth)
  • Circuit breaker
  • Screwdriver
  • Wire strippers
  • Voltage tester
  • Drill with appropriate bits
  • Hammer and chisel
  • Fish tape or wire coat hanger

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Turn off the power: Locate your fuse box and turn off the power to the circuit you'll be working on. Use a voltage tester to ensure the power is off.
  2. Choose the location: Decide where you want to place the new socket. Mark the outline of the pattress box on the wall.
  3. Cut the hole: Use a drill to make holes at the corners of your outline. Then, use a hammer and chisel to carefully remove the plaster and create a hole for the pattress box.
  4. Run the cable: Fish the cable from the nearest existing socket or junction box to your new socket location. You may need to lift floorboards or drill holes to route the cable.
  5. Install the pattress box: Push the pattress box into the hole you've created. Secure it in place according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  6. Connect the wires: Strip the ends of the wires and connect them to the new socket:
    • Brown wire (Live) to the terminal marked 'L'
    • Blue wire (Neutral) to the terminal marked 'N'
    • Green/Yellow wire (Earth) to the terminal marked with the earth symbol
  7. Attach the socket: Screw the socket onto the pattress box.
  8. Test the circuit: Turn the power back on and use a voltage tester to check if the new socket is working correctly.
  9. Tidy up: Fill any holes or gaps with appropriate filler and paint over if necessary.

Safety Tips:

  • Always double-check that the power is off before starting work.
  • Use proper insulated tools to avoid electric shock.
  • Follow local building codes and regulations.
  • If you encounter any unexpected issues or are unsure about any step, stop and consult a professional electrician.

Remember, if you're not confident in your ability to safely complete this task, it's always best to hire a qualified electrician. Electrical work can be dangerous if not done correctly, and improper installation can lead to fire hazards or electric shocks.
