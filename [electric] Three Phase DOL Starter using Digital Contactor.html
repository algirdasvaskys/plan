Three Phase DOL Starter using Digital Contactor

Components

  • Digital Contactor
  • Overload Relay
  • Circuit Breaker or Fuses
  • Start and Stop Push Buttons
  • Three Phase Induction Motor
  • Control Transformer (if required)

Wiring Method

  1. Connect the three-phase power supply to the input terminals of the circuit breaker or fuses.
  2. Connect the output of the circuit breaker to the input terminals of the digital contactor.
  3. Connect the output terminals of the digital contactor to the input of the overload relay.
  4. Connect the output of the overload relay to the three-phase induction motor terminals.
  5. For the control circuit:
    • Connect the start button (normally open) and stop button (normally closed) in series.
    • Connect this series combination to the digital contactor's control input.
    • If required, use a control transformer to step down the voltage for the control circuit.
  6. Connect the overload relay's auxiliary contact to the digital contactor's control circuit for motor protection.

Operation

  1. When the start button is pressed, it energizes the digital contactor's control circuit.
  2. The digital contactor closes its main contacts, connecting the three-phase power to the motor.
  3. The motor starts rotating at full speed (Direct On Line).
  4. The stop button can be pressed at any time to de-energize the contactor and stop the motor.
  5. If an overload occurs, the overload relay trips, opening its auxiliary contact and stopping the motor.
  6. The digital contactor may provide additional features such as:
    • Soft start/stop functionality
    • Overcurrent protection
    • Phase loss protection
    • Remote monitoring and control capabilities

Safety Considerations

  • Ensure all wiring is done by a qualified electrician.
  • Use appropriate wire sizes for the motor current.
  • Properly ground all components and the motor frame.
  • Set the overload relay to the correct motor full load current.
  • Test the emergency stop functionality before regular operation.
  • Follow all local electrical codes and regulations.

Advantages of Digital Contactor

  • Enhanced motor protection features
  • Potential for energy savings through optimized starting
  • Reduced mechanical wear on the contactor
  • Possibility of remote monitoring and control
  • Improved diagnostics and troubleshooting capabilities
