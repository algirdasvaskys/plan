Phase Failure Relay Connection with DOL Motor Starter

Introduction

A Phase Failure Relay (PFR) is an essential component in a Direct-On-Line (DOL) motor starter circuit. It protects three-phase motors from damage caused by phase loss, phase reversal, and voltage imbalance.

Components

  • Three-phase power supply
  • Phase Failure Relay (PFR)
  • DOL starter (contactor and overload relay)
  • Three-phase motor
  • Control circuit components (fuses, switches, indicators)

Connection Diagram

Connection Procedure

  1. Connect the three-phase power supply (L1, L2, L3) to the input terminals of the Phase Failure Relay (PFR).
  2. Connect the output terminals of the PFR to the input of the DOL starter (contactor).
  3. Connect the output terminals of the DOL starter to the three-phase motor.
  4. In the control circuit:
    • Connect the normally closed (NC) contact of the PFR in series with the start button and contactor coil.
    • Connect the overload relay (OLR) contact in series with the PFR contact.
    • Add necessary control components like stop button, indicator lamps, and auxiliary contacts as required.

Operation

When all three phases are present and balanced, the PFR's NC contact remains closed, allowing the motor to start when the start button is pressed. If a phase failure, reversal, or significant imbalance occurs, the PFR opens its NC contact, preventing motor operation or stopping the running motor.

Benefits

  • Protects the motor from damage due to phase issues
  • Prevents motor from running on two phases (single phasing)
  • Ensures proper motor rotation by detecting phase reversal
  • Improves overall system reliability and motor lifespan

Conclusion

Incorporating a Phase Failure Relay in a DOL motor starter circuit is crucial for protecting three-phase motors from electrical faults. Proper connection and integration of the PFR with the starter and control circuit ensure safe and reliable motor operation.
