Transistor as an Oscillator

Introduction

A transistor oscillator is an electronic circuit that uses a transistor as an active element to generate a periodic signal. Oscillators are crucial components in many electronic devices, including radios, computers, and clocks.

How It Works

The transistor oscillator works on the principle of positive feedback. Here's a step-by-step explanation:

  1. The transistor amplifies a small signal.
  2. A portion of the output is fed back to the input in phase with the input signal.
  3. This positive feedback reinforces the original signal, causing oscillations.
  4. The frequency of oscillation is determined by the resonant circuit (usually an LC tank circuit).

Types of Transistor Oscillators

  • Colpitts Oscillator
  • Hartley Oscillator
  • Armstrong Oscillator
  • Crystal Oscillator

Applications

Transistor oscillators are used in various applications, including:

  • Radio and television transmitters
  • Function generators
  • Clock generators for digital circuits
  • Frequency synthesizers
