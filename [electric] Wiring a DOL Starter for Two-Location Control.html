How to Wire a DOL Starter to Turn ON & OFF from Two Different Places

Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always ensure that the power is turned off before working on any electrical connections. If you're not confident in your abilities, please consult a qualified electrician.

Introduction

A Direct-On-Line (DOL) starter is a common method of starting a three-phase induction motor. This guide will show you how to wire a DOL starter so that it can be controlled from two different locations.

Components Needed

  • DOL Starter
  • 3-Phase Induction Motor
  • Two Double Pole Double Throw (DPDT) switches
  • Power supply
  • Wires

Wiring Steps

Step 1: Connect the power supply to the DOL starter's input terminals.
Step 2: Connect the DOL starter's output terminals to the motor.
Step 3: Wire the control circuit:
  • Connect one pole of each DPDT switch in series.
  • Connect the other pole of each switch in parallel.
  • Connect one end of this circuit to the DOL starter's control input.
  • Connect the other end to the control power supply (usually 24V DC or 230V AC).
Step 4: Connect the control power supply neutral to the DOL starter's neutral terminal.
Step 5: Ground all components properly.

Operation

With this wiring setup, you can turn the motor ON or OFF from either location. The switches at both locations will be able to control the motor independently.

Safety Considerations

  • Always use properly rated components for your specific application.
  • Ensure all connections are secure and properly insulated.
  • Install appropriate circuit protection devices (fuses or circuit breakers).
  • Follow all local electrical codes and regulations.
  • If you're not experienced with electrical work, consult a professional electrician.

Maintenance

Regularly inspect all connections and components for signs of wear or damage. Clean the starter and switches periodically to prevent dust accumulation. Replace any damaged parts immediately.

Troubleshooting

If the system doesn't work as expected:

  • Check all connections for loose wires.
  • Verify that both switches are functioning correctly.
  • Ensure the DOL starter is receiving power and functioning properly.
  • Test the motor independently to ensure it's working.

Remember, safety should always be your top priority when working with electrical systems. If you encounter any issues you're not comfortable addressing, please consult a qualified electrician.
