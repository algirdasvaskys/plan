Working Relationships

Ways to Build Good Working Relationships

  • Recognize differences in personality and temperament
  • Treat colleagues politely and with respect
  • Cooperate and assist willingly with requests
  • Communicate about problems, changes, or proposals
  • Be honest about mistakes and report them promptly
  • Address relationship breakdowns honestly and fairly

Factors That Can Upset Good Working Relationships

  • Allowing personal differences to affect work relationships
  • Colleagues not pulling their weight (laziness, incompetence, absenteeism)
  • Ignoring company rules, regulations, or safe working practices
  • Poor personal appearance and hygiene
  • Favoritism or unfair treatment by managers and supervisors

Remember, the company image depends partly on how workers are judged by customers. Your work, behavior, and interactions with colleagues all contribute to this image.

Reflect on Building Good Relationships

Consider the following scenarios and think about how you would handle them:

Do:

  • Offer help to a struggling colleague
  • Communicate openly about workplace issues
  • Respect different opinions and personalities

Don't:

  • Gossip about coworkers
  • Ignore safety regulations
  • Let personal differences affect work

How would you apply these principles in your workplace? Write down specific actions you can take to build better working relationships.
