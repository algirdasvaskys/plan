Using Tower Lights with Industrial Machines

Ever wonder about those colorful lights on industrial machines? Let's explore tower lights and how they work!

What Are Tower Lights?

Tower lights are visual indicators that show a machine's status. They typically have three colors:

  • Red: Machine is running
  • Green: Machine is stopped
  • Orange: Machine has tripped

Setting Up Your Tower Light

What You'll Need:

  • Tower light (5-wire model)
  • 24V DC power supply (SMPS)
  • MCB (circuit breaker)
  • Contactor
  • Overload relay
  • Wires and terminal blocks

Wiring Steps:

  1. Connect AC power to the SMPS through the MCB
  2. Wire the SMPS output to the tower light's power input
  3. Connect the orange light to the overload relay's NO contact
  4. Wire the red light to the contactor's NO contact
  5. Connect the green light to the contactor's NC contact
  6. Wire the buzzer in parallel with the orange light

How It Works

  • Green light on: Machine is off
  • Red light on: Machine is running
  • Orange light + buzzer: Machine has tripped

Testing Your Setup

  1. Turn on the MCB: Green light should come on
  2. Start the machine: Red light should activate
  3. Stop the machine: Green light returns
  4. Trip the machine: Orange light and buzzer activate

Tower lights make it easy to monitor your machines from a distance. They improve safety and help quickly identify issues.

Remember to always follow proper safety procedures when working with electrical equipment!
