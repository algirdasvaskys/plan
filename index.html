Forward Tipping Dumper Q&A

1. What is the definition of, or how can a hazard be described?
Danger to people, property and environment
2. What is the purpose of a risk assessment?
To control the hazard
3. List SIX typical subject areas that should be covered in a site induction.
Fire, first aid, welfare, method statement, access/egress, smoking area
4. What THREE duties of The Health and Safety at Work Act must employees follow?
Take care of yourself and others who may be affected by your actions, co-operate with employer, do not interfere with health & safety
5. What does The Health and Safety at Work Act require employers to do with regards specifically to plant?
Supply safe and maintained plant
6. a) What is the purpose of a Method Statement and b) What is required of the operator?
a) Document which gives specific instruction on how to carry out job/task safely
b) Comply with it
7. Name FOUR different types or levels of sanctions that can be applied (by employers and judicial bodies) to plant operators who do not comply with, or follow legislation and regulations?
Dismissal, verbal warning, written warning, prosecution.
8. Why are plant operators generally regarded as ‘safely-critical’ workers?
Their actions (with the machine) could have significant H&S consequences on themselves and others.
9. Name THREE ways in which an operator can minimise their impact upon the environment whilst using the machine.
Keep engine revs low, stop oil and fuel spill, work
10. In what situation does a hard hat NOT need to be worn when operating a forward tipping dumper?
In full cab with FOPS
11. The operator has to use a new model of dumper i.e. swivel skip that they are unfamiliar with. What do Regulations (i.e. PUWER 98) and other guidance require the operator to have?
Information and training
12. What are the possible outcomes if being prosecuted for not complying with legislation and regulations?
Case dismissal, fine, prison.
13. The function or job role of a dumper driver, when transporting materials, is to?
Transport them efficiently and safely
14. How can a qualification or card benefit a dumper operator?
Shows skill achieved
15. Name THREE ways that a plant operator can contribute in ensuring repeat business with the client or main contractor.
Work safely, co-operate with others, work to lift plan/method statement, be on time
16. Where should the dumper’s Operator’s Manual be kept and why?
On the dumper for easy access
17. When would the skip safety strut or support be used?
When working under the skip
18. If the operator has to top-up the hydraulic oil, state TWO precautions to ensure cleanliness of the system.
Clean around the filler and use clean funnels
19. What is a possible consequence of using a tyre with a deep cut in the sidewall?
Bursting if overloaded
20. What is the purpose of a roll or ROPS frame?
To protect operator when machine rolls over

22. Using the Operator’s Manual, state the cold-starting procedure for the machine.
Note: The Operator’s Manual for the machine being used for the test MUST be available for reference by the candidate.
23. On articulated dumpers, steering is achieved by actuating a hydraulic ram which is controlled by turning the steering wheel. a) How does the engine supply the oil flow and b) What happens to the steering when the engine stops?
a) Hydraulic pump
b) Steering gets harder
24. If checking the oil level using a dipstick, why must gloves be worn?
Stop diseases and contamination of controls
25. Apart from the operator, who else may need to use the machine’s Operator’s Manual?
Supervisor, fitter
26. A dumper is classified by a manufacturer as a 6 tonne dumper. What does this mean?
6 tonne capacity
27. Most site dumpers have the engine at the rear. State the main reason for this.
Counter balance the skip
28. During work, the engine starts to overheat. Explain the danger if someone tries to remove the radiator or expansion tank cap.
Pressurised steam
29. Give FOUR reasons that may cause the dumper to tip over sideways during travel.
Turning on a slope, turning when driving too fast, soft ground, holes
30. a) What is the minimum distance allowed near open trenches when travelling with a dumper and b) Explain why?
Depth of trench away from side to stop collapse
31. Why must a seat belt be worn at all times?
Keep operator in place when machine turns over
32. If the machine is being travelled or working on the public highway, including adjacent pavements and verges, the Road Traffic Act applies. a) What type of licence and which class should the operator hold and b) what is the minimum age allowed?
a) UK class B, up to 7.5T, over 7.5
b) 21
33. When travelling on wet clay, what effect does this have on the dumper?
Traction becomes slippery, steering is reduced
34. Manufacturers must give recommendations on how to travel a dumper up and down inclines. If this information is not known first hand, what is the general rule about travelling up and down slopes?
Forward loaded, reverse empty
35. How is it possible that a dumper can still tip over when travelling on gentle gradient, even if it is not overloaded and not being driven at excessive speed?
Steering across the slope
36. a) Name THREE purposes of the raised lugs on tyres and b) what can happen to a dumper if the lugs are severely worn?
a) Grip and tractions
b) No grip and steering is difficult
37. If setting up work in a pedestrianised area, state THREE factors that need to be taken into account.
Noise, fumes and barriers
38. Whenever possible, who should decide the positioning of the dumper when being loaded?
Excavator driver
39. The operator is asked to tip material into a new trench. State FIVE different requirements that must be considered or implemented before tipping commences.
Barriers or stop blocks, material being discharged, no persons in trench, shoring, permit to tip
40. Give TWO reasons for using a swivel skip dumper over a conventional forward tipping skip.
Continuous pout, restricted access
41. What problems and hazards can soft ground cause to a loaded dumper?
Get stuck, tip over
42. Give TWO reasons why the dumper operator should have an understanding of the type of spoil being transported.
If it is liquid, movement will cause spills; clay may overload tyres
43. When working in a confined area or space, what danger can be present with an articulated dumper with regards to steering?
If too close to wall, cannot steer away
44. Discharging loads whilst moving is not considered best practice. Why would this be?
Instability could occur on uneven ground
45. When working in a confined area or space, name THREE hazards that can occur.
Visibility, fumes, noise
46. a) What determines the minimum distances that any part of plant and machinery has to be kept from overhead electricity lines and b) explain why a distance should be kept?
a) The voltage of the electricity line and type of support (e.g., metal pylons / wooden poles etc.)
b) High voltage electricity can arc across large gaps at certain distances.
47. Why do high tip dumpers need particular care when tipping?
Centre of gravity changes
48. Who should determine the maximum load that should be placed into the skip of the dumper?
The dumper driver
49. State TWO requirements of using a stop block or earth bank (berm) at a trench discharging point.
Stop trench collapse, direct pout into hole
50. When forward tipping a load, the centre of gravity of the machine changes. a) What effect does this have on the dumper and b) state TWO actions to avoid accidents?
a) Cause movement backwards
b) Hand brake on, gear in neutral
51. What may happen if the load is tipped onto a downward slope?
Material could roll down the slope.
52. Operators should lower the skip fully before driving away, particularly on swivel skips. Give TWO possible consequences if this is not carried out.
Centre of gravity, vision is reduced by the skip
53. If considering to use the dumper to transport a wide load. What needs to be taken into account?
Load secure and stable, travel route checked prior to travel, banks man
54. Name THREE problems that could occur if the skip is overloaded with a dense/compacted material.
Tyres/axle overloaded, skip unable to tip, machine tips forward when discharging

56. Larger dumpers use a turbo-charged engine. a) What is the normal procedure before switching off the engine after working and b) what happens if the procedure is not followed?
a) Let it idle
b) Could damage the turbo
57. Before leaving the dumper for a rest break, after parking and switching off the machine, what final action must be carried out and b) why?
a) Isolate by removing the key and/or door locked
b) To prevent unauthorised access
58. When parking the machine at the end of the shift, name THREE places where the dumper should NOT be parked.
Access and egress, site road, on a slope
59. Why should a dumper be re-fuelled at the end of the day?
Prevent condensation
60. The operator has been asked to drive the dumper onto a transporter/trailer. a) Who is responsible for the loading operations and b) state FOUR actions to be considered by the operator before loading commences.
a) Lorry driver
b) Ground condition, ramp condition, overhead hazard, direction of loading
61. How can a qualification or card benefit a plant operator?
Credibility, proof of skills, better employment or promotional prospects.
62. What allows the wheels to follow the contours of the ground when travelling on uneven ground?
The pivoting of the chassis.
