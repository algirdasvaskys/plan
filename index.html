8.8 A Brief Overview of Procurement Systems

Purpose of a Tender Process

The procurement process is more than just making an acceptable award. It's a crucial step in:

  • Finding a capable contractor
  • Ensuring a successful project delivery
  • Setting the stage for the operational phase

Key Insight: The focus should extend beyond procurement and construction to the project's entire lifecycle, including its operational stage.

8.8.1 To Procure or to Tender?

Procuring (Owner)
→
Tendering (Bidders)

Procuring: The owner's activity to organize a process for soliciting project proposals.

Tendering: The bidders' activity in responding to requests for qualification (RFQ) or requests for proposals (RfP).

8.8.2 Relation between Delivery Model and Procurement Process

Design-Bid-Build Models

  • Procured based on detailed design
  • Owner bears full responsibility for site data
  • Uses standard construction contract forms
  • Primary award criterion: lowest price
  • One-tier process often sufficient

Design and Build and Integrated Models

  • Two-tier process more efficient
  • First tier: Prequalification
  • Second tier: Detailed proposal evaluation

Procurement Complexity: The more complex the project-delivery model, the more complex the tender process.

Award Criteria

Potential criteria for award include:

  • Price (when applicable)
  • Quality of team
  • Quality of partnering proposals
  • Quality of management processes
  • Time for completion
  • Environmental commitments (CO2 emissions, renewable energy, etc.)
  • Commitment to capability measurement levels
  • Commitment to contract offered

Key Challenge for Owners

Devise a strategy that optimizes the chance of awarding the contract to a party who will commit to the project objectives in the most outstanding way.
