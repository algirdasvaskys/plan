Decoding Contactor and Auxiliary Contact Numbers

Ever wondered what those numbers on contactors mean? Let's crack the code!

The Last Two Digits Mystery

On contactors, you'll often see numbers like "10" or "11". Here's what they mean:

  • First digit: Number of NO (Normally Open) contacts
  • Second digit: Number of NC (Normally Closed) contacts

Example: "10" means 1 NO contact, 0 NC contacts

Auxiliary Contact Numbering

On auxiliary contacts, you'll see similar numbering:

  • "11" means 1 NO contact, 1 NC contact
  • "22" means 2 NO contacts, 2 NC contacts

Identifying NO/NC Without Labels

If the NO/NC labels are worn off, look at the terminal numbers:

  • NO contacts: Last digits are 3 and 4 (e.g., 13-14)
  • NC contacts: Last digits are 1 and 2 (e.g., 21-22)

The First Digit's Secret

The first digit in terminal numbers (e.g., 13-14) indicates the contact's position:

  • 1 = First contact
  • 2 = Second contact
  • 5 = Fifth contact

Quick Recap

  1. Last two digits on contactors show NO/NC contact count
  2. Terminal numbers reveal if it's NO (3-4) or NC (1-2)
  3. First digit in terminal numbers shows contact position

Understanding these numbers helps you quickly identify contactor types and configurations without guesswork!
