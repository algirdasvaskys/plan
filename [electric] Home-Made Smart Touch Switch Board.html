Home-Made Smart Touch Switch Board

Learn how to create your own smart touch switch board, as demonstrated by @TheElectricalGuy.

Safety Warning: Working with electricity can be dangerous. Always ensure the power is off before starting any electrical work. If you're not confident in your abilities, please consult a professional electrician.

Materials Needed:

  • Arduino Nano or similar microcontroller
  • TTP223 touch sensors (quantity depends on the number of switches you want)
  • 5V relay module (quantity matches the number of switches)
  • Jumper wires
  • Breadboard or PCB
  • 5V power supply
  • Electrical box or enclosure
  • Acrylic or glass panel for the touch surface

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Arduino:

    Upload the necessary code to the Arduino Nano. The code should read the touch sensor inputs and control the relay outputs accordingly.

  2. Connect the Touch Sensors:

    Wire the TTP223 touch sensors to the Arduino's digital input pins. Each sensor will require a power (VCC), ground (GND), and signal connection.

  3. Connect the Relay Module:

    Wire the relay module to the Arduino's digital output pins. Each relay will need a control signal, as well as power and ground connections.

  4. Power Supply:

    Connect the 5V power supply to power both the Arduino and the relay module. Ensure proper voltage regulation if needed.

  5. Prepare the Enclosure:

    Mount the Arduino, relay module, and other components inside the electrical box or enclosure.

  6. Create the Touch Panel:

    Cut the acrylic or glass panel to fit the enclosure. Mark the positions for the touch-sensitive areas.

  7. Assemble the Touch Panel:

    Attach the touch sensors to the back of the acrylic/glass panel at the marked positions. Ensure good contact between the sensors and the panel.

  8. Final Assembly:

    Carefully place the touch panel onto the enclosure, making sure all wires are properly connected and not pinched.

  9. Testing:

    Before connecting to mains power, test the system using a low-voltage power source to ensure all touch controls and relays are functioning correctly.

  10. Installation:

    Once tested, the smart touch switch board can be installed in place of a traditional switch. Ensure all mains wiring is done by a qualified electrician.

Additional Tips:

  • Consider adding LED indicators to show the status of each switch.
  • For advanced functionality, you could incorporate Wi-Fi capabilities using an ESP8266 or ESP32 module instead of an Arduino.
  • Always use appropriate gauge wires for the current load of your circuits.
  • Consider adding a manual override or failsafe mechanism in case of system malfunction.

Remember, this project involves working with both low-voltage electronics and potentially high-voltage mains power. Always prioritize safety and consult with a professional if you're unsure about any step in the process.
