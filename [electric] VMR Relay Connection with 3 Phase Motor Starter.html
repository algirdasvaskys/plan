VMR Relay Connection with 3 Phase Motor Starter

Introduction

A VMR (Voltage Monitoring Relay) is used in conjunction with a 3 Phase Motor Starter to protect three-phase motors from voltage-related issues. This document explains the connection method and working principle.

Components

  • VMR (Voltage Monitoring Relay)
  • 3 Phase Motor Starter (Contactor and Overload Relay)
  • 3 Phase Motor
  • Control Circuit Components

Connection Method

  1. Power Supply: Connect the three-phase power supply (L1, L2, L3) to the main terminals of the motor starter.
  2. VMR Connection: Connect the VMR input terminals to the three-phase supply (L1, L2, L3).
  3. Control Circuit: Wire the VMR output contact in series with the motor starter coil circuit.
  4. Motor Connection: Connect the motor starter output terminals to the three-phase motor (T1, T2, T3).

Working Principle

  1. The VMR continuously monitors the three-phase voltage.
  2. If the voltage falls outside the preset range, the VMR opens its output contact.
  3. This action breaks the control circuit, de-energizing the motor starter coil.
  4. The motor starter contactor opens, disconnecting the motor from the power supply.
  5. When voltage returns to normal, the VMR closes its contact, allowing the motor to be restarted.

Benefits

  • Protects the motor from under-voltage and over-voltage conditions
  • Prevents motor damage due to phase loss or phase reversal
  • Increases motor lifespan and reduces maintenance costs
  • Enhances overall system reliability

Conclusion

Properly connecting a VMR Relay with a 3 Phase Motor Starter provides crucial protection for three-phase motors against voltage-related issues. This setup ensures safe and efficient operation of the motor, reducing the risk of damage and improving system reliability.
