Slab and Beam Shuttering Leveling At Site

Proper leveling of slab and beam shuttering is crucial for ensuring the structural integrity and aesthetic quality of concrete structures. This document outlines the method for achieving accurate leveling at the construction site.

Equipment Required

  • Laser level or water level
  • Measuring tape
  • Spirit level
  • Wooden wedges
  • Hammer
  • Chalk or marker
  • Plumb bob

Method

  1. Establish a reference point: Choose a fixed point on the site as your reference for leveling.
  2. Set up the laser level or water level: Position the leveling equipment at a central location that can cover the entire shuttering area.
  3. Mark the desired height: Using the leveling equipment, mark the desired height for the slab or beam on the surrounding walls or columns.
  4. Install main supports: Set up the vertical supports (props or jacks) for the shuttering, ensuring they are plumb using a spirit level or plumb bob.
  5. Place horizontal bearers: Install the main horizontal bearers on top of the vertical supports.
  6. Add joists: Place joists perpendicular to the main bearers.
  7. Install shuttering panels: Place the shuttering panels (typically plywood) on top of the joists.
  8. Initial leveling: Use the laser level or water level to check the height of the shuttering panels against the marked reference points.
  9. Fine-tune leveling: Adjust the height of the supports using wooden wedges or the adjustable mechanisms on props/jacks.
  10. Cross-check: Use a spirit level to check the levelness in multiple directions across the shuttering surface.
  11. Mark and secure: Once level, mark the positions of supports and secure all connections.
  12. Final inspection: Perform a final check of the entire shuttering system for levelness and stability.

Important Considerations

  • Ensure the ground beneath the supports is stable and compacted.
  • Account for any intended slope or fall in the slab design.
  • Check local building codes for specific requirements on shuttering and formwork.
  • Consider the weight of wet concrete and any dynamic loads during pouring when designing the support system.

Safety Warning

Always prioritize safety when working with shuttering systems:

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE).
  • Ensure all workers are trained in proper shuttering techniques.
  • Regularly inspect the shuttering system for any signs of instability or damage.
  • Follow all local safety regulations and guidelines.

Conclusion

Proper leveling of slab and beam shuttering is essential for achieving high-quality concrete structures. By following this method and paying attention to detail, construction teams can ensure accurate and safe formwork installation.
