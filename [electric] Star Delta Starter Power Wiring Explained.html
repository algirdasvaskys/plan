Star Delta Starter Power Wiring Explained

The Star Delta starter is a method of starting a three-phase induction motor, which reduces the starting current and torque. This guide will explain the power wiring process step by step.

Components Required

  • 3-phase induction motor
  • Main contactor
  • Star contactor
  • Delta contactor
  • Overload relay
  • Timer relay
  • Fuses or circuit breaker
  • Start and stop push buttons

Wiring Procedure

Step 1: Main Power Supply Connection

Connect the three-phase power supply (L1, L2, L3) to the main contactor's incoming terminals.

Step 2: Main Contactor to Overload Relay

Connect the outgoing terminals of the main contactor to the incoming terminals of the overload relay.

Step 3: Overload Relay to Star and Delta Contactors

From the overload relay, connect:

  • L1 to U1 of the star contactor and U1 of the delta contactor
  • L2 to V1 of the star contactor and V1 of the delta contactor
  • L3 to W1 of the star contactor and W1 of the delta contactor

Step 4: Star Contactor to Motor Windings

Connect the star contactor to the motor windings:

  • U2 to U2 of the motor
  • V2 to V2 of the motor
  • W2 to W2 of the motor

Step 5: Delta Contactor to Motor Windings

Connect the delta contactor to the motor windings:

  • U2 to W1 of the motor
  • V2 to U1 of the motor
  • W2 to V1 of the motor

Step 6: Motor Winding Connections

Connect the motor windings:

  • U1 to U1 of the overload relay
  • V1 to V1 of the overload relay
  • W1 to W1 of the overload relay

Operation Sequence

  1. When the start button is pressed, the main contactor and star contactor are energized.
  2. The motor starts in star connection, reducing the starting current.
  3. After a preset time (usually 5-10 seconds), the timer relay activates.
  4. The star contactor is de-energized, and the delta contactor is energized.
  5. The motor now runs in delta connection at full speed and torque.

Important Notes:

  • Always ensure proper insulation and cable sizing for the current ratings.
  • Use appropriate protection devices like fuses or circuit breakers.
  • Follow local electrical codes and safety regulations.
  • The control wiring is not shown in this explanation, which focuses on power wiring.

This wiring method allows for a smooth start of large three-phase induction motors, reducing mechanical stress on the motor and electrical stress on the power supply system.
