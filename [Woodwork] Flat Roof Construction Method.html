Flat Roof Construction Method

Flat roofs are popular in modern architecture and commercial buildings. This guide outlines the step-by-step process for constructing a flat roof.

Warning: Roof construction can be dangerous. Always prioritize safety and consider hiring professionals for complex projects.

Materials Needed:

  • Roof decking (plywood or OSB)
  • Roofing felt
  • EPDM rubber membrane or built-up roofing materials
  • Insulation boards
  • Fasteners (nails, screws)
  • Adhesive
  • Flashing
  • Drip edge

Steps for Flat Roof Construction:

1. Prepare the Roof Structure

Ensure the roof joists are properly installed and can support the weight of the flat roof. The structure should have a slight slope (about 1/4 inch per foot) for drainage.

2. Install Roof Decking

Cover the joists with plywood or OSB sheets. Secure them with nails or screws, leaving a 1/8 inch gap between sheets for expansion.

3. Apply Roofing Felt

Cover the decking with roofing felt, overlapping each sheet by about 2 inches. This acts as a moisture barrier.

4. Install Drip Edge

Attach metal drip edge around the perimeter of the roof to guide water away from the fascia.

5. Add Insulation

Install rigid insulation boards over the roofing felt. This improves energy efficiency.

6. Apply the Roofing Membrane

For EPDM: Roll out the rubber membrane, allowing it to relax and expand. Adhere it to the insulation using bonding adhesive.
For built-up roofing: Apply alternating layers of bitumen and reinforcing fabric.

7. Secure the Edges

Use termination bars or batten strips to secure the edges of the membrane. Seal all seams and edges with appropriate sealant.

8. Install Flashing

Apply flashing around all roof penetrations (vents, chimneys) and at the junction of the roof and walls.

9. Create Drainage

Install scuppers or internal drains to ensure proper water drainage from the roof.

10. Final Inspection

Thoroughly inspect the roof for any gaps, loose areas, or potential leak points. Make necessary repairs.

Maintenance: Regularly inspect and maintain your flat roof to ensure its longevity and performance. Clean debris, check for damage, and reapply sealant as needed.

Remember, while this guide provides an overview of flat roof construction, the specific details may vary based on local building codes, climate, and the exact materials used. Always consult with local authorities and consider professional help for best results.
