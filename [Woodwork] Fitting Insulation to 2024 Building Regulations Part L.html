Fitting Insulation to 2023 Building Regulations Part L

1. Introduction

This guide outlines the method for fitting insulation in accordance with the 2023 Building Regulations Part L in England. These regulations aim to improve energy efficiency in buildings.

2. Key Changes in 2023 Regulations

  • Increased minimum U-values for various building elements
  • Enhanced focus on thermal bridging
  • Greater emphasis on airtightness

3. Insulation Requirements

3.1 U-values

New minimum U-values for building elements:

  • External walls: 0.18 W/m²K
  • Roofs: 0.11 W/m²K
  • Floors: 0.13 W/m²K
  • Windows and doors: 1.4 W/m²K

3.2 Insulation Materials

Choose insulation materials that meet or exceed these U-values, such as:

  • Mineral wool
  • Polyisocyanurate (PIR) boards
  • Expanded polystyrene (EPS)
  • Natural insulation (e.g., sheep's wool, cellulose)

4. Fitting Method

4.1 Preparation

  1. Assess the area to be insulated
  2. Calculate the required insulation thickness based on the material's thermal conductivity and target U-value
  3. Ensure the area is clean, dry, and free from debris
  4. Check for any electrical wiring or plumbing that needs consideration

4.2 Installation Process

  1. For cavity walls:
    • Use cavity wall insulation blown in by professionals
    • Ensure complete filling of the cavity to prevent cold spots
  2. For internal wall insulation:
    • Install a vapour barrier on the warm side of the insulation
    • Fit insulation boards or quilt insulation between studs
    • Cover with plasterboard and seal all joints
  3. For loft insulation:
    • Lay the first layer of insulation between joists
    • Add a second layer perpendicular to the first to reach required thickness
    • Ensure ventilation is maintained to prevent condensation
  4. For floor insulation:
    • For suspended timber floors, fit insulation between joists
    • For solid floors, lay insulation board over the slab before screed

5. Addressing Thermal Bridging

Pay special attention to junctions and corners to minimize thermal bridging:

  • Use thermal breaks at wall-floor junctions
  • Insulate window and door reveals
  • Ensure continuity of insulation at corners and junctions

6. Airtightness

Improve airtightness to meet the maximum air permeability of 8m³/h/m² at 50Pa:

  • Use airtight membranes and tapes
  • Seal all penetrations through the building fabric
  • Pay attention to junctions between different building elements

7. Ventilation

Ensure adequate ventilation to prevent condensation and maintain indoor air quality:

  • Install trickle vents in windows
  • Consider mechanical ventilation with heat recovery (MVHR) for highly airtight buildings

Note: Always consult with a qualified professional or building control body to ensure compliance with the latest regulations. Local authorities may have additional requirements.
