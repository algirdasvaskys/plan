Using AutoLevel During Excavation

Introduction

AutoLevel is a crucial tool in modern excavation projects, providing accurate measurements and ensuring precise grading. This guide will walk you through the process of using AutoLevel effectively during excavation work.

Equipment Needed

  • AutoLevel instrument
  • Tripod
  • Grade rod (leveling rod)
  • Field book and pencil
  • Calculator (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Set up the AutoLevel:
    • Choose a stable location with a clear view of the excavation area.
    • Set up the tripod and ensure it's level.
    • Mount the AutoLevel on the tripod and secure it.
  2. Level the instrument:
    • Use the leveling screws to center the bubble in the circular level.
    • Rotate the instrument 90 degrees and re-check the bubble.
    • Repeat until the bubble remains centered in all positions.
  3. Establish a benchmark:
    • Identify a stable point outside the excavation area as your benchmark.
    • Take a backsight reading on this point using the grade rod.
    • Record this reading in your field book.
  4. Take foresight readings:
    • Position the grade rod at key points within the excavation area.
    • Take readings at each point and record them in your field book.
    • Ensure the rod is held vertically and steady for accurate readings.
  5. Calculate elevations:
    • Subtract foresight readings from the height of instrument to determine elevations.
    • Compare these elevations to your project plans.
  6. Guide the excavation:
    • Communicate elevation information to the excavator operator.
    • Regularly check progress and take new readings as needed.
  7. Final grade check:
    • Once excavation is complete, take a final set of readings.
    • Verify that all points meet the required elevations per the project plans.

Tips for Accurate Readings

Tip 1: Always check the level of the instrument before taking readings.

Tip 2: Keep the grade rod vertical using a rod level or have an assistant check it with a spirit level.

Tip 3: Take multiple readings at each point and average them for increased accuracy.

Tip 4: Regularly check your benchmark to ensure the instrument hasn't shifted.

Conclusion

Using an AutoLevel during excavation ensures accurate grading and helps maintain project specifications. With practice, this process becomes an efficient way to guide excavation work and achieve precise results.
