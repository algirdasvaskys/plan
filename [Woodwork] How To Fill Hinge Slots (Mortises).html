How To Fill Hinge Slots (Mortises)

Filling hinge slots, also known as mortises, is a common task in woodworking and home improvement. Whether you're repairing old furniture or repurposing a door, this guide will walk you through the process step by step.

Materials Needed:

  • Wood filler or epoxy wood filler
  • Putty knife or spatula
  • Sandpaper (various grits)
  • Clean cloth
  • Wood stain or paint (optional)
  • Protective gear (gloves, dust mask)

Steps to Fill Hinge Slots:

Step 1: Clean the Hinge Slot

Remove any old screws, nails, or debris from the hinge slot. Use a chisel or screwdriver to scrape out any loose wood or old filler. Wipe the area clean with a damp cloth and allow it to dry completely.

Step 2: Prepare the Wood Filler

If using two-part epoxy wood filler, mix it according to the manufacturer's instructions. For regular wood filler, it's usually ready to use straight from the container.

Step 3: Apply the Filler

Use a putty knife or spatula to apply the wood filler to the hinge slot. Press it firmly into the slot, making sure to fill all corners and crevices. Overfill slightly, as the filler may shrink as it dries.

Step 4: Allow to Dry

Let the filler dry completely. This can take anywhere from 15 minutes to 24 hours, depending on the type of filler used and the depth of the slot. Refer to the product instructions for specific drying times.

Step 5: Sand the Filled Area

Once the filler is completely dry, sand the area smooth. Start with a coarse-grit sandpaper (e.g., 80-grit) and progressively move to finer grits (e.g., 120, 220). Sand until the filled area is flush with the surrounding wood.

Step 6: Clean Up

Wipe away any sanding dust with a clean, dry cloth. For a more thorough cleaning, use a slightly damp cloth followed by a dry one.

Step 7: Finish (Optional)

If desired, apply wood stain or paint to match the surrounding area. Follow the product instructions for best results.

Tip:

For deep mortises, you may need to apply the filler in layers, allowing each layer to dry before adding the next.

Warning:

Always work in a well-ventilated area and wear appropriate protective gear, especially when sanding or using chemical products.
