How to Build a Deck - Part 2: Joists and Decking

Measuring, Cutting, and Fixing the Joists

Step 1: Measure and Mark Joist Locations

Begin by measuring and marking where your joists will be placed on the ledger board and beam. Typically, joists are spaced 16 inches on center for residential decks.

Tip: Use a pencil or chalk line to mark the centers clearly.

Step 2: Cut the Joists

Measure the distance between the ledger board and the beam, then cut your joists to this length. Remember to account for any overhang if your design includes one.

Tip: Always measure twice and cut once to avoid wasting materials.

Step 3: Install Joist Hangers

Attach joist hangers to the ledger board at each marked location using joist hanger nails or screws. Ensure they're level and properly aligned.

Step 4: Place and Secure Joists

Place each joist into its corresponding hanger and secure it with nails or screws. At the beam end, toe-nail or use hurricane ties to secure the joists.

Tip: Check for level and plumb as you go to ensure a flat deck surface.

Step 5: Install Blocking

Add blocking between joists at the midpoint for additional support and to prevent twisting. Secure with nails or screws from both sides.

Choosing and Installing Decking

Decking Material Options

There are several popular decking materials to choose from:

  • Pressure-treated lumber: Affordable and widely available
  • Cedar or redwood: Naturally resistant to rot and insects
  • Composite decking: Low maintenance and long-lasting
  • PVC decking: Completely synthetic and highly durable
  • Tropical hardwoods: Exotic look and very dense

Installing the Decking

  1. Start at the house end of the deck, leaving a 1/8 inch gap between the first board and the house for drainage.
  2. Use deck screws or hidden fasteners to secure each board to the joists.
  3. Leave a 1/8 inch gap between boards for drainage and expansion.
  4. Use a spacer tool to maintain consistent gaps.
  5. Cut boards to length as needed, ensuring ends are supported by joists.
  6. For the last board, you may need to rip it lengthwise to fit.
Tip: Pre-drill holes near the ends of boards to prevent splitting.

Finishing Touches

Once all decking is installed:

  • Sand any rough spots or splinters.
  • Apply finish or sealant if using natural wood.
  • Install fascia boards to cover the end grain of decking and joists.
