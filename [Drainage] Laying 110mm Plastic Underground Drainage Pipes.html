Laying 110mm Plastic Underground Drainage Pipes

This guide provides a comprehensive method for laying 110mm plastic underground drainage pipes. Follow these steps carefully to ensure proper installation and functionality of your drainage system.

Safety First!

Before beginning any excavation work, always check for underground utilities. Contact your local utility companies or use a utility locating service to mark any existing underground lines.

Materials Needed:

  • 110mm plastic drainage pipes
  • Pipe fittings (connectors, elbows, etc.)
  • Gravel or crushed stone
  • Sand
  • Pipe adhesive (if using solvent weld pipes)
  • Level
  • Measuring tape
  • String line
  • Shovel
  • Wheelbarrow
  • Protective gear (gloves, safety glasses, etc.)

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Planning and Marking

  • Plan the route of your drainage system, considering the slope and final outflow point.
  • Mark the path of the trench using spray paint or stakes and string.
  • Ensure a minimum slope of 1:40 (25mm drop per 1m length) for proper water flow.

Step 2: Excavation

  • Dig a trench along the marked path.
  • The trench should be about 300mm wider than the pipe diameter and at least 600mm deep.
  • Ensure the bottom of the trench is smooth and free from rocks or debris.

Step 3: Preparing the Trench Base

  • Add a 100mm layer of gravel or crushed stone to the bottom of the trench.
  • Compact the gravel layer using a plate compactor or hand tamper.
  • Add a 50mm layer of sand over the gravel and level it.

Step 4: Laying the Pipes

  • Start laying pipes from the lowest point of the system, working uphill.
  • Use a string line to ensure proper alignment and slope.
  • Connect pipes using appropriate fittings, ensuring a tight seal.
  • If using solvent weld pipes, apply adhesive according to manufacturer's instructions.

Step 5: Testing

  • Before backfilling, test the system by running water through it to check for leaks and proper flow.
  • Make any necessary adjustments or repairs.

Step 6: Backfilling

  • Once satisfied with the installation, begin backfilling the trench.
  • Add a 150mm layer of sand around and over the pipes.
  • Carefully compact this layer, taking care not to damage the pipes.
  • Continue filling the trench with excavated soil, compacting in layers.

Step 7: Final Steps

  • Restore the surface to its original condition (grass, paving, etc.).
  • Mark the location of any access points or inspection chambers for future reference.

Maintenance

Regularly inspect and clean your drainage system to ensure its longevity and effectiveness. Address any issues promptly to prevent more serious problems.

By following these steps, you should be able to successfully lay 110mm plastic underground drainage pipes. Remember to always comply with local building codes and regulations, and consult a professional if you're unsure about any aspect of the installation process.
