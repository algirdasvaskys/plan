The Big Build 12: Removing External Walls, Concreting the Oversites, and Installing Damp Proof Membrane

1. Removing External Walls

Step 1: Safety Preparations

  • Ensure all utilities (gas, electricity, water) are safely disconnected.
  • Set up safety barriers and warning signs around the work area.
  • Equip all workers with appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

Step 2: Wall Removal

  • Start from the top and work downwards.
  • Use appropriate tools such as sledgehammers, pry bars, or power tools depending on the wall material.
  • Remove debris as you go to maintain a safe working environment.

Warning: Always check if the wall is load-bearing before removal. If it is, consult a structural engineer and install temporary supports before removal.

2. Concreting the Oversites

Step 1: Site Preparation

  • Clear the area of all debris and level the ground.
  • Compact the soil to provide a stable base.
  • Install a layer of hardcore (crushed stone) and compact it.

Step 2: Laying the Concrete

  • Mix the concrete according to specifications.
  • Pour the concrete, ensuring an even spread.
  • Use a vibrating screed or float to level the surface.
  • Allow the concrete to cure for the recommended time (usually 24-48 hours for initial set).

3. Installing Damp Proof Membrane

Step 1: Preparation

  • Ensure the concrete is fully cured and the surface is clean and dry.
  • Repair any cracks or imperfections in the concrete surface.

Step 2: Laying the Membrane

  • Roll out the damp proof membrane over the entire concrete surface.
  • Overlap joints by at least 150mm and seal with tape designed for this purpose.
  • Ensure the membrane extends up the walls by at least 150mm.

Step 3: Protection

  • Cover the membrane with a layer of sand or screed to protect it from damage.
  • Take care not to puncture the membrane during subsequent construction activities.

Note: Always follow local building regulations and manufacturer's instructions. For complex projects, consider hiring professional contractors.
