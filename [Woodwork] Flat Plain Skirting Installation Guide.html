Flat Plain Skirting Installation Guide

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Flat plain skirting boards
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Miter saw or hand saw with miter box
  • Coping saw (for internal corners)
  • Nail gun or hammer and finishing nails
  • Wood filler
  • Sandpaper (fine grit)
  • Paintbrush or roller
  • Paint or varnish
  • Safety goggles and dust mask

Step-by-Step Installation Guide:

Step 1: Measure and Mark

Measure the length of each wall where you'll be installing the skirting. Mark the measurements on your skirting boards, allowing for corner joints.

Step 2: Cut the Skirting Boards

Use a miter saw to cut the skirting boards to the correct length. For external corners, cut at a 45-degree angle. For internal corners, one piece should be cut straight, while the other should be coped.

Step 3: Coping Internal Corners

For the coped joint, cut the board at a 45-degree angle, then use a coping saw to cut along the profile of the skirting, removing the back portion of the miter cut.

Step 4: Dry Fit

Before attaching, dry fit all the pieces to ensure they fit correctly. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 5: Attach the Skirting

Using a nail gun or hammer and finishing nails, attach the skirting to the wall. Place nails every 16 inches and near the top and bottom edges of the skirting.

Step 6: Fill Nail Holes

Use wood filler to fill in the nail holes and any gaps in the joints. Allow to dry completely.

Step 7: Sand

Once the wood filler is dry, sand the filled areas smooth using fine-grit sandpaper.

Step 8: Paint or Varnish

Apply paint or varnish to match your d√©cor. Use a paintbrush for detailed areas and a roller for larger surfaces. Apply multiple coats as needed, allowing each coat to dry thoroughly.

Tips:

  • Always measure twice and cut once to avoid waste.
  • Use a stud finder to locate wall studs for secure nailing.
  • If your walls aren't perfectly straight, you may need to scribe the skirting to fit flush against the wall.
  • For a professional finish, caulk any small gaps between the skirting and the wall before painting.

Remember to work safely and wear appropriate safety gear, especially when cutting and sanding. Take your time with each step to ensure a professional-looking result.
