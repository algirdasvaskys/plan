Auto Changeover Switch (ATS) Connection in Distribution Board

Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. This guide is for informational purposes only. Always consult a licensed electrician for professional installation and follow local electrical codes and regulations.

Introduction

An Auto Changeover Switch (ATS) is an essential component in electrical systems where an alternative power source, such as a generator, is used as a backup to the main power supply. The ATS automatically switches between the main power supply and the backup power source when it detects a power failure, ensuring continuous power supply to critical loads.

Components Required

  • Auto Changeover Switch (ATS)
  • Distribution Board
  • Main Power Supply
  • Backup Power Source (e.g., Generator)
  • Circuit Breakers
  • Cables and Wires
  • Tools (screwdrivers, wire strippers, multimeter, etc.)

Step-by-Step Connection Process

  1. Prepare the Distribution Board: Ensure the main power is off and the distribution board is ready for the installation of the ATS.
  2. Install the ATS: Mount the ATS securely in or near the distribution board, ensuring easy access for maintenance.
  3. Connect the Main Power Supply:
    • Connect the main power supply lines (L1, L2, L3, and N) to the corresponding terminals on the ATS marked for utility or mains power.
    • Install a suitable circuit breaker between the main power supply and the ATS for protection.
  4. Connect the Backup Power Source:
    • Connect the backup power source (generator) lines to the corresponding terminals on the ATS marked for generator power.
    • Install a circuit breaker between the generator and the ATS for protection.
  5. Connect the Load:
    • Connect the load lines from the ATS output to the main bus bars of the distribution board.
    • Ensure proper sizing of cables based on the load requirements.
  6. Connect the Control Circuits:
    • Connect the control circuits for the ATS, including start signal for the generator (if applicable).
    • Ensure proper connection of neutral and grounding as per local electrical codes.
  7. Set Up ATS Parameters: Configure the ATS settings, including:
    • Transfer time delay
    • Voltage sensitivity
    • Frequency tolerance
    • Cool-down time for the generator
  8. Test the System:
    • Verify all connections are secure and properly insulated.
    • Power up the system and test the ATS operation by simulating a power failure.
    • Ensure smooth transition between main power and backup power.
  9. Label and Document: Clearly label all connections and document the setup for future reference and maintenance.

Safety Considerations

  • Always work with the power off and use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE).
  • Follow all local electrical codes and regulations.
  • Ensure proper grounding of all components.
  • Use appropriately sized cables and circuit breakers.
  • Regularly inspect and maintain the ATS and associated equipment.

Conclusion

Proper installation of an Auto Changeover Switch in a distribution board is crucial for ensuring reliable power supply in critical applications. While this guide provides an overview of the process, it's essential to engage a qualified electrician for the actual installation to ensure safety and compliance with local regulations.
