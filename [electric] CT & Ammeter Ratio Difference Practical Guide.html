Learning Practically: What to Do When CT & Ammeter Ratio is Different

In electrical measurements, it's crucial to understand how to handle situations where the Current Transformer (CT) ratio and the Ammeter ratio are different. This guide will help you navigate through this practical scenario.

Understanding the Basics

  • CT (Current Transformer): A device used to measure alternating current.
  • Ammeter: An instrument for measuring electric current in amperes.
  • Ratio: The relationship between the primary and secondary currents in a CT or the full-scale reading of an ammeter.

Steps to Handle Different CT and Ammeter Ratios

  1. Identify the CT ratio and Ammeter ratio
  2. Calculate the multiplication factor
  3. Take the Ammeter reading
  4. Apply the multiplication factor to get the actual current

Detailed Procedure

  1. Identify Ratios:
    • Check the CT nameplate for its ratio (e.g., 100:5)
    • Note the Ammeter's full-scale reading (e.g., 5A)
  2. Calculate Multiplication Factor:
    • Multiplication Factor = (CT Primary Current) / (CT Secondary Current * Ammeter Full Scale)
    • Example: For CT ratio 100:5 and Ammeter 5A full scale:
      Multiplication Factor = 100 / (5 * 5) = 4
  3. Take Ammeter Reading: Record the current shown on the Ammeter
  4. Calculate Actual Current: Multiply the Ammeter reading by the multiplication factor

Safety Warning

Always prioritize safety when working with electrical equipment. Ensure proper insulation and use appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

Practical Example

Let's say you have a CT with a ratio of 200:5 and an Ammeter with a 5A full scale. The Ammeter shows a reading of 3A.

  1. CT Ratio: 200:5, Ammeter Full Scale: 5A
  2. Multiplication Factor = 200 / (5 * 5) = 8
  3. Ammeter Reading: 3A
  4. Actual Current = 3A * 8 = 24A
