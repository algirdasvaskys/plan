Slab Shuttering and Steel Reinforcement Guide

1. Tips for Shuttering

  • Ensure the surface is clean and level before starting shuttering.
  • Use sturdy materials like plywood or metal sheets for shuttering.
  • Apply a release agent to the shuttering surface for easy removal after concrete curing.
  • Check for proper alignment and bracing to prevent bulging during concrete pouring.
  • Seal all joints to prevent concrete leakage.
Tip: Always double-check measurements and levels before fixing the shuttering in place.

2. Steel Providing on Slab

  • Calculate the required steel reinforcement based on structural design and load requirements.
  • Use the correct size and grade of reinforcement bars as specified in the design.
  • Ensure proper spacing between reinforcement bars.
  • Tie reinforcement bars securely at intersections.
  • Provide adequate concrete cover to protect steel from corrosion.
Tip: Use plastic spacers or chairs to maintain the required concrete cover.

3. Wooden Ballies for Slab Shuttering

  • Choose straight, strong wooden ballies to support the shuttering.
  • Ensure ballies are of uniform size and free from defects.
  • Space ballies at regular intervals to provide adequate support.
  • Use adjustable props or wedges for fine-tuning the height of the shuttering.
  • Cross-brace the ballies for added stability.
Tip: Treat wooden ballies with preservatives to extend their lifespan and prevent insect infestation.

4. Safety Considerations

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) such as hard hats, gloves, and safety shoes.
  • Ensure proper access and working platforms are in place.
  • Regularly inspect shuttering and support systems for any signs of weakness or damage.
  • Follow proper lifting techniques when handling heavy materials.
  • Keep the work area clean and free from trip hazards.
Tip: Conduct a safety briefing before starting work and ensure all workers are aware of potential hazards.

5. Quality Control

  • Regularly check levels and alignments during the shuttering process.
  • Inspect reinforcement placement before concrete pouring.
  • Ensure proper vibration during concrete pouring to eliminate air pockets.
  • Monitor concrete curing conditions and duration.
  • Carefully remove shuttering to avoid damaging the concrete surface.
Tip: Keep detailed records of all quality control checks for future reference and compliance.
