Using Metric OSB and Tru Stud System for Easy Stud Work

Introduction

Metric Oriented Strand Board (OSB) and the Tru Stud System offer an efficient and precise method for stud work. This guide will walk you through the process of using these materials to create sturdy, accurate wall frames.

Materials Needed

  • Metric OSB sheets
  • Tru Stud System components
  • Screws or nails
  • Measuring tape
  • Circular saw or table saw
  • Drill/driver
  • Level
  • Pencil

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Measure and Plan

    Measure the area where you'll be installing the stud wall. Plan your stud spacing according to local building codes, typically 400mm or 600mm on center for metric measurements.

  2. Prepare the Tru Stud System

    Cut the Tru Stud tracks to the desired length for the top and bottom plates of your wall frame.

  3. Install Bottom Track

    Secure the bottom track to the floor, ensuring it's level and properly aligned.

  4. Cut Studs

    Measure and cut the Tru Stud vertical members to fit between the top and bottom tracks, accounting for the thickness of the tracks.

  5. Install Studs

    Insert the cut studs into the bottom track at your marked intervals. Ensure they're plumb before securing.

  6. Attach Top Track

    Place the top track over the vertical studs and secure it in place.

  7. Measure and Cut OSB

    Measure the frame and cut your Metric OSB sheets to fit. Remember to account for any openings like doors or windows.

  8. Install OSB

    Attach the OSB sheets to the frame using appropriate screws or nails. Start at one corner and work your way across, ensuring the edges align with the center of the studs.

  9. Finish

    Once all OSB is installed, double-check all connections and ensure the wall is straight and plumb.

Tips for Success

Tip 1: Use a stud finder to locate existing studs if you're attaching your new wall to an existing structure.

Tip 2: When cutting OSB, always wear appropriate safety gear including safety glasses and a dust mask.

Tip 3: Pre-drill holes in the OSB to prevent splitting when attaching to the frame.

Conclusion

Using Metric OSB and the Tru Stud System simplifies the process of creating accurate and sturdy stud walls. By following these steps and tips, you can efficiently complete your stud work project with professional results.
