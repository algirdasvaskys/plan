Measuring HACK (Hip Angle Correction Kit)

The Hip Angle Correction Kit (HACK) is a measurement used in orthopedics and biomechanics to assess and correct hip joint angles. This guide will walk you through the process of measuring HACK accurately.

Equipment Needed:

  • Goniometer
  • Marking pen
  • Flat, firm surface
  • Assistant (recommended)

Step-by-Step Measurement Process:

Step 1: Patient Positioning

Have the patient lie supine (on their back) on a flat, firm surface. Ensure their legs are straight and relaxed.

Step 2: Identify Anatomical Landmarks

Locate and mark the following points with a marking pen:

  • Anterior Superior Iliac Spine (ASIS)
  • Center of the hip joint (approximately 2cm below the midpoint of the inguinal ligament)
  • Lateral epicondyle of the femur

Step 3: Measure Resting Hip Angle

Using the goniometer:

  1. Align the stationary arm with a line from the ASIS to the center of the hip joint.
  2. Align the movable arm with the lateral midline of the femur, using the greater trochanter and lateral epicondyle as reference points.
  3. Record the angle shown on the goniometer. This is the resting hip angle.

Step 4: Apply HACK

Apply the Hip Angle Correction Kit according to the manufacturer's instructions. This usually involves placing wedges or supports under specific areas of the pelvis or thigh.

Step 5: Measure Corrected Hip Angle

Repeat the measurement process from Step 3 with the HACK in place. Record this new angle as the corrected hip angle.

Step 6: Calculate HACK Measurement

The HACK measurement is the difference between the corrected hip angle and the resting hip angle:

HACK = Corrected Hip Angle - Resting Hip Angle

Important Notes:

  • Ensure consistent patient positioning throughout the measurement process.
  • Take multiple measurements and use the average for increased accuracy.
  • Always follow proper hygiene and safety protocols when working with patients.
  • The HACK measurement should be interpreted by a qualified healthcare professional in the context of the patient's overall clinical picture.
