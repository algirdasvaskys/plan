Two Wattmeter Method: 3 Phase Power Measurement

Introduction

The Two Wattmeter Method is a widely used technique for measuring power in a three-phase system. It allows for the measurement of total power using only two wattmeters, making it an efficient and cost-effective approach.

Principle

The method is based on the principle that the total power in a balanced or unbalanced three-phase system can be measured using two wattmeters connected appropriately.

Connection Diagram

In the Two Wattmeter Method:

  • One current coil of a wattmeter is connected in series with one line (e.g., R phase).
  • The corresponding voltage coil is connected between this line and the third line (B phase).
  • The second wattmeter is similarly connected, with its current coil in the third line (B phase) and its voltage coil between B and Y phases.

Phasor Diagram

Power Calculation

The total power in the three-phase system is given by the algebraic sum of the two wattmeter readings:

Ptotal = W1 + W2

Where:

  • Ptotal is the total three-phase power
  • W1 is the reading of the first wattmeter
  • W2 is the reading of the second wattmeter

Power Factor Determination

The power factor of the system can be determined from the ratio of the two wattmeter readings:

tan φ = √3 * (W1 - W2) / (W1 + W2)

Where φ is the phase angle between voltage and current.

Advantages

  • Requires only two wattmeters, reducing cost and complexity
  • Applicable to both balanced and unbalanced loads
  • Can measure power factor in addition to total power

Limitations

  • Not suitable for very low power factor loads (below 0.5)
  • Requires careful interpretation when one wattmeter reading is negative
