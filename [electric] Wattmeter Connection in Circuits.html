Wattmeter Connection in Circuits

A wattmeter is an instrument used to measure the electric power (or the rate of energy transfer) in a circuit. Understanding how to properly connect a wattmeter is crucial for accurate power measurements.

Types of Wattmeter Connections

There are two main types of wattmeter connections:

  1. Low Power Factor (LPF) Connection
  2. High Power Factor (HPF) Connection

1. Low Power Factor (LPF) Connection

In the LPF connection:

  • The current coil is connected in series with the load.
  • The potential coil is connected across the load.

This connection is used when the circuit has a low power factor (typically less than 0.5).

2. High Power Factor (HPF) Connection

In the HPF connection:

  • The potential coil is connected before the current coil.
  • The current coil is still in series with the load.

This connection is used when the circuit has a high power factor (typically greater than 0.5).

Choosing the Correct Connection

The choice between LPF and HPF connections depends on the power factor of the circuit:

  • For power factors less than 0.5, use the LPF connection.
  • For power factors greater than 0.5, use the HPF connection.

Using the correct connection ensures more accurate power measurements, especially in circuits with very high or very low power factors.

Problem: Wattmeter Connection

Question: A wattmeter is to be connected in a circuit with a power factor of 0.8. The load draws a current of 5A at 220V. Which type of connection should be used, and what would be the expected wattmeter reading?

Solution:

  1. Given power factor = 0.8, which is greater than 0.5, we should use the HPF connection.
  2. To calculate the expected wattmeter reading:
    • Power = Voltage × Current × Power Factor
    • P = 220V × 5A × 0.8
    • P = 880 Watts

Answer: Use the High Power Factor (HPF) connection. The expected wattmeter reading would be 880 Watts.
