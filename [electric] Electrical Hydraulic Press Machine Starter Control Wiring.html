Electrical Hydraulic Press Machine Starter Control Wiring

This guide provides a comprehensive method for wiring the starter control of an electrical hydraulic press machine. Follow these steps carefully to ensure proper and safe installation.

Components Required:

  • Three-phase power supply
  • Main circuit breaker
  • Contactor
  • Overload relay
  • Start and stop pushbuttons
  • Emergency stop button
  • Hydraulic pump motor
  • Control transformer
  • Fuses
  • Indicator lights

Wiring Procedure:

Step 1: Main Power Circuit

Connect the three-phase power supply to the main circuit breaker. From the circuit breaker, connect to the contactor's main contacts.

Step 2: Motor Connection

Connect the output of the contactor to the overload relay, then to the hydraulic pump motor.

Step 3: Control Circuit

Install a control transformer to step down the voltage for the control circuit (typically 120V or 24V).

Step 4: Start and Stop Buttons

Wire the start button (normally open) and stop button (normally closed) in series with the contactor coil.

Step 5: Emergency Stop

Wire the emergency stop button (normally closed) in series with the stop button.

Step 6: Overload Protection

Connect the normally closed auxiliary contact of the overload relay in series with the control circuit.

Step 7: Indicator Lights

Wire indicator lights in parallel with the contactor coil to show machine status (e.g., green for running, red for stopped).

Step 8: Fuse Protection

Install fuses in the control circuit for additional protection.

Safety Considerations:

  • Ensure all power is disconnected before starting the wiring process.
  • Follow local electrical codes and regulations.
  • Use appropriate wire sizes for the current ratings.
  • Properly ground all components.
  • Test all safety features, including the emergency stop, before operation.

Conclusion

After completing the wiring, double-check all connections and perform a thorough test of the system. Ensure that the start and stop buttons function correctly, the emergency stop immediately cuts power to the motor, and all indicator lights work as intended. If any issues arise during testing, review the wiring and consult with a qualified electrician if necessary.
