Cutting Thick Cement Boards: It's All About the Blade!

Cutting thick cement boards can be challenging, but with the right blade and technique, you can achieve clean, precise cuts. This guide will walk you through the process, emphasizing the crucial role of the blade in successful cement board cutting.

1. Choosing the Right Blade

The most important factor in cutting cement boards is selecting the appropriate blade:

  • Diamond Blade: Ideal for thick cement boards. These blades have diamond particles embedded in the cutting edge, providing superior cutting power and longevity.
  • Carbide-Tipped Blade: A good alternative for occasional use. These blades are less expensive but wear out faster than diamond blades.
Tip: For thick cement boards (1/2 inch or thicker), always opt for a diamond blade for the best results.

2. Preparing for the Cut

  1. Measure and mark your cutting line clearly on the cement board.
  2. Secure the cement board on a stable work surface, ensuring the cut line extends beyond the edge.
  3. Wear appropriate safety gear: safety glasses, dust mask, and hearing protection.

3. Cutting Technique

  1. Use a circular saw with the chosen blade for straight cuts.
  2. Set the blade depth to about 1/8 inch deeper than the thickness of the cement board.
  3. Start the saw before contacting the board and maintain a steady, moderate speed throughout the cut.
  4. Apply gentle pressure, letting the blade do the work. Forcing the saw can cause the blade to overheat or the board to crack.
Warning: Never use a jigsaw for cutting thick cement boards. The up-and-down motion can cause the board to crack or chip.

4. Making Curved or Corner Cuts

For curved cuts or corners:

  1. Use a diamond-grit hole saw for circular cuts.
  2. For complex shapes, make a series of closely spaced plunge cuts along the curve, then break off the waste pieces and smooth the edge with a rasp.

5. Finishing the Cut

After cutting:

  1. Smooth any rough edges with a rasp or sandpaper.
  2. Clean up the dust immediately to prevent it from settling and hardening.

6. Blade Maintenance

To ensure your blade remains effective:

  • Clean the blade after each use to remove cement dust.
  • Check the blade regularly for wear and replace when necessary.
  • Store the blade in a dry place to prevent rusting.
Tip: A sharp, well-maintained blade not only cuts better but also extends the life of your power tools.

Remember, when it comes to cutting thick cement boards, investing in a high-quality blade and using the proper technique will save you time, effort, and frustration in the long run. With practice and the right tools, you'll be cutting cement boards like a pro!
