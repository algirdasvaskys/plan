Making a Tilting Eave Fillet for a Roof

A tilting eave fillet is an important component in roof construction that helps direct water away from the building and prevents water from seeping under the roofing material. This guide will walk you through the process of making and installing a tilting eave fillet.

Materials Needed:

  • Pressure-treated lumber (1x4 or 1x6, depending on roof pitch)
  • Galvanized roofing nails
  • Roofing felt or underlayment
  • Drip edge flashing

Tools Required:

  • Circular saw or hand saw
  • Hammer or nail gun
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Chalk line
  • Level

Safety Precautions:

  • Wear safety glasses and work gloves
  • Use caution when working at heights
  • Ensure proper ladder safety
  • Be aware of power tool safety

Steps to Make and Install a Tilting Eave Fillet:

Step 1: Measure and Mark

Measure the length of the eave where you'll be installing the fillet. Mark this measurement on your lumber.

Step 2: Cut the Lumber

Using a circular saw or hand saw, cut the lumber to the measured length. If your roof has a steep pitch, you may need to cut the lumber at an angle to match the roof slope.

Step 3: Position the Fillet

Place the cut lumber along the eave, ensuring it's flush with the edge of the roof decking. The fillet should tilt slightly towards the outer edge of the roof to direct water away.

Step 4: Secure the Fillet

Use galvanized roofing nails to secure the fillet to the roof decking. Space the nails about 8 inches apart.

Step 5: Install Drip Edge

Install the drip edge flashing over the fillet, ensuring it extends past the edge of the roof. Nail the drip edge in place.

Step 6: Apply Roofing Felt

Roll out the roofing felt or underlayment, starting at the bottom edge of the roof. Overlap the felt over the drip edge and fillet. Secure with roofing nails.

Step 7: Continue Roofing

Proceed with installing your roofing material (shingles, tiles, etc.) according to manufacturer instructions, ensuring proper overlap with the drip edge and fillet.

By following these steps, you'll create a tilting eave fillet that helps protect your roof and building from water damage. Remember to always prioritize safety and consult local building codes or a professional if you're unsure about any part of the process.
