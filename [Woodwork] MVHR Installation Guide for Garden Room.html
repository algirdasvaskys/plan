MVHR Installation Guide for Garden Room

This guide provides a step-by-step method for installing a Mechanical Ventilation with Heat Recovery (MVHR) system in your garden room.

Prerequisites

  • MVHR unit
  • Ducting and vents
  • Insulation materials
  • Power tools (drill, saw)
  • Mounting brackets
  • Sealant and tape
  • Safety equipment (gloves, goggles, dust mask)

Installation Steps

Step 1: Plan the System Layout

  • Determine the best location for the MVHR unit (usually in the loft or a utility space)
  • Plan the routes for supply and extract air ducts
  • Identify locations for air vents in each room

Step 2: Prepare for Installation

  • Ensure the garden room's structure can support the MVHR unit
  • Cut holes for ducting and vents
  • Install proper insulation around duct routes

Step 3: Mount the MVHR Unit

  • Securely attach mounting brackets to the chosen location
  • Carefully lift and fix the MVHR unit to the brackets
  • Ensure the unit is level and stable

Step 4: Install Ducting

  • Connect ducts to the MVHR unit's intake and exhaust ports
  • Route ducts to planned vent locations, ensuring smooth bends to maintain airflow
  • Secure ducts with brackets and seal joints with tape or mastic

Step 5: Install Vents

  • Fit supply vents in living areas and bedrooms
  • Install extract vents in humid areas (bathroom, kitchen)
  • Ensure all vents are properly sealed to prevent air leakage

Step 6: Electrical Connections

  • Connect the MVHR unit to a suitable power supply
  • Install any control panels or sensors as per manufacturer's instructions

Warning: Electrical work should be carried out by a qualified electrician to ensure safety and compliance with local regulations.

Step 7: System Testing and Balancing

  • Turn on the system and check for any unusual noises or vibrations
  • Test airflow at each vent to ensure proper distribution
  • Adjust fan speeds and dampers if necessary to balance the system

Step 8: Final Checks and Handover

  • Ensure all ducts and vents are properly insulated
  • Check for any air leaks and seal if found
  • Clean up the installation area
  • Provide the user with operation and maintenance instructions

Maintenance Tips

  • Regularly clean or replace filters (typically every 3-6 months)
  • Annual inspection of ductwork and vents for damage or blockages
  • Clean vents and grilles to ensure unrestricted airflow
  • Schedule professional servicing as recommended by the manufacturer

By following these steps, you can successfully install an MVHR system in your garden room, improving air quality and energy efficiency. Remember to consult local building regulations and consider professional installation for complex systems.
