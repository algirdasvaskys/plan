How to Check a Motor with an Insulation Tester

⚠️ Safety First!

Always ensure the motor is completely disconnected from its power source before testing. Wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) including insulated gloves and safety glasses.

Steps to Check Motor Insulation:

Step 1: Prepare the Motor

Disconnect the motor from all power sources and remove it from its mounting if necessary. Clean the motor thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris.

Step 2: Set Up the Insulation Tester

Select an appropriate test voltage based on the motor's rated voltage. For motors rated up to 1000V, typically use a 500V or 1000V test voltage.

Step 3: Connect the Insulation Tester

Connect the negative (earth) lead of the tester to the motor frame. Connect the positive lead to one of the motor terminals.

Step 4: Perform the Test

Activate the tester and apply the test voltage for about 60 seconds. Record the insulation resistance reading.

Step 5: Test Other Windings

Repeat the test for each motor winding, always keeping the negative lead connected to the frame.

Step 6: Interpret Results

The minimum acceptable insulation resistance is typically 1 megohm per 1000V of motor rating, plus 1 megohm. For example, a 460V motor should have at least 1.46 megohms of insulation resistance.

Step 7: Record and Compare

Record all readings and compare them to previous tests or manufacturer specifications. A significant drop in insulation resistance over time may indicate deterioration.
