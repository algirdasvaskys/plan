Temperature Controller Connection Guide

Warning: Working with electrical components can be dangerous. Always ensure power is disconnected before making any connections. If you're not confident in your abilities, consult a qualified electrician.

1. Components Overview

  • Temperature Controller
  • RTD (Resistance Temperature Detector)
  • Thermocouple
  • Solid State Relay (SSR)
  • Power Supply
  • Heating Element or Cooling Device

2. RTD Connection

RTDs are typically more accurate than thermocouples for lower temperature ranges.

  1. Identify the RTD input terminals on your temperature controller. They are usually labeled as RTD+ and RTD-.
  2. Connect the RTD's red wire to the RTD+ terminal.
  3. Connect the RTD's white wire to the RTD- terminal.
  4. If your RTD has a third wire (often black), connect it to the RTD- terminal as well. This is for lead wire compensation.

3. Thermocouple Connection

Thermocouples are better for higher temperature ranges and faster response times.

  1. Locate the thermocouple input terminals on your controller. They are usually marked as TC+ and TC-.
  2. Connect the positive (usually red) wire of the thermocouple to the TC+ terminal.
  3. Connect the negative (usually blue or white) wire to the TC- terminal.
  4. Ensure you're using the correct type of thermocouple (K, J, T, etc.) that matches your controller's settings.

4. Solid State Relay (SSR) Connection

  1. Identify the output terminals on your temperature controller. They're often labeled as OUT+ and OUT-.
  2. Connect the OUT+ terminal to the positive input of the SSR.
  3. Connect the OUT- terminal to the negative input of the SSR.
  4. On the output side of the SSR:
    • Connect one terminal to your heating element or cooling device.
    • Connect the other terminal to your power source.

5. Power Supply Connection

  1. Locate the power input terminals on your temperature controller.
  2. Connect the positive wire from your power supply to the positive terminal.
  3. Connect the negative wire from your power supply to the negative terminal.
  4. Ensure you're using the correct voltage as specified by your controller's requirements.

6. Final Steps

  1. Double-check all connections to ensure they are secure and correct.
  2. Configure your temperature controller settings:
    • Set the input type (RTD or Thermocouple)
    • Set the temperature range
    • Set the control parameters (PID values if applicable)
  3. Power on the system and test its operation.
  4. Monitor the system closely during initial operation to ensure everything is functioning correctly.
Note: This guide provides a general overview. Always refer to the specific manuals for your components, as connection details may vary between manufacturers and models.
