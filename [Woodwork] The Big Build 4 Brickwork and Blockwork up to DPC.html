The Big Build 4: Brickwork and Blockwork up to DPC

This guide outlines the process of laying brickwork and blockwork up to the Damp Proof Course (DPC) level in construction. This is a crucial stage in building as it forms the foundation of the structure.

Steps:

1. Preparation

  • Ensure the foundation is level and clean
  • Mark out the corners and wall lines
  • Set up string lines to guide the brickwork

2. Mixing Mortar

  • Mix cement, sand, and water to the correct consistency
  • Use a 1:4 cement to sand ratio for general purpose mortar
  • Ensure the mix is workable but not too wet

3. Laying the First Course

  • Spread a layer of mortar on the foundation
  • Lay the first brick at a corner, checking it's level
  • Continue laying bricks along the string line
  • Use a spirit level to ensure the course is level

4. Building Up the Courses

  • Apply mortar to the top of the first course
  • Lay the next course, offsetting the joints (brick bonding)
  • Use a trowel to remove excess mortar
  • Check vertical alignment with a plumb bob or spirit level

5. Installing the DPC

  • Lay the DPC material (usually a plastic sheet) when you reach ground level
  • Ensure it covers the full width of the wall
  • Overlap joints by at least 100mm

6. Continuing Above DPC

  • Continue laying bricks or blocks above the DPC
  • Ensure the DPC is not damaged during this process
  • Build up to the required height for floor level

Important Considerations:

  • Maintain consistent mortar joint thickness (usually 10mm)
  • Regularly check levels and alignments
  • Allow for ventilation bricks if required
  • Clean tools and bricks as you go
  • Protect the work from rain and frost

Remember, building regulations may vary by location. Always consult local building codes and consider professional help for complex projects.
