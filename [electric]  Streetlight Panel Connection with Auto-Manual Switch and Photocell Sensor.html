Streetlight Panel Connection with Auto-Manual Switch and Photocell Sensor

Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always follow local electrical codes and safety guidelines. If you're not qualified, please consult a licensed electrician.

Components Required:

  • Streetlight panel
  • Auto-manual switch (3-position switch: Auto, Off, Manual)
  • Photocell sensor
  • Contactor
  • Circuit breaker
  • Power supply
  • Streetlight(s)
  • Wiring and connectors

Connection Method:

  1. Power Supply Connection:
    • Connect the main power supply to the input of the circuit breaker.
    • Connect the output of the circuit breaker to the contactor's power input terminals.
  2. Contactor Connection:
    • Connect the contactor's output terminals to the streetlight(s).
    • The contactor will serve as the switch to turn the streetlights on and off.
  3. Auto-Manual Switch Connection:
    • Connect the common terminal of the auto-manual switch to the contactor's control circuit.
    • Connect the "Manual" position of the switch directly to the power supply (through appropriate fusing).
    • Connect the "Auto" position of the switch to the photocell sensor.
  4. Photocell Sensor Connection:
    • Connect the photocell sensor to the power supply.
    • Connect the output of the photocell sensor to the "Auto" position on the auto-manual switch.
  5. Grounding:
    • Ensure all components are properly grounded according to local electrical codes.

Operation:

  • Auto Mode: When the switch is in the "Auto" position, the photocell sensor controls the streetlights. They will turn on at dusk and off at dawn.
  • Manual Mode: When the switch is in the "Manual" position, the streetlights will be on regardless of the time of day.
  • Off Mode: When the switch is in the "Off" position, the streetlights will remain off regardless of the time of day.

Safety Considerations:

  • Always turn off the main power before working on the panel.
  • Use appropriately rated components for the voltage and current of your system.
  • Ensure proper insulation and weather protection for outdoor components.
  • Regular maintenance and inspection of the system is recommended.
  • Follow all local electrical codes and regulations.
