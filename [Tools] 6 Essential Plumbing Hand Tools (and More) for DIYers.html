6 Essential Plumbing Hand Tools (and More) for DIYers

1. Pipe Wrench

A pipe wrench is crucial for gripping and turning pipes and fittings. It features serrated jaws that provide a strong grip on round surfaces.

  • Use: Tightening and loosening pipes and fittings
  • Sizes: Typically available in 10", 14", and 18" lengths
  • Tip: Always use two pipe wrenches to avoid damaging the pipes - one to hold, one to turn

2. Adjustable Wrench

An adjustable wrench, also known as a Crescent wrench, is versatile and can fit various sizes of nuts and bolts.

  • Use: Tightening and loosening nuts and bolts of different sizes
  • Sizes: Common sizes include 6", 8", and 10"
  • Tip: Ensure the adjustable jaw is facing the direction you're turning to prevent slipping

3. Plumber's Tape

Plumber's tape, also called Teflon tape, is used to create a watertight seal on threaded pipe joints.

  • Use: Wrapping around pipe threads before connecting fittings
  • Tip: Wrap the tape clockwise around the threads, overlapping by about 50%

4. Plunger

A plunger is essential for clearing clogs in sinks, toilets, and drains.

  • Types: Cup plunger for sinks and flange plunger for toilets
  • Tip: Ensure there's enough water to cover the plunger's head for better suction

5. Basin Wrench

A basin wrench is designed specifically for reaching and tightening or loosening nuts in tight spaces under sinks.

  • Use: Installing or removing faucets and supply lines
  • Tip: Familiarize yourself with how to adjust and use the wrench before attempting a job

6. Plumber's Snake (Auger)

A plumber's snake is a long, flexible tool used to remove clogs that are too far into the pipes for a plunger to reach.

  • Types: Hand-operated and machine-operated
  • Use: Clearing stubborn clogs in drains and toilets
  • Tip: Start with the snake fully retracted and slowly feed it into the drain

Other Useful Plumbing Tools for DIYers

  • Channellock pliers: For gripping and turning various shapes and sizes
  • Hacksaw: For cutting pipes and other materials
  • Plumber's putty: For creating watertight seals around fixtures
  • Pipe cutter: For making clean, straight cuts on pipes
  • Propane torch: For soldering copper pipes (use with caution)
  • Plumbing-specific screwdrivers: For removing and installing various fixtures

Remember, safety should always be your top priority when doing DIY plumbing. If you're unsure about a task or if it involves complex systems like gas lines, it's best to consult a professional plumber.
