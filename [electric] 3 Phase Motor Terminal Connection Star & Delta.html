3 Phase Motor Terminal Connection: Star & Delta

Three-phase induction motors are widely used in industrial applications due to their efficiency and reliability. The two most common types of connections for these motors are Star (Y) and Delta (Δ) configurations. This document explains both connection methods in detail.

Star (Y) Connection

In a Star connection, also known as a Wye connection, the ends of the three windings are connected together at a common point, forming a "star" shape.

Connection Method:

  1. Identify the six terminal ends of the motor (U1, V1, W1, U2, V2, W2).
  2. Connect U2, V2, and W2 together. This forms the neutral point or star point.
  3. Connect U1, V1, and W1 to the three phases of the power supply (L1, L2, L3 respectively).

Characteristics:

  • Lower starting current
  • Higher starting torque
  • Suitable for high starting load applications
  • Line voltage = √3 × Phase voltage
  • Line current = Phase current

Delta (Δ) Connection

In a Delta connection, the end of each winding is connected to the start of the next winding, forming a triangular or "delta" shape.

Connection Method:

  1. Identify the six terminal ends of the motor (U1, V1, W1, U2, V2, W2).
  2. Connect U1 to W2, V1 to U2, and W1 to V2.
  3. Connect the junctions of U1-W2, V1-U2, and W1-V2 to the three phases of the power supply (L1, L2, L3 respectively).

Characteristics:

  • Higher starting current
  • Lower starting torque
  • Suitable for low starting load applications
  • Line voltage = Phase voltage
  • Line current = √3 × Phase current

Comparison: Star vs Delta

Characteristic Star (Y) Connection Delta (Δ) Connection
