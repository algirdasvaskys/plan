Fitting New MDF Door Linings in Old Openings

This guide will walk you through the process of fitting new MDF door linings and frames in old openings to ensure a perfect fit for your new doors.

Materials Needed:

  • MDF door lining set
  • Wood screws
  • Wood filler
  • Sandpaper (various grits)
  • Wood glue
  • Expanding foam
  • Saw (hand saw or power saw)
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Level
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Chisel
  • Hammer

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Remove the Old Door and Frame

  • Remove the existing door from its hinges.
  • Remove any trim or molding around the door frame.
  • Carefully remove the old door frame, being cautious not to damage the surrounding wall.

Step 2: Clean and Prepare the Opening

  • Remove any remaining nails, screws, or debris from the opening.
  • Check for any damage to the surrounding wall and repair if necessary.
  • Ensure the opening is clean and dry.

Step 3: Measure the Opening

  • Measure the height of the opening in three places: left side, center, and right side.
  • Measure the width of the opening at the top, middle, and bottom.
  • Use the smallest measurements to ensure your new lining will fit.
Tip: Add about 10mm to your measurements to allow for adjustments and ensure a snug fit.

Step 4: Cut the MDF Lining to Size

  • Cut the head (top) piece to the width of the opening plus twice the thickness of the side pieces.
  • Cut the two side pieces to the height of the opening minus the thickness of the head piece.

Step 5: Assemble the Frame

  • Apply wood glue to the joints where the side pieces meet the head piece.
  • Secure the joints with screws for added strength.
  • Use a square to ensure the frame is perfectly square.

Step 6: Test Fit the Frame

  • Place the assembled frame into the opening to check the fit.
  • Mark any areas that need adjustment.
  • Remove the frame and make necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Install the Frame

  • Apply a bead of expanding foam around the perimeter of the opening.
  • Place the frame into the opening, ensuring it's level and plumb.
  • Use wooden wedges to hold the frame in place temporarily.
  • Secure the frame with screws through the sides into the wall studs.
Tip: Use a level frequently during installation to ensure the frame remains straight and plumb.

Step 8: Fill and Sand

  • Once the foam has dried, trim any excess.
  • Fill screw holes and any gaps with wood filler.
  • Once dry, sand the filled areas smooth.

Step 9: Install Door Stops

  • Measure and cut door stops to fit the frame.
  • Attach the door stops to the frame, ensuring they're properly aligned for the new door.

  • Paint or stain the new lining to match your d√©cor.
  • Install any trim or molding around the frame.
  • Hang your new door and adjust as necessary.

By following these steps, you should now have a perfectly fitted new MDF door lining in your old opening, ready for your new door. Remember to take your time and measure carefully throughout the process for the best results.
