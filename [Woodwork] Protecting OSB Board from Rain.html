Protecting OSB Board from Rain

Important: OSB (Oriented Strand Board) is susceptible to moisture damage. Proper protection is crucial to maintain its structural integrity and longevity.

Understanding OSB and Moisture

OSB is an engineered wood panel made from wood strands arranged in layers and bonded with resin. While it's a cost-effective and versatile building material, it's vulnerable to moisture, especially when exposed to rain.

Methods to Protect OSB from Rain

  1. Apply a Water-Resistant Barrier

    Cover the OSB with a water-resistant barrier such as:

    • House wrap (e.g., Tyvek)
    • Roofing felt
    • Synthetic underlayment

    These materials create a protective layer that sheds water while allowing the OSB to breathe.

  2. Seal the Edges

    Apply a water-resistant sealant or paint to the exposed edges of the OSB. This prevents water from penetrating through the more vulnerable edge grain.

  3. Proper Installation

    Ensure correct installation:

    • Leave a 1/8 inch gap between panels for expansion
    • Use H-clips or tongue-and-groove edges for roof decking
    • Install panels with the correct orientation (look for stamped arrows)

  4. Temporary Protection During Construction

    If the OSB will be exposed during construction:

    • Use plastic tarps to cover the OSB
    • Install the roof covering as soon as possible
    • Minimize the time OSB is left exposed to the elements

  5. Water-Resistant OSB Products

    Consider using specially treated OSB products designed for enhanced moisture resistance, such as:

    • ZIP System Sheathing
    • AdvanTech Flooring and Sheathing

Maintenance and Inspection

Regular inspection and maintenance are crucial:

  • Check for any signs of water damage or swelling
  • Ensure all protective barriers remain intact
  • Address any leaks or water intrusion promptly

Conclusion

Protecting OSB from rain is essential for maintaining the integrity of your building structure. By applying water-resistant barriers, sealing edges, ensuring proper installation, and using temporary protection measures, you can significantly extend the life of your OSB panels and prevent costly water damage.
