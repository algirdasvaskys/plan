Calculate Transformer Short Circuit Current in 3 Steps

Short circuit current is a crucial parameter in electrical systems, particularly for transformers. It helps in designing protection systems and determining the withstand capabilities of electrical equipment. Here's a simple 3-step method to calculate the short circuit current of any transformer.

Step 1: Determine the Transformer's Full Load Current

Calculate the full load current using the transformer's rated kVA and voltage:

Full Load Current (IFL) = (kVA × 1000) / (√3 × VL-L)

Where:

  • kVA is the transformer's rated capacity
  • VL-L is the line-to-line voltage

Step 2: Determine the Transformer's Impedance

Find the transformer's percentage impedance (%Z) from its nameplate or specifications.

Step 3: Calculate the Short Circuit Current

Use the following formula to calculate the short circuit current:

Short Circuit Current (ISC) = (IFL × 100) / %Z
