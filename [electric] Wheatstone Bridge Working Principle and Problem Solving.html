Wheatstone Bridge: Working Principle and Problem Solving

1. Introduction

The Wheatstone bridge is an electrical circuit used to measure an unknown electrical resistance by balancing two legs of a bridge circuit, one containing the unknown component. It's particularly useful for medium range resistance measurements.

2. Working Principle

The Wheatstone bridge consists of four resistors arranged in a diamond shape:

When the ratio of the two resistors on one side (R1/R2) equals the ratio of the two resistors on the other side (R3/Rx), the voltage between the two midpoints (B and C) will be zero and no current will flow through the galvanometer G.

3. Balance Condition

The bridge is balanced when:

R1/R2 = R3/Rx

Rearranging this equation, we can find the unknown resistance Rx:

Rx = (R2 * R3) / R1

4. Procedure for Measurement

  1. Connect the unknown resistance Rx to one arm of the bridge.
  2. Adjust the variable resistor (usually R2) until the galvanometer shows zero current.
  3. When balanced, use the formula above to calculate Rx.

5. Advantages for Medium Range Resistance Measurement

  • High accuracy for resistances between 1Ω and 100kΩ.
  • Nulling method eliminates errors due to meter resistance.
  • Can be used with both DC and AC power sources.

6. Problem Solving Example

Problem: In a Wheatstone bridge circuit, R1 = 100Ω, R2 = 150Ω, and R3 = 50Ω. When the bridge is balanced, what is the value of the unknown resistance Rx?

Solution:

  1. We know that at balance: R1/R2 = R3/Rx
  2. Rearranging to solve for Rx: Rx = (R2 * R3) / R1
  3. Substituting the known values:
    Rx = (150Ω * 50Ω) / 100Ω = 7500 / 100 = 75Ω

Answer: The unknown resistance Rx is 75Ω.

7. Conclusion

The Wheatstone bridge is a powerful tool for measuring unknown resistances, especially in the medium range. By understanding its working principle and applying problem-solving techniques, you can accurately determine resistance values in various electrical applications.
