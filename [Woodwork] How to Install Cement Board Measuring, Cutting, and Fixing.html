How to Install Cement Board: Measuring, Cutting, and Fixing

1. Preparation

  • Ensure the subfloor is clean, dry, and level
  • Gather necessary tools: tape measure, utility knife, cement board saw, drill, screws, thinset mortar, trowel
  • Wear safety gear: dust mask, safety glasses, gloves

2. Measuring

  • Measure the room dimensions accurately
  • Plan the layout to minimize cuts and waste
  • Account for expansion gaps (usually 1/4 inch) along walls
  • Mark cut lines on cement board using a pencil and straight edge

3. Cutting

  • Score the cement board along the marked line using a utility knife
  • Snap the board along the scored line
  • For curved cuts or holes, use a cement board saw or drill with appropriate bits
  • Smooth cut edges with sandpaper if necessary

4. Fixing

  • Apply a layer of thinset mortar to the subfloor using a notched trowel
  • Place the cement board onto the mortar, pressing firmly
  • Leave 1/8 inch gaps between boards
  • Secure boards with cement board screws every 6-8 inches, countersinking slightly
  • Tape and mortar the joints between boards

5. Finishing

  • Allow the mortar to cure according to manufacturer's instructions
  • Check for any protruding screw heads and adjust if necessary
  • Clean the surface thoroughly before tiling or applying other finishes

Tips

  • Always follow manufacturer's instructions for specific products
  • Use cement board specific screws to prevent corrosion
  • Work in a well-ventilated area due to dust production
  • Consider using a waterproofing membrane for wet areas
