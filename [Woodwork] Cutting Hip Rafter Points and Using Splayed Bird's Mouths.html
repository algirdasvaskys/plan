Cutting Hip Rafter Points and Using Splayed Bird's Mouths

Introduction

Hip rafters are crucial components in roof framing, connecting the ridge board to the outside corners of the roof. Properly cutting hip rafter points and creating splayed bird's mouths ensures a secure and stable roof structure.

Tools Required

  • Framing square
  • Speed square
  • Circular saw
  • Handsaw
  • Pencil
  • Tape measure
  • Calculator

Step 1: Calculating the Hip Rafter Length

Before cutting, you need to calculate the hip rafter length:

  1. Measure the run of the common rafter.
  2. Measure the rise of the roof.
  3. Use the Pythagorean theorem to calculate the hip rafter length: √(run² + rise²)
Tip: For a more accurate calculation, consider using a construction calculator or rafter tables.

Step 2: Marking the Plumb Cut

  1. Place the framing square on the rafter stock.
  2. Align the square so that the rise is on the tongue and the run is on the blade.
  3. Mark the plumb line along the tongue.

Step 3: Marking the Bird's Mouth

The bird's mouth is the notch cut into the rafter to allow it to sit securely on the wall plate.

  1. Measure down from the top edge of the rafter along the plumb line to mark the depth of the seat cut.
  2. Use the framing square to draw a level line perpendicular to the plumb line.

Step 4: Creating a Splayed Bird's Mouth

A splayed bird's mouth provides better support and distribution of load:

  1. Instead of cutting a right angle, angle the seat cut slightly (typically 5 to 15 degrees).
  2. Use a speed square to mark this angle from the level line.
  3. Extend this angled line to intersect with the plumb line of the bird's mouth.
Tip: The splay angle should match the slope of the roof for optimal load distribution.

Step 5: Cutting the Hip Rafter

  1. Use a circular saw to make the plumb cut at the top of the rafter.
  2. Cut the bird's mouth:
    • Make the vertical (plumb) cut first.
    • Then make the angled seat cut.
  3. Use a handsaw to finish any cuts that the circular saw can't complete.
Warning: Always wear safety goggles and follow proper safety procedures when using power tools.

Step 6: Test Fitting

Before installing all hip rafters:

  1. Test fit one rafter to ensure proper alignment and seat.
  2. Make any necessary adjustments to your calculations or cutting technique.
  3. Once satisfied, use the first rafter as a template for cutting the others.

Conclusion

Cutting hip rafter points and creating splayed bird's mouths requires precision and careful calculation. With practice, you'll be able to create sturdy and well-fitted hip rafters that contribute to a strong and durable roof structure.
