How to Wire a Selector Switch in Starter with Limit Switch and Push Button

Warning: Working with electrical circuits can be dangerous. Always ensure the power is off before working on any electrical system. If you're not confident in your abilities, consult a licensed electrician.

Components Needed:

Selector Switch
Starter
Limit Switch
Push Button
Contactor
Overload Relay
Wires

Wiring Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Components

Ensure all components are in good condition and rated for your specific application. Verify that the power supply is disconnected before starting.

Step 2: Wire the Selector Switch

Connect the common terminal of the selector switch to the power supply. Wire the "Auto" position to the limit switch and the "Manual" position to the push button.

Step 3: Wire the Limit Switch

Connect one terminal of the limit switch to the "Auto" position of the selector switch. Wire the other terminal to one side of the contactor coil.

Step 4: Wire the Push Button

Connect one terminal of the push button to the "Manual" position of the selector switch. Wire the other terminal to the same side of the contactor coil as the limit switch.

Step 5: Complete the Control Circuit

Connect the other side of the contactor coil to the neutral or return line of the power supply, completing the control circuit.

Step 6: Wire the Starter

Connect the starter to the main power supply through the contactor's main contacts. Wire the overload relay in series with one of the power lines to the motor.

Step 7: Final Connections

Make sure all ground connections are secure. Double-check all wiring connections for tightness and proper insulation.

Step 8: Testing

Before powering on, perform a continuity test to ensure there are no short circuits. Once verified, power on the circuit and test both the manual and automatic operations.

Important: Always follow local electrical codes and regulations. This wiring should be done by a qualified electrician or under professional supervision.

Operation:

When the selector switch is in the "Auto" position, the starter will be controlled by the limit switch. When in the "Manual" position, the starter can be controlled by the push button.

Maintenance:

Regularly inspect all components for signs of wear or damage. Clean connections and check for loose wires. Test the operation of the selector switch, limit switch, and push button periodically to ensure proper functioning.
