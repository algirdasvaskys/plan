Key Components

1. Main Power Supply

The main power supply is typically a three-phase AC supply that provides the primary electrical power to the entire crane system. It's usually connected to the crane through a main disconnect switch or circuit breaker for safety purposes.

2. Control Transformer

The control transformer steps down the main supply voltage to a lower voltage (usually 110V or 24V) for the control circuit. This ensures that the control components operate at a safer, lower voltage.

3. Motor Starters

Motor starters are used to control the various motors in the crane (hoist, trolley, and bridge motors). They typically consist of contactors and overload relays. The contactors switch the power to the motors on and off, while the overload relays protect the motors from overheating due to excessive current.

4. Limit Switches

Limit switches are safety devices that prevent the crane from moving beyond its safe operating limits. They're typically installed at the ends of the crane's travel paths and will cut off power to the respective motor when activated.

5. Control Pendant

The control pendant is the operator's interface with the crane. It contains push buttons or joysticks that allow the operator to control the movement of the crane in various directions (up/down, left/right, forward/backward).

6. Brakes

Electrically operated brakes are used to stop and hold the load. They're typically spring-applied and electrically released, ensuring that the load is held securely in case of power failure.

Circuit Operation

When the operator activates a control on the pendant:

  1. The control circuit energizes the appropriate contactor in the motor starter.
  2. The contactor closes, connecting power to the corresponding motor.
  3. Simultaneously, the brake for that motion is electrically released.
  4. The motor operates, moving the crane or hoist as directed.
  5. When the control is released, the contactor opens, the motor stops, and the brake is re-applied.
  6. If a limit switch is activated during operation, it will interrupt the control circuit, stopping the motion in that direction.

This circuit design ensures safe and controlled operation of the EOT crane, with multiple safety features to prevent accidents and equipment damage.
