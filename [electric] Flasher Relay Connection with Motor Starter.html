Flasher Relay Connection with Motor Starter

Warning: Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always ensure that power is disconnected before working on any electrical connections. If you're not confident in your abilities, consult a licensed electrician.

Introduction

A flasher relay is often used in combination with a motor starter to create a flashing or intermittent operation of a motor. This setup is commonly used in industrial applications, signaling systems, or wherever intermittent motor operation is required.

Components Needed

  • Flasher relay
  • Motor starter
  • Motor
  • Power supply
  • Wiring and connectors
  • Control switches (if required)

Connection Procedure

Step 1: Ensure all power is disconnected before beginning the installation.

Step 2: Mount the flasher relay and motor starter securely in the control panel or enclosure.

Step 3: Connect the power supply to the flasher relay:

  • Connect the positive (+) terminal of the power supply to the input terminal of the flasher relay.
  • Connect the negative (-) terminal of the power supply to the common terminal of the flasher relay.

Step 4: Connect the flasher relay to the motor starter coil:

  • Connect the normally open (NO) terminal of the flasher relay to one side of the motor starter coil.
  • Connect the other side of the motor starter coil to the negative (-) terminal of the power supply.

Step 5: Connect the motor starter to the motor:

  • Follow the manufacturer's instructions for connecting the motor starter to the motor.
  • Typically, this involves connecting the motor starter's output terminals to the motor's input terminals.

Step 6: If required, install and connect any control switches:

  • Connect the control switch in series with the flasher relay's input.
  • This allows manual control of the flashing operation.

Step 7: Double-check all connections to ensure they are secure and correct.

Step 8: Restore power to the system and test the operation:

  • The motor should start and stop at intervals determined by the flasher relay's settings.
  • Adjust the flasher relay's timing if necessary.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you can successfully connect a flasher relay with a motor starter to achieve intermittent motor operation. Remember to always prioritize safety and consult the specific documentation for your components, as details may vary depending on the exact models used.
