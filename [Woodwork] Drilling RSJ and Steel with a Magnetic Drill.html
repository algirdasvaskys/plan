Drilling RSJ and Steel for Bolts and Fixings with a Magnetic Drill

Safety First

Always wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) including safety glasses, hearing protection, and work gloves. Ensure the work area is clean and well-lit.

Equipment Needed:

  • Magnetic drill
  • Appropriate drill bits for steel
  • Cutting fluid or lubricant
  • Marker or center punch
  • Measuring tape
  • Level
  • Deburring tool

Step-by-Step Method:

1. Preparation

  • Clean the steel surface to remove any dirt, rust, or paint.
  • Mark the drilling locations accurately using a marker or center punch.
  • Ensure the steel is properly supported and secured.

2. Set Up the Magnetic Drill

  • Position the magnetic drill on the steel surface, aligning it with your marked drilling point.
  • Activate the magnet to secure the drill in place.
  • Double-check the alignment and adjust if necessary.

3. Prepare for Drilling

  • Install the appropriate drill bit for the size of hole you need.
  • Apply cutting fluid or lubricant to the drill bit and drilling area.
  • Set the drill speed according to the bit size and steel thickness.

4. Start Drilling

  • Begin drilling slowly to create a pilot hole.
  • Gradually increase pressure and speed as the hole deepens.
  • Maintain steady pressure throughout the drilling process.
  • Periodically add more cutting fluid as needed.

5. Complete the Hole

  • Continue drilling until you've penetrated through the steel.
  • Reduce pressure as you near the end to prevent the drill from suddenly breaking through.
  • Once through, keep the drill running as you withdraw it from the hole.

6. Finishing

  • Deactivate the magnetic base and remove the drill.
  • Use a deburring tool to smooth any rough edges around the hole.
  • Clean the area of any metal shavings or debris.

7. Quality Check

  • Measure the hole to ensure it meets the required specifications.
  • Check that the hole is perpendicular to the surface using a level if necessary.
  • Test-fit any bolts or fixings to ensure proper fit.

Important Notes:

  • Always follow the manufacturer's instructions for your specific magnetic drill model.
  • For larger holes, you may need to start with a smaller pilot hole and gradually increase the bit size.
  • When drilling multiple holes, allow the drill and bit to cool between operations to prevent overheating.
  • If drilling overhead or on vertical surfaces, use extra caution and ensure the magnetic base is securely attached.
