How to Fix Skirting Board Using Grab Adhesives

  1. Prepare the surface: Ensure both the wall and the back of the skirting board are clean, dry, and free from dust, grease, or loose particles.
  2. Measure and cut: Measure the length of the wall and cut the skirting board to size using a miter saw for precise angles at corners.
  3. Choose the right adhesive: Select a grab adhesive suitable for both the wall material and the skirting board material.
  4. Apply the adhesive: Apply the grab adhesive to the back of the skirting board in a zigzag pattern, ensuring to cover the edges and middle.
  5. Position the skirting board: Carefully place the skirting board against the wall, pressing firmly to ensure good contact.
  6. Secure and align: Use a spirit level to ensure the skirting board is straight. You may need to use temporary supports or masking tape to hold it in place while the adhesive sets.
  7. Clean excess: Immediately remove any excess adhesive that squeezes out using a damp cloth or as per the manufacturer's instructions.
  8. Allow to dry: Leave the skirting board undisturbed for the recommended drying time (usually 24-48 hours).
  9. Finish: Once dry, remove any temporary supports and fill any gaps with caulk if necessary. Paint or varnish as desired.

Tips for Best Results

  • For uneven walls, use a flexible grab adhesive that can fill small gaps.
  • In areas with high moisture, choose a water-resistant or waterproof adhesive.
  • For heavy skirting boards or those on very uneven surfaces, consider using both adhesive and nails for extra security.
  • Always read and follow the manufacturer's instructions for the specific grab adhesive you're using.
  • Wear gloves and work in a well-ventilated area when using strong adhesives.
