Valley Lay Boards: Marking, Cutting, and Fitting Guide

Valley lay boards are essential components in roof construction, particularly where two roof planes intersect to form a valley. This guide will walk you through the process of marking angles, cutting, and fitting valley lay boards.

1. Understanding Valley Lay Boards

Valley lay boards are installed along the valley of a roof to provide support for valley flashing and to create a smooth transition between intersecting roof planes.

2. Tools and Materials

  • Valley lay boards (typically 1x8 or 1x10 lumber)
  • Measuring tape
  • Carpenter's pencil
  • Speed square or framing square
  • Circular saw or hand saw
  • Hammer and nails or nail gun
  • Safety equipment (goggles, gloves, etc.)

3. Marking the Angles

  1. Position the valley lay board along the valley, ensuring it extends beyond both ends.
  2. Use a speed square or framing square to mark the angle where the board meets the roof decking on both sides of the valley.
  3. Draw a line along the entire length of the board following this angle.

4. Cutting the Valley Lay Boards

  1. Set your circular saw to the correct angle as marked on the board.
  2. Carefully cut along the marked line, ensuring a straight and accurate cut.
  3. Repeat the process for both edges of the valley lay board.
Tip: Always wear safety goggles and follow proper safety procedures when using power tools.

5. Fitting the Valley Lay Boards

  1. Place the cut valley lay board into position along the valley.
  2. Ensure the board sits flush against the roof decking on both sides.
  3. Adjust as necessary to achieve a snug fit.
  4. Nail the board in place, starting from one end and working your way to the other.
  5. Use galvanized nails to prevent rust and ensure longevity.

6. Joining Multiple Boards

For longer valleys, you may need to join multiple boards:

  • Cut the ends of the boards at a 45-degree angle where they will meet.
  • Apply waterproof adhesive to the joint.
  • Nail the boards together, ensuring a tight and smooth joint.

7. Final Checks

  1. Inspect the installed valley lay boards to ensure they're securely fastened.
  2. Check for any gaps or unevenness along the valley.
  3. Make sure the boards provide a smooth surface for the valley flashing to be installed over.
Remember: Proper installation of valley lay boards is crucial for the overall integrity of your roof. If you're unsure about any step in the process, it's best to consult with or hire a professional roofer.
