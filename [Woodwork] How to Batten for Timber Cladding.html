How to Batten for Timber Cladding

Battening is a crucial step in preparing a wall for timber cladding. It provides a level surface for the cladding and allows for proper ventilation. Follow this guide to ensure your battening is done correctly.

Materials Needed:

  • Timber battens (typically 25mm x 38mm or 50mm x 25mm)
  • Screws or nails
  • Spirit level
  • Saw
  • Drill (if using screws)
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Waterproof membrane (if not already installed)

Steps:

1. Prepare the Wall

Ensure the wall is clean, dry, and in good condition. Install a waterproof membrane if not already present.

2. Mark Batten Positions

Using a pencil and measuring tape, mark vertical lines where the battens will be installed. Typically, battens should be spaced 400-600mm apart, but check your cladding manufacturer's recommendations.

3. Install Vertical Battens

Start at a corner and work your way across the wall. Use a spirit level to ensure each batten is perfectly vertical. Secure battens to the wall using appropriate screws or nails.

4. Add Horizontal Battens (if necessary)

For vertical cladding, install horizontal battens over the vertical ones. Space these according to your cladding specifications.

5. Install Additional Battens

Add extra battens around windows, doors, and other openings to provide secure fixing points for trimming.

6. Check for Level and Plumb

Use a long spirit level to ensure all battens are level and plumb. Adjust as necessary.

7. Treat Cut Ends

Apply preservative to any cut ends of the battens to prevent moisture ingress.

Tips:

  • Use treated timber battens to resist rot and insect infestation.
  • Ensure there's a gap at the bottom of the vertical battens to allow water drainage.
  • Consider using spacers behind the battens to create a larger cavity for improved ventilation.
  • Always follow local building codes and regulations.

Conclusion

Proper battening is essential for the longevity and appearance of your timber cladding. It provides a stable base and ensures adequate ventilation, which helps prevent issues like warping or rot. Take your time with this process to ensure the best results for your cladding project.
