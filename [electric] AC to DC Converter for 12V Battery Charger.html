AC to DC Converter Circuit for 12V Battery Charger

Circuit Explanation

This circuit converts AC power to regulated 12V DC for charging a battery. Here's how it works:

  1. AC Input: The circuit starts with the AC power source, typically 120V or 240V from a wall outlet.
  2. Transformer: The transformer steps down the high voltage AC to a lower voltage, usually around 15-18V AC. This is necessary because we need a voltage slightly higher than 12V to account for losses in the rest of the circuit.
  3. Bridge Rectifier: The bridge rectifier converts the AC to pulsating DC. It uses four diodes arranged in a bridge configuration to convert both positive and negative cycles of the AC input into positive DC pulses.
  4. Capacitor: The capacitor smooths out the pulsating DC from the rectifier. It charges during voltage peaks and discharges during troughs, resulting in a smoother DC voltage.
  5. Voltage Regulator: The voltage regulator (often an LM7812 or similar) ensures a steady 12V DC output regardless of small fluctuations in input voltage or load current. It also provides protection against voltage spikes.
  6. 12V DC Output: The final output is a stable 12V DC suitable for charging a battery.

Important Considerations

  • The transformer's secondary voltage should be slightly higher than 12V (typically 15-18V) to account for voltage drops across the rectifier and regulator.
