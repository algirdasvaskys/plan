Joining Cables Outside With a Waterproof IP68 Connector

This guide will walk you through the process of joining cables outside using a waterproof IP68 connector. This method ensures a secure and water-resistant connection for outdoor applications.

Safety First!

Always ensure that the power is turned off before working with electrical connections. Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) such as insulated gloves and safety glasses.

Materials Needed:

  • Waterproof IP68 connector
  • Cables to be joined
  • Wire strippers
  • Screwdriver
  • Multimeter (for testing)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Cables

Strip the outer insulation of each cable, exposing about 2 inches (5 cm) of the inner wires. Then, strip about 0.5 inches (1.2 cm) of insulation from each individual wire.

Step 2: Open the IP68 Connector

Unscrew the end caps of the IP68 connector. Make sure to keep track of all the parts, including any rubber seals or gaskets.

Step 3: Insert the Cables

Thread each cable through its respective end cap and rubber seal. Ensure that the rubber seal fits snugly around the cable's outer insulation.

Step 4: Connect the Wires

Match the wires according to their colors or functions. Insert each stripped wire into the appropriate terminal in the connector body. Tighten the terminal screws securely.

Step 5: Close the Connector

Carefully push the connected wires into the connector body. Screw the end caps back onto the connector body, ensuring they are tight and properly aligned.

Step 6: Test the Connection

Use a multimeter to test the continuity of the connection. This ensures that the wires are properly connected and there are no short circuits.

Step 7: Waterproof Test

If possible, perform a waterproof test by submerging the connected IP68 connector in water (while the cables are not powered) to ensure no water ingress.

Important Notes:

  • Always follow local electrical codes and regulations.
  • If you're unsure about any step, consult a professional electrician.
  • Regularly inspect outdoor connections for signs of wear or damage.
