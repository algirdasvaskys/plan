Storing Construction Timber in Hot Weather

Proper storage of construction timber in hot weather is crucial to maintain its quality and prevent damage. Follow these guidelines to ensure your timber remains in good condition:

1. Choose the Right Location

  • Store timber in a cool, dry place
  • Avoid direct sunlight
  • Ensure good air circulation
Tip: If possible, use a covered storage area or warehouse to protect the timber from the elements.

2. Elevate the Timber

  • Use wooden pallets or concrete blocks to raise the timber off the ground
  • Ensure a minimum clearance of 6 inches (15 cm) from the ground

3. Stack Properly

  • Stack timber horizontally
  • Use stickers (small pieces of wood) between layers to allow air circulation
  • Align stickers vertically to prevent warping

4. Protect from Moisture

  • Cover the timber with a waterproof tarp or plastic sheeting
  • Ensure the cover doesn't touch the ground to prevent moisture wicking
  • Allow for some air circulation under the cover
Warning: Do not wrap the timber too tightly, as this can trap moisture and promote mold growth.

5. Regular Inspection

  • Check the timber regularly for signs of warping, cracking, or mold
  • Rotate the stack periodically to ensure even exposure

6. Acclimatization

  • Before use, allow the timber to acclimatize to the environment where it will be installed
  • Gradually expose the timber to the new conditions over several days
Tip: For interior use, bring the timber inside at least a week before installation to allow it to adjust to indoor humidity levels.

7. Moisture Content

  • Use a moisture meter to check the timber's moisture content regularly
  • Aim for a moisture content of 6-8% for interior use and 9-14% for exterior use

By following these guidelines, you can help ensure that your construction timber remains in good condition, even in hot weather. Proper storage will minimize warping, cracking, and other forms of damage, ultimately resulting in a better final product.
