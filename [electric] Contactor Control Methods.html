How to Turn ON and OFF a Contactor

Contactors are essential components in electrical systems, used to control power circuits. This guide explains two common methods for controlling contactors: Two Wire and Three Wire Control Methods.

Two Wire Control Method

The Two Wire Control Method is a simple and straightforward way to control a contactor.

Components:

  • Contactor
  • Power supply
  • Control switch (ON/OFF)

How it works:

  1. The control switch is connected in series with the contactor coil.
  2. When the switch is closed (ON position), current flows through the coil, energizing it and closing the contactor's main contacts.
  3. When the switch is opened (OFF position), current stops flowing, de-energizing the coil and opening the contactor's main contacts.

Advantages:

  • Simple design
  • Fewer components
  • Easy to troubleshoot

Disadvantages:

  • No maintained contact
  • Less suitable for applications requiring multiple control points

Three Wire Control Method

The Three Wire Control Method offers more advanced control options and is widely used in industrial applications.

Components:

  • Contactor
  • Power supply
  • START pushbutton (momentary)
  • STOP pushbutton (momentary, normally closed)
  • Auxiliary contact from the contactor

How it works:

  1. The START button, STOP button, and auxiliary contact are connected in series with the contactor coil.
  2. Pressing the START button energizes the contactor coil, closing the main contacts and the auxiliary contact.
  3. The auxiliary contact creates a holding circuit, maintaining the contactor in the ON state even after releasing the START button.
  4. Pressing the STOP button breaks the circuit, de-energizing the coil and opening the contactor's main contacts.

Advantages:

  • Maintained contact operation
  • Suitable for multiple control points
  • Enhanced safety with normally closed STOP button
  • Self-holding feature

Disadvantages:

  • More complex wiring
  • Requires more components

Conclusion

Both Two Wire and Three Wire Control Methods have their places in electrical systems. The choice between them depends on the specific application requirements, safety considerations, and control complexity needed. The Three Wire Control Method is generally preferred in industrial settings due to its versatility and enhanced safety features.
