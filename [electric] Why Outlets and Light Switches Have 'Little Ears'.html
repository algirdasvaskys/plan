Why Outlets and Light Switches Have "Little Ears"

If you've ever taken a close look at electrical outlets or light switches, you may have noticed small protruding tabs on either side. These are often referred to as "little ears," but they serve an important purpose in the installation and functionality of these devices.

[Image: Outlet with mounting tabs highlighted]

What Are These "Little Ears"?

The "little ears" on outlets and light switches are officially called mounting tabs or mounting flanges. They are small, flat extensions that protrude from the sides of the device.

Purpose of the Mounting Tabs

These mounting tabs serve several important functions:

  1. Secure Installation: The primary purpose of these tabs is to provide a secure method of attaching the outlet or switch to the electrical box in the wall.
  2. Proper Alignment: They help ensure that the device is properly aligned within the electrical box and sits flush with the wall surface.
  3. Load Distribution: The tabs distribute the load of the device evenly, preventing stress on the wiring connections.
  4. Grounding: In some cases, these tabs can provide an additional grounding point for metal electrical boxes.

How They Work

The mounting tabs have small holes that align with the screw holes on the electrical box. When installing the outlet or switch:

  1. The device is placed into the electrical box.
  2. Screws are inserted through the holes in the tabs.
  3. These screws are then tightened, securing the device to the box.
[Image: Diagram showing installation process]

Benefits of This Design

  • Stability: Prevents the outlet or switch from moving when plugs are inserted or removed.
  • Safety: Ensures a proper fit, reducing the risk of electrical hazards.
  • Aesthetics: Helps maintain a neat appearance by keeping the device flush with the wall.
  • Ease of Installation: Simplifies the process for electricians and DIY enthusiasts.

Variations

Some modern devices may have slightly different designs, such as:

  • Push-in tabs that don't require screws
  • Adjustable tabs for different wall thicknesses
  • Tabs with multiple screw holes for flexibility

Conclusion

While they might seem like a small detail, these "little ears" or mounting tabs play a crucial role in the safe and proper installation of electrical outlets and switches. They are a testament to the thoughtful design that goes into even the most common household fixtures.
