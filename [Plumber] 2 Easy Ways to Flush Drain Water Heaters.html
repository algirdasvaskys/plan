2 Easy Ways to Flush Drain Water Heaters

Regular maintenance of your water heater is crucial for its longevity and efficiency. Here are two easy methods to flush your water heater, whether it's gas or electric.

Method 1: The Traditional Flush

  1. Turn off the power source:
    • For gas heaters: Turn the thermostat to "pilot"
    • For electric heaters: Turn off the circuit breaker
  2. Turn off the cold water supply to the heater
  3. Connect a garden hose to the drain valve at the bottom of the tank
  4. Place the other end of the hose in a suitable drainage area
  5. Open a hot water faucet in your home to allow air into the system
  6. Open the drain valve and let the tank empty completely
  7. Once empty, turn on the cold water supply briefly to flush out remaining sediment
  8. Close the drain valve and disconnect the hose
  9. Allow the tank to refill completely
  10. Turn the power source back on
Pro Tip: For stubborn sediment, repeat the process of draining and refilling a few times.

Method 2: The Quick Flush

  1. Turn off the power source (as in Method 1)
  2. Connect a short garden hose to the drain valve
  3. Place a 5-gallon bucket under the hose end
  4. Open the drain valve and let water flow for about 1-2 minutes
  5. Close the valve and empty the bucket
  6. Repeat steps 4-5 until the water runs clear
  7. Close the drain valve and disconnect the hose
  8. Turn the power source back on
Pro Tip: This method is quicker but may not remove as much sediment as the traditional flush.

Additional Pro Plumber Tips

  • Flush your water heater at least once a year for optimal performance
  • Always exercise caution when working with hot water and electrical components
  • If your drain valve is stuck or leaking, consider replacing it
  • For older heaters, be prepared to replace the anode rod during maintenance
  • Consider installing a water softener to reduce sediment buildup
Warning: If you're uncomfortable performing these tasks or encounter any issues, always consult a professional plumber.
