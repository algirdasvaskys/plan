How to Lay Chipboard Flooring in a Garden Room

This guide will walk you through the process of laying 22mm Egger Protect chipboard flooring in your garden room. Follow these steps for a professional finish.

Step 1: Prepare the Area

Ensure the subfloor is clean, dry, and level. Remove any debris or old flooring materials.

Step 2: Install a Damp Proof Membrane (DPM)

Lay a 1000-gauge polythene sheet over the entire floor area, overlapping joints by at least 150mm. Tape the joints for added protection.

Step 3: Install Underfloor Insulation

Place rigid foam insulation boards between the floor joists. Cut the boards to fit snugly.

Step 4: Acclimatize the Chipboard

Allow the 22mm Egger Protect chipboard to acclimatize in the room for at least 48 hours before installation.

Step 5: Plan the Layout

Start laying boards from the corner furthest from the door. Stagger the joints for added strength.

Step 6: Apply Adhesive

Apply a continuous bead of wood adhesive along the tongue and groove of each board.

Step 7: Lay the First Row

Place the first board with the tongue facing the wall. Leave a 10mm expansion gap around the edges.

Step 8: Secure the Boards

Use ring shank nails or screws to secure the boards to the joists. Space fixings at 300mm intervals along the joists.

Step 9: Continue Laying Boards

Fit subsequent boards, ensuring tight joints. Use a tapping block to avoid damaging the board edges.

Step 10: Cut Boards to Fit

Use a circular saw or jigsaw to cut boards to fit around obstacles or at room edges.

Step 11: Finish the Edges

Once all boards are laid, install skirting boards to cover the expansion gaps.

Pro Tips:

  • Always wear appropriate safety gear, including goggles and a dust mask when cutting boards.
  • Use a moisture meter to ensure the subfloor's moisture content is below 14% before installation.
  • For areas prone to moisture, consider sealing the cut edges of the chipboard with PVA adhesive.

By following these steps, you'll successfully install 22mm Egger Protect chipboard flooring in your garden room, creating a sturdy and long-lasting floor surface.
