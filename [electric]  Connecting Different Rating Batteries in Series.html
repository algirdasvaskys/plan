Connecting Different Rating Batteries in Series

Warning: Connecting batteries in series can be dangerous if not done properly. Always exercise caution and follow proper safety guidelines.

What Happens When Connecting Different Rating Batteries in Series?

When connecting batteries with different ratings in series, several important things occur:

  1. Voltage Addition: The total voltage of the series connection will be the sum of all individual battery voltages.
  2. Current Limitation: The current will be limited by the battery with the lowest capacity (Amp-hours).
  3. Capacity Mismatch: The overall capacity of the series connection will be determined by the battery with the lowest capacity.
  4. Potential Imbalance: Batteries may discharge at different rates, leading to potential imbalances and reduced overall performance.

Voltage in Series Connection

The primary effect of connecting batteries in series is the addition of voltages. The formula for calculating the total voltage is:

Vtotal = V1 + V2 + V3 + ... + Vn

Example: If you connect a 1.5V AA battery, a 9V battery, and a 12V car battery in series, the total voltage would be:
Vtotal = 1.5V + 9V + 12V = 22.5V

Current and Capacity Considerations

While the voltage adds up, the current and capacity are limited by the weakest battery in the series:

  • The maximum current will be determined by the battery with the lowest current rating.
  • The overall capacity (in Amp-hours) will be limited by the battery with the smallest capacity.

Potential Issues and Risks

Connecting batteries with different ratings in series can lead to several problems:

  1. Overcharging: Some batteries may become overcharged during the charging process.
  2. Over-discharging: Weaker batteries may be completely discharged before others, potentially damaging them.
  3. Reduced Lifespan: The overall lifespan of the battery pack may be reduced due to imbalances.
  4. Safety Hazards: In extreme cases, overcharging or over-discharging can lead to battery leakage, swelling, or even explosion.

Best Practices

To minimize risks and optimize performance when connecting batteries in series:

  • Use batteries of the same type, capacity, and age whenever possible.
  • If mixing is necessary, use batteries with similar voltages and capacities.
  • Implement a battery management system (BMS) to monitor and balance individual cells.
  • Regularly check the voltage of individual batteries in the series connection.
  • Avoid mixing rechargeable and non-rechargeable batteries in the same series connection.

Conclusion

While it is technically possible to connect different rating batteries in series, it's generally not recommended due to the potential risks and reduced efficiency. For optimal performance and safety, it's best to use matching batteries in series connections whenever possible.
