Automatic Water Level Controller: Wiring and Installation Guide

Warning: This guide is intended for qualified electrical technicians. Working with electrical systems can be dangerous. Always follow safety protocols and local electrical codes.

1. Components Required

  • Automatic Water Level Controller unit
  • Contactor or Relay
  • Float switches (typically 3: for low, medium, and high levels)
  • Electrical wires
  • Circuit breaker
  • Water pump
  • PVC pipes and fittings

2. Wiring Instructions

Step 1: Turn off the main power supply before starting any wiring work.
Step 2: Connect the power supply to the controller:
  • Connect the live wire to the 'L' terminal of the controller
  • Connect the neutral wire to the 'N' terminal
  • Connect the earth wire to the 'E' terminal
Step 3: Wire the float switches:
  • Connect the common wire of all float switches to the 'COM' terminal on the controller
  • Connect the low-level float switch to the 'LOW' terminal
  • Connect the medium-level float switch to the 'MED' terminal
  • Connect the high-level float switch to the 'HIGH' terminal
Step 4: Wire the contactor or relay:
  • Connect the 'NO' (Normally Open) terminal of the controller to one end of the contactor coil
  • Connect the other end of the contactor coil to the neutral line
Step 5: Connect the water pump:
  • Wire the pump through the contactor's main contacts
  • Ensure proper earthing of the pump

3. Installation Process

Step 1: Mount the controller unit in a dry, accessible location near the water tank.
Step 2: Install the float switches in the overhead tank:
  • Low-level switch at about 1/4 of the tank height
  • Medium-level switch at about 1/2 of the tank height
  • High-level switch near the top of the tank
Step 3: Secure all wiring connections and insulate them properly.
Step 4: Double-check all connections before powering on the system.
Step 5: Turn on the power supply and test the system:
  • Manually move the float switches to simulate different water levels
  • Verify that the pump turns on when the water level is low and turns off when it's high

4. Maintenance

  • Regularly inspect the float switches for any debris or damage
  • Check all electrical connections periodically for any signs of loosening or corrosion
  • Test the system functionality at least once a month
  • Clean the water tank and float switches annually
Note: Always refer to the manufacturer's specific instructions for your particular water level controller model, as wiring and installation details may vary.
