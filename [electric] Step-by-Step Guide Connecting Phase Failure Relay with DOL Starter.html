Step-by-Step Guide: Connecting Phase Failure Relay with DOL Starter

This guide provides a detailed method for connecting a Phase Failure Relay with a Direct On-Line (DOL) Starter. Follow these steps carefully to ensure proper installation and functionality.

Warning: Working with electrical equipment can be dangerous. Always ensure that power is disconnected before starting any work, and consult a qualified electrician if you're unsure about any step.

Materials Needed:

  • Phase Failure Relay
  • DOL Starter
  • Wires
  • Wire strippers
  • Screwdriver
  • Multimeter

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Prepare the workspace

Ensure the power is off and the area is clean and well-lit. Lay out all your materials and tools.

Step 2: Identify the terminals

Locate the input and output terminals on both the Phase Failure Relay and the DOL Starter. Refer to the manufacturer's diagrams if necessary.

Step 3: Connect the Phase Failure Relay to the power supply

Connect the three-phase power supply (L1, L2, L3) to the corresponding input terminals of the Phase Failure Relay.

Step 4: Wire the Phase Failure Relay to the DOL Starter

Connect the output terminals of the Phase Failure Relay to the control circuit of the DOL Starter. Typically, this involves connecting to the coil of the main contactor.

Step 5: Set up the DOL Starter

Wire the DOL Starter as per its manual, connecting the power supply, motor connections, and control circuit.

Step 6: Connect the neutral (if required)

If your Phase Failure Relay requires a neutral connection, connect it to the neutral line.

Step 7: Double-check all connections

Verify that all connections are secure and correctly made according to the wiring diagrams of both devices.

Step 8: Set the Phase Failure Relay parameters

Adjust the settings on the Phase Failure Relay according to your specific requirements (e.g., voltage limits, time delay).

Step 9: Test the setup

Power on the system and test the functionality. The Phase Failure Relay should prevent the DOL Starter from operating if there's a phase failure or imbalance.

Safety Note: Always use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) and follow all relevant safety guidelines and regulations when working with electrical systems.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you should have successfully connected a Phase Failure Relay with a DOL Starter. This setup will help protect your three-phase motor from potential damage due to phase failures or imbalances.
