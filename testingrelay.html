Troubleshooting Tips for Control Device (Terminal 9 to Terminal 11)

If the measured voltage is not as expected (e.g., 24V), check for:

  • Power supply issues or incorrect voltage.
  • Faulty control device.
  • Loose connections or damaged wires.

Note: Measure voltage across the terminals when the control device is in the closed state. Never measure resistance on a live circuit.

Troubleshooting Tips for Control Device (Terminal 8 to Terminal 11)

If the measured value is not OL (Open Line), check for:

  • Short circuits in the wiring.
  • Stuck or faulty contacts in the control device.

Note: Always ensure the circuit is de-energized before measuring resistance to avoid inaccurate readings and ensure safety.

Troubleshooting Tips for Relay (Coil) (Terminal A1 to Terminal A2 - De-energized)

If the measured value is outside 70-75 ohms, check for:

  • Possible coil damage or degradation.
  • Incorrect relay type or specifications.

Note: Always ensure the circuit is de-energized before measuring resistance to avoid inaccurate readings and ensure safety.

Troubleshooting Tips for Relay (Coil) (Terminal A1 to Terminal A2 - Energized)

If the measured voltage is not as expected (e.g., 24V), check for:

  • Power supply issues.
  • Faulty relay or control circuit.
  • Incorrect wiring or connections.

Note: Measure voltage across the coil terminals when the relay is energized to ensure it is receiving the correct voltage. Never measure resistance on a live circuit.
